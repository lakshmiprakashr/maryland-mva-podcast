# Chapter-07 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Covers sharing the road with pedestrians, emergency vehicles, large trucks and buses, school buses, motorcycles, bicycles, mopeds/scooters, and understanding vehicle technologies (ADAS). Each section defines right-of-way rules, blind spots, and safe practices.

## Why It Matters

Maryland roads are shared by many types of road users. Knowing right-of-way rules and blind spot zones prevents crashes with vulnerable road users. The MVA test heavily emphasizes pedestrian, motorcycle, bicycle, and school bus rules.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Pedestrians:** Have right-of-way at crosswalks (marked and unmarked); drivers must stop even when no signal exists
- **Crosswalk rules:** Do not pass vehicles stopped at crosswalks; yield to pedestrians turning on green or red
- **Emergency vehicles:** Right-of-way when using sirens and flashing lights; pull to edge, stay out of intersections, remain stopped
- **Same direction as emergency vehicle:** Do not pass unless it stops or officer tells you to
- **Truck No-Zones:** Four blind spots — side, rear, front, backing up; if you can't see the truck's mirrors, the driver can't see you
- **Truck stopping distance:** Fully loaded trucks can weigh 80,000+ pounds and need a football field to stop
- **School buses:** Stop at least 20 feet from front or back when flashing red lights are on; do not pass on multi-lane road with physical barrier on opposite side only
- **Motorcycles:** Six times more likely to be hurt in crash; drivers at fault in over half of car-motorcycle crashes; yield right-of-way when turning left; leave 3-4 second following distance
- **Bicycles:** Are vehicles under Maryland law; must yield when turning; give at least 3 feet when passing; bicyclists may use full lane
- **Mopeds/scooters:** Allowed on roads with 50 mph or less posted speed; ride on road or shoulder
- **ADAS:** Prevents 40% of crashes when used properly; driver remains responsible at all times; limitations in rain, snow, fog, hills, curves

## MVA Test Traps

- Thinking crosswalks only exist where marked — most intersections have crosswalks even if not marked
- Believing you don't have to stop for pedestrians if you have a green light — you must yield to pedestrians when turning on green
- Thinking you can pass a truck on the right safely — passing on the right is always risky
- Confusing school bus stopping rules on divided highways — you do NOT stop if the bus is on the other side of a physical barrier
- Thinking motorcycles can share a lane with cars — motorcyclists are allowed to use the whole lane
- Believing ADAS replaces driver responsibility — you are always responsible for safe operation

## Common Mistakes

- Not stopping for pedestrians at unmarked crosswalks
- Forgetting that you must yield to pedestrians even when turning on a green signal
- Not checking for motorcycles in blind spots before lane changes
- Passing a bicyclist with less than 3 feet of space
- Assuming a truck driver can see you if you're alongside the trailer
- Not knowing that bicycles are vehicles with full road rights in Maryland
- Forgetting that mopeds and scooters must follow all traffic laws

## Memory Tricks

- **Pedestrians:** "Crosswalk = must stop, marked or not"
- **Truck No-Zones:** "If you can't see their mirrors, they can't see you"
- **School bus:** "Twenty feet, red lights flashing, or you're stopping"
- **Motorcycles:** "Look twice, leave space, yield on left turns"
- **Bicycles:** "Three feet to pass, they own the lane too"
- **ADAS:** "Assist, not replace — you're still the driver"

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

- **No-Zones diagram:** Side blind spots (both sides), rear No-Zone (200 feet), front No-Zone (10-20 feet), backing up No-Zone (50 feet)
- **Truck mirrors rule:** If you can't see the driver's face in their side-view mirror, they can't see you
- **Right-side blind spot:** Runs the length of the trailer and extends out three lanes

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- Pedestrians have right-of-way at all crosswalks (marked and unmarked)
- Bicycles are vehicles under Maryland law with full road rights
- Bicyclists may use full lane if lane is too narrow for safe motor vehicle passing
- Failing to yield to bicycle resulting in crash = fines, prison time, and points
- Bicycles allowed on sidewalks statewide (local jurisdictions may prohibit)
- Mopeds/scooters allowed on roads posted 50 mph or less
- ADAS driver is always responsible for safe vehicle operation

## Rapid Recall Questions

1. Must you stop for pedestrians at an unmarked crosswalk? → Yes
2. Can you pass a vehicle stopped at a crosswalk? → No
3. What must you do when turning on a green light and pedestrians are crossing? → Yield to them
4. How far must you stop from a school bus with flashing red lights? → At least 20 feet
5. On a divided highway with a physical barrier, must you stop for a school bus on the opposite side? → No
6. If you can't see a truck driver's face in their side mirror, what does it mean? → They can't see you
7. How much following distance should you leave behind a motorcycle? → 3-4 seconds
8. What is the minimum passing distance for a bicycle? → 3 feet
9. Are bicycles allowed on Maryland roads? → Yes, they are vehicles under Maryland law
10. On what speed roads can mopeds ride? → Roads posted 50 mph or less
