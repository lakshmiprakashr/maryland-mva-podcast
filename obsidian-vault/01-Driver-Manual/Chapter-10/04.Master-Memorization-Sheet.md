# Chapter 10 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Chapter 10 covers essential supplementary information every Maryland driver must know: medical conditions that must be reported to the MVA, supervising driver requirements for learner's permit holders, organ donor registration, voter registration at the MVA, insurance requirements, seat belt and child safety seat laws, air bag safety, ABS braking, headset/earphone prohibition, driving test maneuvers, bicyclist rules, and electric vehicle classifications.

## Why It Matters

This chapter tests practical knowledge that affects your daily driving. From insurance cards to child seats to sharing the road with bicyclists, these rules keep you legal and safe. The MVA test pulls specific numbers, ages, and requirements from this chapter — miss one and you could fail a question.

## High-Yield Facts

### Medical Conditions to Report
- Must notify MVA when applying, renewing, or when diagnosed after licensure
- MVA Driver Wellness and Safety Division and/or Medical Advisory Board must approve
- Key conditions: diabetes with low blood sugar episodes (past 6 months), epilepsy/seizures, heart condition with loss of consciousness (past 6 months), stroke, dizzy spells/fainting/blackouts, sleep apnea/narcolepsy, TBI history, limb weakness/shaking/numbness, absent/amputated limbs, eye problems (corrected visual acuity worse than 20/70 in one eye or binocular field under 110 degrees), alcohol/drug use problems, mental health conditions, schizophrenia, dementia

### Supervising Driver Requirements
- At least **21 years old**
- Currently licensed for at least **3 years** in Maryland or another state
- Must drive the **same class of vehicles**
- Must be **seated beside you** (except for motorcycles)

### Organ Donor
- Anyone **18+** may be an organ donor
- Minors **16+** may donate with parent/guardian written consent
- Information goes to Maryland Organ Donor Registry via Maryland Department of Health

### Voter Registration
- **National Voter Registration Act of 1993** ("Motor Voter") — federal law
- MVA allows eligible customers to register or update voting info when getting license or photo ID card

### Insurance Requirements
- All MD-registered vehicles must be insured by a **Maryland-licensed company**
- Must carry insurance for **personal injury and property damage liability**
- Must have valid insurance card (electronic OK) when driving
- Refusing to show insurance card to officer = **fine**
- No insurance = fine up to **$2,500 per vehicle per year**

### Seat Belt Law
- **Driver AND all passengers** must wear safety belt or be in child safety seat

### Child Safety Seats
- All children **under 8** must ride in child safety seat **unless 4'9" or taller**
- Children **under 2**: rear-facing seat until reaching manufacturer's height/weight limits
- Contact **KISS (Kids In Safety Seats)** at Maryland Department of Health: **800-370-SEAT** for low-cost seat programs

### Air Bags
- Use with lap and shoulder belt
- Keep **10-12 inches** between chest and steering wheel
- Pregnant women: lap belt under abdomen, low on hips, across upper thighs; shoulder belt over belly
- **Rear-facing car seat: NEVER in front seat with passenger air bag**
- **Children under 13: ride in rear seat** with safety seat or seat belt

### ABS (Anti-Lock Braking System)
- Prevents wheels from locking
- When engaged: **rumble sound + brake pedal vibrates** — this is NORMAL
- Do NOT pump brakes — maintain steady pressure

### Headsets/Earphones/Earplugs
- May **NOT** drive with earplugs, headsets, or earphones in **both ears**
- Hearing aid devices ARE allowed

### Driving Test Maneuvers
- **Parallel parking: NO LONGER required** on driving test
- Skills covered by "reverse two-point turn" in driving education course
- **Required on test**: pull-in parking, back-in parking, three-point turn
- Pull-in/back-in parking: done in **10' x 20'** space
- Three-point turn: done in **16' x 20'** space

### Bicyclist Rules
- Bicyclists have rights AND responsibilities on the road
- Ride **straight, single file, on the right side**
- Signal all turns; look back before changing lanes
- Left turns: turn like a motor vehicle OR walk bicycle through crosswalk
- **Helmets required** for anyone **under 16** (including passengers)
- Night riding: white headlight + red reflector visible from **600 feet**
- Brakes must stop bicycle within **15 feet** at **10 mph** on dry, level, clean pavement
- Headlight visible from **500 feet** at night

### Electric Vehicles
- **Electric bicycles**: human power + electric motor, operable pedals, 2-3 wheels, 750 watts or less
  - **Class 1**: motor assists only while pedaling, stops at **20 mph**
  - **Class 2**: motor assists whether pedaling or not, stops at **20 mph**
  - **Class 3**: motor assists only while pedaling, stops at **28 mph**
- **Electric low-speed vehicles**: operator only, under 100 pounds, handlebars + standing platform, up to **20 mph**

## MVA Test Traps

- **"Under 8" not "under 12"** for child safety seat requirement — students confuse with air bag rule (under 13 for rear seat)
- **4'9" height exception** for child safety seats — if child is 4'9" or taller, no seat required even under age 8
- **Rear-facing seats: NEVER in front with air bag** — this is absolute, no exceptions
- **ABS pedal vibration is NORMAL** — students think something is wrong and release the brake
- **Parallel parking is NOT on the test** — but pull-in, back-in, and three-point turn ARE
- **Earphone prohibition: both ears** — one ear may be okay, but both ears is illegal
- **Supervising driver must be 21, not 18** — students confuse with passenger restriction age
- **Insurance card can be electronic** — students think it must be paper
- **$2,500 per vehicle per year** fine for no insurance — not a one-time fine
- **Bicycle headlight: 500 feet** visibility; reflector: **600 feet** — different numbers

## Common Mistakes

- Reporting medical conditions only at license application (must also report when diagnosed after licensure)
- Thinking the supervising driver must be a parent (must be 21+ and licensed 3+ years, not necessarily a parent)
- Forgetting that children under 2 must be rear-facing until manufacturer limits are met
- Confusing child safety seat age (under 8) with air bag rear seat age (under 13)
- Not knowing that parallel parking is no longer on the driving test
- Believing you need a paper insurance card (electronic is fine)
- Forgetting hearing aids are exempt from the headset/earphone prohibition

## Memory Tricks

- **"8 and 4-9"** — child safety seats required until age 8 OR 4'9" tall
- **"Two, rear-facing"** — children under 2 must be rear-facing
- **"13 in the back"** — children under 13 ride in rear seat
- **"10-12 inches from the wheel"** — air bag safety distance
- **"21, 3 years, same class"** — supervising driver requirements
- **"500 light, 600 reflect"** — bicycle headlight 500 feet, reflector 600 feet
- **"15 at 10"** — bicycle brakes stop within 15 feet at 10 mph
- **"Pull, Back, Three"** — the three maneuvers required on driving test
- **"ABS = don't let go"** — maintain pressure, don't pump, even when it vibrates

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

No specific road signs in this chapter. Content focuses on vehicle equipment, safety requirements, and driver responsibilities.

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **Medical reporting**: Must notify MVA of specified conditions when applying, renewing, or upon diagnosis
- **Supervising driver**: Must be 21+, licensed 3+ years, same vehicle class, seated beside driver
- **Organ donor**: 18+ may donate; 16+ with written parental consent
- **Motor Voter**: National Voter Registration Act of 1993 — register at MVA
- **Insurance**: Maryland-licensed company required; must carry card (electronic OK)
- **Seat belts**: Driver and ALL passengers
- **Child safety seats**: Under 8 (unless 4'9"+); under 2 must be rear-facing
- **Air bags**: Under 13 in rear seat; rear-facing never in front with passenger air bag
- **Headsets/earphones**: Not in both ears; hearing aids exempt
- **Driving test**: No parallel parking; pull-in, back-in parking, and three-point turn required
- **Bicyclists**: Helmets under 16; lights visible 500/600 feet at night
- **Electric bicycles**: Class 1 (20 mph pedaling only), Class 2 (20 mph always), Class 3 (28 mph pedaling only)

## Rapid Recall Questions

1. **Q:** What medical conditions must you report to the MVA?
   **A:** Diabetes with low blood sugar episodes, epilepsy/seizures, heart conditions with loss of consciousness, stroke, dizzy spells, sleep apnea, TBI, limb weakness, absent limbs, eye problems below 20/70 or 110 degrees, alcohol/drug problems, mental health conditions, schizophrenia, and dementia.

2. **Q:** What are the three requirements for a supervising driver?
   **A:** At least 21 years old, licensed for at least 3 years in Maryland or another state for the same class of vehicles, and seated beside you (except motorcycles).

3. **Q:** At what age can someone be an organ donor in Maryland without parental consent?
   **A:** 18 years old or older. Minors 16+ may donate with written parental or guardian consent.

4. **Q:** What is the fine for driving without insurance in Maryland?
   **A:** Up to $2,500 per vehicle per year.

5. **Q:** Until what age must children ride in a child safety seat?
   **A:** Under age 8, unless the child is 4'9" or taller. Children under 2 must be rear-facing.

6. **Q:** How far should you sit from the steering wheel for air bag safety?
   **A:** 10-12 inches between your chest and the steering wheel.

7. **Q:** What happens when ABS engages?
   **A:** You hear a rumble and the brake pedal vibrates. Maintain steady pressure — do not pump the brakes.

8. **Q:** Is parallel parking required on the Maryland driving test?
   **A:** No. Parallel parking is no longer required. You must perform pull-in parking, back-in parking, and a three-point turn.

9. **Q:** What bicycle equipment is required by law at night?
   **A:** A white beam headlight visible from 500 feet and a red rear reflector visible from 600 feet.

10. **Q:** What are the three classes of electric bicycles in Maryland?
    **A:** Class 1 (motor assists while pedaling, stops at 20 mph), Class 2 (motor assists always, stops at 20 mph), Class 3 (motor assists while pedaling, stops at 28 mph).
