# Chapter-03 - Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary
Basic driving rules: defensive driving, right-of-way, speed control, following distance, lane discipline, turning, passing, parking, and traffic signals.

## Why It Matters
These are foundational rules tested heavily on the Maryland MVA knowledge exam. Every question about intersections, speed, or signal behavior traces back to this chapter.

## High-Yield Facts
- **Defensive driving** = constantly observing surroundings, scanning front/sides/rear
- **Right-of-way**: yield to driver on your right at simultaneous arrival; yield to pedestrians always
- **Posted speed limit** = maximum speed under ideal conditions only
- **Following distance**: minimum 3-4 seconds ideal; 4-5 seconds for buses/delivery vans
- **Stopping distance** depends on: reaction time, road condition, tire tread, brake condition
- **Lane changes**: never drift across lines; never change lanes in an intersection
- **Turning**: signal before the turn, check front/behind/both sides, adjust speed
- **U-turns**: check if allowed; yield to oncoming traffic; turn into outside/right lane
- **Passing**: pass left; return when you see both headlights in rearview mirror
- **No passing zones**: solid yellow on your side, double solid yellow, within 100 feet of intersection/railroad/bridge/tunnel
- **Parking**: park in designated areas; set parking brake; put in park/gear; lock doors
- **Traffic signals override**: only a police officer or uniformed school crossing guard may direct you to disobey a signal
- **Red light**: full stop; may turn right (unless prohibited) or left one-way to one-way; yield to all
- **Yellow light**: preparing to turn red; stop if safe; if too close, proceed through
- **Green light**: proceed with caution; yield to pedestrians and vehicles already in intersection
- **Non-working signal**: treat as four-way stop

## Why It Matters
Failing to follow basic driving rules is a leading cause of crashes. Knowing right-of-way, speed limits, and signal meanings is heavily tested on the Maryland knowledge test and prevents collisions.

## High-Yield Facts
- You must carry valid license + registration card while driving
- Always wear seat belt; ensure all passengers do too
- "Drive defensively" = anticipate risks, never assume other drivers will follow rules
- Driving slower than traffic also increases crash risk (encourages passing)
- Speeding increases stopping distance, decreases reaction time, increases crash force
- You may drive below speed limit but never above it (Maryland Vehicle Law)
- When being passed, do NOT increase speed
- On road with "stay right" signs, drive in right lane unless passing
- Emergency only exceptions to traffic control devices: police officer, school crossing guard in uniform

## MVA Test Traps
- "You may turn left on red" — only from a one-way street TO another one-way street
- "Yellow means go" — yellow means prepare to stop; proceed only if you cannot safely stop
- "You must always yield at a T-intersection" — you yield to drivers already on the road at a T-intersection
- "Passing on the right is always illegal" — legal on unobstructed multi-lane same-direction roads
- "Following distance is 2 seconds" — MVA recommends minimum 3-4 seconds under ideal conditions
- "Green arrow means you can go without looking" — you must still yield to pedestrians and vehicles in the intersection

## Common Mistakes
- Changing lanes while in an intersection
- Not adjusting speed for adverse conditions (even when under posted limit)
- Assuming you have right-of-way just because the rules say you should
- Not yielding to opposing traffic when making left turns
- Forgetting to signal when passing or changing lanes
- Parking too close to a curb or in non-designated areas

## Memory Tricks
- **Red = Stop** (one syllable = one action: STOP)
- **Yellow = Slow** (yellow = caution, like a yellow warning label)
- **Green = Go** (green = money = go)
- **Following distance**: "One-one thousand, two-one thousand, three-one thousand" (say it out loud to measure)
- **Passing rule**: "See both headlights in the mirror before you return"
- **Yield rule**: "When in doubt, yield — it's never wrong to be safe"

## Must-Know Signs and Signals
- **Steady Red**: Complete stop; right turn on red allowed unless prohibited
- **Steady Yellow**: Signal changing to red; stop if safe
- **Steady Green**: Proceed with caution; yield to those already in intersection
- **Red Arrow**: Complete stop; cannot turn in arrow direction until green
- **Green Arrow**: Protected turn in arrow direction; yield to pedestrians
- **Flashing Red**: Complete stop; treat as stop sign
- **Flashing Yellow**: Slow down; proceed with caution
- **Non-working signal**: Treat as four-way stop; yield to vehicles/pedestrians already in intersection

## Maryland-Specific Laws
- Maryland Vehicle Law requires driving at a **reasonable speed** and being aware of hazards
- You may drive slower than posted limit but never faster
- You must turn on headlights when using windshield wipers in inclement weather
- Red light turn: right turn allowed unless sign prohibits; left turn only one-way to one-way
- When being passed, you must yield and not increase speed

## Rapid Recall Questions
1. What is the minimum following distance recommended by the MVA? → 3 to 4 seconds under ideal conditions
2. When must you yield at a four-way stop with stop signs? → To the driver on your right
3. Can you turn left on a red light? → Only from a one-way street to another one-way street
4. What should you do when being passed by another vehicle? → Yield and do not increase speed
5. Within how many feet of an intersection may you not pass? → 100 feet
6. When a traffic signal is not working, how should you treat the intersection? → As a four-way stop
7. What does a steady yellow signal mean? → The signal is changing from green to red; stop if safe
8. What must you do before opening your car door after parking? → Check mirrors and traffic
