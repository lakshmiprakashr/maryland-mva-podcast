# Chapter-08 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Covers what to do after a crash (injury vs. no injury scenarios, information exchange, hit-and-run), roadside emergencies (finding safe place, staying in or exiting vehicle), proper conduct during traffic stops, and Maryland's Move Over Law with its penalty structure.

## Why It Matters

Knowing what to do after a crash protects you legally and physically. Improper crash response can result in criminal charges, license revocation, and prison time. Traffic stop conduct and the Move Over Law are directly tested on the MVA exam.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Crash with injury:** Remain at scene, call 911, report number of people, type of injury, location; do not move vehicles
- **Hit-and-run (injury):** License revoked, prison up to 5 years, fine up to $5,000
- **Hit-and-run (death):** Felony, prison up to 10 years, fine up to $10,000
- **No injury but vehicle can't move:** Call 911, stay away from traffic, do not cross road
- **No injury and vehicle can move:** Move off road, exchange info (name, address, phone, license plate, license number, vehicle make/model, insurance), get witness info, document damage and conditions
- **Always call police when:** Someone injured, vehicle can't move, driver appears impaired, driver has no license, driver tries to leave, public property damaged
- **Unattended vehicle/property:** Stop, try to find owner, leave note with contact info if owner not found
- **Domestic animal hit:** Immediately call police (dogs, cats, horses, cows)
- **Roadside emergency:** Assess situation, find safe place, stay buckled if in vehicle, exit to higher ground behind barrier if not safe to stay
- **Traffic stops:** Pull far off road, flashers on, engine off, window down, stay in vehicle, seat belt on, hands in plain view on steering wheel, do not argue about citation
- **Move Over Law:** Move over or slow down for stopped vehicles with flashing lights; $110 fine + 1 point; $150 + 3 points if causes crash; $750 + 3 points if death/serious injury

## MVA Test Traps

- Thinking you can leave the scene if no one appears hurt — remain at scene if anyone is injured
- Confusing the penalty amounts — $5,000 for injury hit-and-run vs. $10,000 for death
- Thinking you must always stop for roadside emergencies on the shoulder — you should move to a safe place if possible
- Forgetting that you must stay buckled even if your vehicle is disabled
- Thinking the Move Over Law only applies to police cars — it applies to all vehicles with flashing lights
- Confusing point values — 1 point for violation, 3 points if it causes a crash or death

## Common Mistakes

- Not exchanging information after a minor crash
- Forgetting to document weather and road conditions
- Leaving the scene before police arrive when someone is injured
- Standing near a disabled vehicle instead of moving to safety
- Not turning off engine and radio during a traffic stop
- Keeping hands hidden instead of on steering wheel during a traffic stop
- Not knowing when you must call the police after a crash

## Memory Tricks

- **Crash response:** "Injury = stay, call 911; No injury = move if possible, exchange info"
- **Hit-and-run:** "Injury = 5 years, $5K; Death = 10 years, $10K" (double both)
- **Traffic stop:** "Pull over, flashers, engine off, window down, hands up, belt on"
- **Move Over Law:** "Flashers = move over or slow down"
- **Points:** "1 point base, 3 points if crash, 3 points if death"

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- Hit-and-run with serious injury: license revoked, up to 5 years prison, $5,000 fine
- Hit-and-run with death: felony, up to 10 years prison, $10,000 fine
- Move Over Law: $110 fine + 1 point; $150 + 3 points if causes crash; $750 + 3 points if death/serious injury
- Points system: MVA uses points to track violations and issue penalties leading to suspension/revocation
- Must call police when: injury, vehicle immobilized, impaired driver, unlicensed driver, hit-and-run attempt, public property damage
- Domestic animal collision: must immediately call police
