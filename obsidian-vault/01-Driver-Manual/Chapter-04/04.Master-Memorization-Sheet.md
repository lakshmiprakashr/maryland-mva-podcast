# Chapter-04 - Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary
Traffic signals (all types including arrows and flashing), crosswalk signals, traffic sign colors and shapes, regulatory signs, warning signs, and guide signs.

## Why It Matters
Traffic signs and signals are one of the most heavily tested areas on the Maryland knowledge exam. You must instantly recognize signs by color and shape to pass.

## High-Yield Facts
- **Traffic control devices** = signals, signs, and pavement markings that regulate, warn, and guide traffic
- Only exceptions to obeying control devices: police officer or uniformed school crossing guard
- **Steady Red**: Complete stop; yield to pedestrians/all traffic; right turn on red allowed unless prohibited; left turn one-way to one-way only
- **Steady Yellow**: Signal changing from green to red; stop if safe; if too close, proceed through
- **Steady Green**: Proceed with caution; yield to pedestrians and vehicles already in intersection
- **Steady Red Arrow**: Complete stop; cannot turn in arrow direction until green
- **Steady Yellow Arrow**: Permission to turn in arrow direction is ending
- **Steady Green Arrow**: Proceed in arrow direction; still yield to pedestrians/vehicles in intersection
- **Flashing Red**: Complete stop; treat as stop sign; yield; proceed when clear
- **Flashing Yellow**: Slow down; proceed with caution
- **Flashing Red Arrow**: Complete stop; yield; proceed in arrow direction when clear
- **Lane Use Signals**: Red X = never drive in that lane; Green arrow = you may drive; Steady yellow X = move out safely; Flashing yellow X = left turn only
- **Crosswalk signals**: Pedestrian sees walking person/don't walk hand; Driver sees solid red = stop, flashing red = stop then proceed with caution, solid yellow = get ready to stop

## Sign Colors (MEMORIZE)
- **Red**: Stop, yield, do not enter, wrong way
- **Yellow**: General warning ahead
- **White**: Speed limit, keep right, some guide signs
- **Orange**: Construction/work zone warning
- **Green**: Destinations, distances, directions
- **Blue**: Services (food, gas, rest areas)
- **Brown**: Recreation, cultural interest areas
- **Fluorescent Yellow-Green**: School, pedestrian, bicycle signs
- **Fluorescent Pink**: Emergency traffic incidents
- **Black**: One Way signs, changeable message signs

## Sign Shapes (MEMORIZE)
- **Octagon (8 sides)**: Stop — always means stop
- **Triangle (3 sides)**: Yield — slow down, give right-of-way
- **Diamond**: Warning — hazards ahead, may need to slow down
- **Pennant**: No passing zone — do not pass
- **Rectangle**: Regulatory (vertical) or Guide (horizontal)
- **Pentagon (5 sides)**: School zone and school crossing
- **Round**: Railroad warning ahead
- **Crossbuck**: Highway-rail grade crossing location
- **Trapezoid**: Recreation, cultural interest, National Forest routes

## Must-Know Regulatory Signs
- No Right Turn, No Left Turn, No U-Turn, No Turns
- Left Turn Only, Do Not Enter, No Parking, No Turn On Red
- Do Not Pass, Stay to Right of Median
- Left Turn Yield on Green, Bike Lane, Wrong Way
- Reserved for Disability Parking Permits
- Two-way Left Turn Lane, One Way, HOV Lane

## Must-Know Warning Signs
- Stop Ahead, Yield Ahead, Signal Ahead
- Sharp Right Turn, Pedestrian Crossing, Traffic Circle/Roundabout
- No Passing Zone, Lane Ends Merge Left, Road Divides
- Steep Hill, Slippery When Wet, Railroad Before T-Intersection
- School Crossing Ahead, School Bus Stop Ahead
- Work Zone Active when Flashing (increased fines)

## Guide Signs
- Interstate shields, U.S. Route markers, State Route markers
- Mile Markers (every mile on interstate)
- Service signs: blue background for food, gas, hospital, lodging, airport, train
- Destination guide signs: green background for distances and directions
- Park and Ride signs

## MVA Test Traps
- "A flashing red light means proceed with caution" — it means complete stop, treat as stop sign
- "You must stop at a yellow light" — you should stop if safe; if too close, proceed through
- "A diamond sign means stop" — diamond means WARNING, not stop (octagon = stop)
- "Red X means you may drive in the lane" — red X means NEVER drive in that lane
- "A green arrow means you can go without yielding" — you must still yield to pedestrians and vehicles in the intersection
- "Flashing yellow means stop" — flashing yellow means slow down and proceed with caution, do not stop

## Common Mistakes
- Confusing solid yellow arrow with flashing yellow arrow (solid = permission ending; flashing = caution, proceed)
- Forgetting that a non-working signal means four-way stop
- Mixing up sign colors (red = stop/wrong way; yellow = warning; orange = construction)
- Forgetting that you must stop at a flashing red at a railroad crossing even if no train is visible
- Not yielding to pedestrians when turning on a green arrow

## Memory Tricks
- **Sign colors**: "Rusty Old Yellows Get Brighter Every Day" = Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Brown, fluorescent Yellow-Green, fluorescent Pink, Black
- **Sign shapes**: "Stop = Octagon (8), Yield = Triangle (3), Warn = Diamond (4)"
- **Signal sequence**: "Red means stop, Yellow means caution, Green means go — add Arrow for protected turns"
- **Flashing signals**: Flashing Red = Stop sign; Flashing Yellow = Caution only
- **Lane signals**: Red X = No; Green Arrow = Yes; Yellow X = Exit; Flashing Yellow X = Left turn

## Maryland-Specific Laws
- Only police officers and uniformed school crossing guards may direct you to disobey a traffic control device
- You may not drive across private property to avoid a traffic control device
- You must stop at flashing red signals at railroad crossings even if no train is visible
- Red light turn: right turn allowed unless sign prohibits; left turn only one-way to one-way

## Rapid Recall Questions
1. What does a flashing red signal mean? → Complete stop; treat as stop sign; yield; proceed when clear
2. What does a green arrow signal mean? → Proceed in arrow direction; yield to pedestrians and vehicles in intersection
3. What color background indicates a construction zone sign? → Orange
4. What shape is a school zone sign? → Pentagon (five-sided)
5. When must you stop at a flashing red at a railroad crossing? → Always, even if no train is visible
6. What must you do when a traffic signal is not working? → Treat as four-way stop
7. What does a steady yellow arrow mean? → Permission to turn in the arrow direction is ending
8. What color are service signs for food, gas, and rest areas? → Blue
