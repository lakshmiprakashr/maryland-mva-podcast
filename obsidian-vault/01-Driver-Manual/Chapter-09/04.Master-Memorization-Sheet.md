# Chapter 09 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Driving is a **privilege, not a right**. Maryland MVA can impose restrictions, suspend, revoke, or cancel your license. This chapter covers four key areas: license restrictions for provisional drivers, implied consent for drug and alcohol testing, administrative actions against your license, and penalties for violations — including disability parking misuse.

## Why It Matters

Every restriction, penalty, and administrative action exists to protect you and others on the road. Knowing these rules prevents costly fines, license loss, and — most importantly — dangerous situations. The MVA test heavily tests the differences between suspension, revocation, and cancellation, and the escalating penalty structure for provisional drivers.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Driving is a privilege** — MVA can impose or remove it
- **Seat Belt Restriction**: Provisional License holders AND all passengers must wear safety belts or child seats at all times
- **Passenger Restriction**: Under 18 with Provisional License — no unsupervised passengers under 18 for first **151 days** (unless immediate family)
- **Nighttime Restriction**: No unsupervised driving **12:00 AM – 5:00 AM** except for work, volunteer, school activity, or athletic event/training
- **Wireless Device Restriction**: No wireless device use (including hands-free) with Provisional License or Learner's Permit — **only exception is 911**
- **Implied Consent**: By driving in Maryland, you agree to take a drug/alcohol test if asked
- **BAC limit**: **.08 percent** — license suspended at or above
- **Refusing a test**: MVA will suspend your license upon certified refusal
- **False/forged ID or license**: Fine and/or prison under federal and state law
- **License suspension** = temporary loss of driving privilege; restricted license possible
- **License revocation** = more severe; minimum waiting period before reinstatement
- **License cancellation** = most severe; driving privilege **terminated and not reinstated**
- **MVA notice** required for all administrative actions — must state action, reason, and steps to appeal
- **Minors (under 18) penalties** for moving violations with Provisional License:
  - 1st offense: driver improvement program
  - 2nd offense: 30-day suspension + 90-day employment/education-only restriction
  - 3rd offense: 180-day suspension + driver improvement program for young drivers + 180-day restriction
  - 4th+ offense: license **revoked**; must pass all tests again
- **Adults (18+) penalties** for moving violations with Provisional License:
  - 1st offense: driver improvement program
  - 2nd offense: 30-day suspension
  - 3rd+ offense: up to 180-day suspension or revocation
- After each conviction or PBJ: new **18-month conviction-free period** required
- Penalties are **in addition to** court-imposed penalties
- **Disability parking**: Big fines for illegal use — must have plate/placard AND qualified person present
- **Curb cuts and access aisles**: Cannot block even with disability plate/placard
- Breathalyzer car lock required after DUI conviction

## MVA Test Traps

- **"151 days"** not "180 days" for passenger restriction — students often confuse this number
- **Cancellation is MORE severe than revocation**, not the other way around — cancellation = permanent termination
- **Refusing a test = automatic suspension** — many think refusing avoids consequences
- **Wireless device restriction applies even if hands-free** — students think "hands-free = allowed"
- **Immediate family** includes step-relatives and anyone living at your address — not just parents/siblings
- **Provisional License holders (adults 18+) still face penalties** — students assume penalties only apply to minors
- **Disability parking: you can be ticketed even with a placard** if blocking a curb cut or access aisle
- **Penalties are IN ADDITION to court penalties** — not a replacement
- **18-month conviction-free period restarts** after each violation — not cumulative

## Common Mistakes

- Confusing suspension (temporary) with revocation (more severe) with cancellation (permanent)
- Thinking the passenger restriction applies to ALL new drivers (it applies only to provisional holders under 18)
- Believing nighttime driving exceptions cover social outings (they do not — only work, volunteer, school, athletic)
- Forgetting that wireless device restriction includes hands-free phones for provisional/permit holders
- Not knowing that refusing a breath/blood test results in license suspension
- Assuming penalties after suspension/restriction are court-only (MVA imposes additional penalties)

## Memory Tricks

- **"S.R.C."** for administrative actions: **S**uspension, **R**evocation, **C**ancellation — think severity escalates: S → R → C
- **"1-5-1"** for passenger restriction: **1**st **5**1 days (remember the extra 1: **151** days)
- **"Midnight to five, keep it alive"** — nighttime restriction window
- **"No phone, no exceptions"** for wireless device restriction (except 911)
- **"0-8 means fate"** — BAC of .08 and above = license suspended
- **"1, 2, 3, gone"** — 1st = improvement, 2nd = 30 days, 3rd = 180 days, 4th = revoked

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

No specific road signs in this chapter. Focus is on license restrictions, penalties, and administrative actions.

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **Implied Consent Law**: Driving in Maryland = automatic consent to drug/alcohol testing
- **Provisional License restrictions** apply to graduated license holders under 18
- **Wireless device ban**: Provisional License or Learner's Permit — no wireless device use while driving (including hands-free), except 911
- **Disability parking**: Must have MVA-issued plate/placard AND qualified person as driver or passenger
- **False/forged documents**: Federal and state law penalties; license may be suspended
- **PBJ (Probation Before Judgment)**: Counts same as conviction for MVA penalty purposes
- **Breathalyzer interlock**: Required after drinking and driving conviction
- **18-month conviction-free period** resets after each moving violation for provisional holders

## Rapid Recall Questions

1. **Q:** What are the four restrictions for provisional drivers under 18?
   **A:** Seat belt, passenger, nighttime, and wireless device restrictions.

2. **Q:** How long does the passenger restriction last for provisional drivers under 18?
   **A:** The first 151 days — after that, they may drive with non-family passengers under 18.

3. **Q:** What does "implied consent" mean in Maryland?
   **A:** By driving in Maryland, you automatically agree to take a drug or alcohol test if a law enforcement officer asks you to.

4. **Q:** At what BAC level will your license be suspended?
   **A:** .08 percent or higher.

5. **Q:** What is the difference between suspension, revocation, and cancellation?
   **A:** Suspension is temporary; revocation is more severe with a waiting period before reinstatement; cancellation is permanent — your privilege is terminated and not reinstated.

6. **Q:** Can a provisional driver use a hands-free phone while driving?
   **A:** No. The wireless device restriction applies to all wireless communication devices including hands-free, with the only exception being a 911 emergency call.

7. **Q:** What are the four nighttime driving exceptions for provisional drivers?
   **A:** Driving to/from/during work, organized volunteer programs, official school activities, or athletic events/training sessions.

8. **Q:** What happens if you refuse a drug or alcohol test in Maryland?
   **A:** Your license will be suspended — a certified refusal statement from a police officer triggers automatic suspension.

9. **Q:** Can you be fined for disability parking misuse even with a valid placard?
   **A:** Yes — if you park in front of a curb cut, on an access aisle, or without the qualifying person present.

10. **Q:** After each moving violation conviction as a provisional holder, how long must you remain violation-free?
    **A:** A new 18-month conviction-free period begins after each conviction or PBJ.
