# Chapter 10 — Rookie Manual — Answer Key

## Answers with Explanations

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** A) (1) Maintain proper space cushion, (2) check traffic in all directions, (3) select a safe gap, (4) use turn signal, (5) recheck in the direction of the lane change, (6) adjust speed and steer smoothly into the lane, (7) cancel turn signal once established.

**Explanation:** This is a complete sequence. The recheck step is critical because traffic conditions can change between your initial check and the lane change.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Signal and go" — there are seven steps, including maintaining space, rechecking, and canceling the signal.

**Common Mistake:** Skipping the recheck step or forgetting to cancel the signal after the lane change.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 10, Changing Lanes section

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** C) A child may run into traffic, a driver ahead may stop suddenly, someone may swerve into your lane while looking at a phone. Unexpected hazards can appear at any time.

**Explanation:** The manual states that "risk is the constant danger that something unexpected will happen on the road." It is not limited to busy roads or bad weather.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Risk is only in heavy traffic" — risk exists everywhere, all the time.

**Common Mistake:** Letting your guard down on quiet or residential roads because they "seem safe."

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 10, Risk Is Everywhere section

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** C) Within 12 inches of the curb.

**Explanation:** Twelve inches is the maximum distance. This is approximately one foot. Parking farther than this is a violation and creates hazards.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Close to the curb" is not specific enough — the manual says 12 inches maximum.

**Common Mistake:** Parking 2-3 feet from the curb and believing it's close enough.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 10, Leaving Traffic section

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** C) Physically turn your head to check blind spots.

**Explanation:** Mirrors have blind spots that cannot show vehicles approaching from behind in adjacent lanes. A head turn is the only way to see into these areas.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Both mirrors cover blind spots" — they don't. The manual explicitly requires a head turn.

**Common Mistake:** Relying on mirrors because turning your head feels inconvenient.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 10, Blind Spots section

**MVA Test Trap:** "Novice drivers are safer because they drive slowly" — speed doesn't help if you don't recognize the hazard.

### Question 8
**Correct Answer:** C) Without a proper space cushion, you don't have room to maneuver if the car ahead brakes suddenly or the car behind accelerates into your intended space.

**Explanation:** The space cushion is the first step in the lane change sequence because it provides a safety buffer. Without it, you're committed to the change with no room for error.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Space cushion is optional" — it's step one of seven for a reason.

### Question 19
**Correct Answer:** B) No. The driver should wait for a safe gap. Speeding up to force a merge is dangerous and unpredictable.

**Explanation:** The manual says to "select a safe gap" and "adjust speed." Speeding up to force a merge puts you in the approaching car's path if they don't slow down.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Just go faster to fit in" — the manual says to select a safe gap, not force one.

**Common Mistake:** Feeling pressured to merge and creating an unsafe situation.

### Question 20
**Correct Answer:** D) (1) Search ahead for a safe and legal place to park, (2) check mirrors, (3) turn on your turn signal, (4) slow the vehicle, (5) steer within 12 inches of the curb.

**Explanation:** Planning ahead is step one — don't suddenly swerve to park. Signal, slow, and park within the 12-inch curb requirement.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Just pull over" — five steps are required, including searching ahead and signaling.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to signal before pulling over to park.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 10, Leaving Traffic section
