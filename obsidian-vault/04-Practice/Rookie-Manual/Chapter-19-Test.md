**Q17. [SYNTHESIS]** Compare the three different crash scenarios covered in the manual: injuries, no injuries with immovable vehicle, and no injuries with movable vehicle. What is the key decision point that determines which procedure to follow?

- A) Varies by county
- B) Only required for commercial vehicles
- C) The chain is: (1) officer issues citation and driver signs it (not admitting guilt), (2) driver is convicted or receives PBJ in court, (3) the eighteen-month provisional waiting period restarts from zero, (4) driver must attend Driver Improvement Program, (5) if driver fails to attend, license is suspended. Each step leads to the next and the consequences compound.
- D) The key decision point is whether anyone is injured. If yes: call 911, stay at scene, do not move vehicles. If no injuries, the next question is whether the vehicle can move. If it cannot: call 911, move to safety, use flashers/flares. If it can: move out of travel lane and collect information. The decision tree branches based on injury status first, then vehicle mobility.
