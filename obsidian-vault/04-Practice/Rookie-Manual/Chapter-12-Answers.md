# Chapter 12 — Rookie Manual — Answer Key

## Answers with Explanations

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** A) The shared left turn lane is used exclusively for making left turns into or out of driveways, parking lots, and for entering traffic from a driveway.

**Explanation:** It provides a designated space for left-turning vehicles so they do not block through traffic. It is not a regular driving lane.

**MVA Test Trap:** Many students think it can be used to pass slower traffic. It cannot.

**Common Mistake:** Treating the shared left turn lane as a regular travel or passing lane.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** B) 3 to 4 seconds before moving into the shared left turn lane.

**Explanation:** Early signaling alerts drivers behind you and oncoming traffic to your intentions, giving them time to react.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students sometimes think signaling on entry is sufficient. The manual requires signaling 3 to 4 seconds BEFORE entry.

**Common Mistake:** Signaling at the same time as steering into the lane rather than in advance.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** D) No more than 2 to 3 seconds before the turn.

**Explanation:** Entering too early creates a hazard because you are occupying the shared lane longer than necessary, increasing the risk of a head-on collision.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking you can enter the shared lane whenever convenient. The time limit is strict.

**Common Mistake:** Entering the shared lane far in advance and driving in it for several seconds.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** A) You must yield to traffic already in the roundabout coming from your left.

**Explanation:** Traffic in the roundabout has the right of way. You enter only when there is a safe gap from the left.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often think you yield to the right like at a normal intersection. In a roundabout, you yield to the left.

**Common Mistake:** Yielding to the right instead of the left.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 5
**Correct Answer:** B) As you approach your intended exit.

**Explanation:** Signaling right tells drivers behind you that you are leaving the roundabout at the next exit. Do not signal until you are approaching your exit.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think you signal when entering the roundabout. You only signal right when exiting.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to signal right or signaling too early.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 6
**Correct Answer:** A) 3 feet.

**Explanation:** Maryland law requires a minimum 3-foot space cushion when overtaking a bicyclist. This protects them from being hit by your vehicle.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may confuse this with other states' rules or guess 4 feet. In Maryland, it is 3 feet.

**Common Mistake:** Underestimating how much space a bicyclist needs.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 7
**Correct Answer:** C) Yield to pedestrians and bicyclists when exiting the roundabout.

**Explanation:** Even though you are leaving the roundabout, pedestrians and bicyclists in the crosswalk or path have the right of way.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think yielding only applies when entering the roundabout. You yield both entering AND exiting.

**Common Mistake:** Failing to check for pedestrians when exiting.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 8
**Correct Answer:** A) Safe complex intersection navigation, proper use of shared left turn lane for entering and exiting traffic, safe lane changes, obeying all traffic signs and signals, observing all posted speed limits, and feeling comfortable on large busy roads.

**Explanation:** The Coach's Checklist ensures the rookie driver has mastered the current skill set before advancing. All items must be checked yes.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may not know that a parent or supervising driver must verify all skills before advancing.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming partial proficiency is enough to move forward.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 9
**Correct Answer:** C) Signal left 3 to 4 seconds before moving. Check traffic in all directions. Enter the shared left turn lane no more than 2 to 3 seconds before the turn. Wait for a safe gap in oncoming traffic. Complete the left turn into the parking lot.

**Explanation:** Following the exact sequence ensures you occupy the shared lane for the minimum time and complete the turn safely.

**MVA Test Trap:** Entering the shared lane too early or not signaling in advance are the most common errors tested.

**Common Mistake:** Not waiting for a safe gap in oncoming traffic before turning.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 10
**Correct Answer:** D) Move into the shared left turn lane, stop, switch to your right turn signal, recheck oncoming traffic, and when traffic clears, accelerate and merge into the nearest lane.

**Explanation:** This is the two-phase entry process. First you get into the shared lane, then you transition into active traffic with the right signal.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students forget to switch signals from left to right, which confuses drivers behind them.

**Common Mistake:** Not switching from left signal to right signal during the maneuver.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 11
**Correct Answer:** B) Slow down, obey all signs and pavement markings, yield to pedestrians at the crosswalk, then yield to traffic already in the roundabout coming from your left. Enter when safe.

**Explanation:** Roundabouts require you to yield at two points: pedestrians at entry and exit, and circulating traffic from the left.

**MVA Test Trap:** Forgetting to yield to pedestrians at the entry and exit points.

**Common Mistake:** Only focusing on vehicular traffic and forgetting about pedestrians and bicyclists.

**Reference:** See chapter

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may not know that driving in the shared lane is prohibited when not actively turning.

### Question 14
**Correct Answer:** C) At least 3 feet of clearance.

**Explanation:** Maryland law requires a minimum 3-foot space cushion when passing a bicyclist, whether or not a bicycle lane exists.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think bicycle lanes are always required. Many Maryland roads do not have them, but the 3-foot rule still applies.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the 3-foot rule only applies when bicycle lanes are present.

### Question 15
**Correct Answer:** B) Identify the correct left turn lane for your destination and move into it one to two blocks ahead.

**Explanation:** Getting into the correct lane early prevents dangerous last-second lane changes and ensures you turn into the correct corresponding lane.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may not realize that turning from the wrong lane can result in a crash or violation.

**Common Mistake:** Waiting until the intersection to try to change into the correct turn lane.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 16
**Correct Answer:** B) Entering a driveway is a single-action maneuver — signal, enter, yield, turn. Entering traffic is a two-phase maneuver — signal left, enter the shared lane, stop, switch to right signal, recheck, then merge into traffic.

**Explanation:** Entering traffic requires changing signals and re-entering the flow of traffic, making it more complex than simply turning into a driveway.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may use the simpler driveway procedure when entering traffic, missing the signal switch and recheck steps.

**Common Mistake:** Not distinguishing between the two procedures and applying the wrong one.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 17
**Correct Answer:** A) Roundabouts allow continuous traffic flow without stopping at a light. They work because all entering traffic yields to circulating traffic from the left, and exiting drivers signal to leave.

**Explanation:** The yield-to-left rule creates a one-way flow pattern. The exit signal helps drivers behind predict movements. Together, these rules eliminate the need for traffic signals.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think roundabouts are the same as traffic circles. Roundabouts use yield rules, not traffic signals.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing roundabouts with traditional traffic circles that may have signals.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 18
**Correct Answer:** B) Bicyclists travel on the same roads as cars but are much more vulnerable. The 3-foot rule recognizes that they need physical space for safety, even on roads without dedicated bicycle lanes.

**Explanation:** Many Maryland roads lack bicycle lanes. The 3-foot rule ensures bicyclists can ride safely regardless of infrastructure. It is part of the shared road principle.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think the 3-foot rule only applies where bicycle lanes are marked. It applies on all Maryland roads.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the rule is conditional on road design rather than universal.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 19
**Correct Answer:** C) The Coach's Checklist is the practical assessment tool that parents use to verify a rookie driver has mastered the current skill level before advancing to more challenging driving environments.

**Explanation:** The GDL system requires supervised practice. The Coach's Checklist ensures that progression is based on demonstrated skill, not just time behind the wheel.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think the checklist is optional. In Maryland, it is a required part of the supervised driving process.

**Common Mistake:** Not understanding that a supervising driver must sign off on all checklist items.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 20
**Correct Answer:** D) Trucks and buses need more room to turn, so you must give them extra space at intersections. They also have larger blind spots, making lane changes near them riskier.

**Explanation:** Large vehicles swing wide when turning. Their blind spots are much larger than a car's. The manual specifically warns to stay alert for them at intersections and when changing lanes.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may not realize that a truck's turning radius requires them to use a different lane, which could conflict with your lane.

**Common Mistake:** Cutting off a truck or bus at an intersection without accounting for their wider turning radius.

**Reference:** See chapter
