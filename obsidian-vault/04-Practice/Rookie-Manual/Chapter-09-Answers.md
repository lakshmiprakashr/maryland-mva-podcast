# Chapter 09 — Rookie Manual — Answer Key

## Answers with Explanations

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** A) At least 3 to 4 seconds in advance.

**Explanation:** Maryland law requires signaling at least 3-4 seconds before turning. This gives other drivers adequate time to react to your maneuver.

**MVA Test Trap:** "1-2 seconds is enough" — the manual specifies 3-4 seconds. Signaling too late is a common violation.

**Common Mistake:** Waiting until right before the turn to signal, giving other drivers no time to react.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Turning at Intersection section

---

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** C) Within 12 inches of the curb.

**Explanation:** Parking more than 12 inches from the curb is a violation. It also creates a hazard for cyclists and pedestrians, and can obstruct traffic.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Close to the curb" is vague — the manual specifies 12 inches, which is about one foot.

**Common Mistake:** Parking 2-3 feet from the curb and thinking it's close enough.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Leaving Traffic section

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** D) Come to a full and complete stop first, then proceed when the intersection is clear and safe.

**Explanation:** A right turn on red is only legal after a complete stop. You cannot roll through the stop. After stopping, check for traffic and pedestrians, then proceed when safe.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Slow down and look" is not enough. You must come to a FULL STOP.

**Common Mistake:** Performing a rolling stop before turning right on red.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Turning at Intersection section

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** A) Mirrors cannot show all areas around the vehicle. Blind spots exist in areas that mirrors don't cover, and only physically turning your head reveals them.

**Explanation:** Every vehicle has blind spots in the areas just beside and behind it. These spots are not visible in side or rearview mirrors. Failing to check them is a leading cause of lane-change collisions.

**MVA Test Trap:** "My mirrors cover everything" — they don't. The manual specifically says to turn your head.

**Common Mistake:** Relying only on mirrors when changing lanes or turning.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Blind Spots section

---

### Question 5
**Correct Answer:** C) Check for vehicles, pedestrians, signs, and devices. Turn head to check blind spots. Signal 3-4 seconds in advance. Position vehicle in correct lane. Adjust speed and stop if required. Identify safe gap. Check mirrors for hazards. Steer into proper lane. Adjust speed as necessary.

**Explanation:** This is a nine-step sequence. The MVA tests whether you know each step, especially the blind spot check and the 3-4 second signal requirement.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Just signal and turn" — there are nine steps, including blind spot checks and speed adjustments.

**Common Mistake:** Skipping the blind spot check because mirrors "seem to show everything."

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Turning at Intersection section

### Question 6
**Correct Answer:** A) The driver failed to verify the intersection was clear before proceeding. A green light means permission to go, not that it's automatically safe.

**Explanation:** The manual specifically states to "make sure that the intersection is clear before proceeding" when the light turns green. Other vehicles may still be clearing the intersection.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Green light means I have right of way" — right of way doesn't help if a car is physically in the intersection.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming green automatically means safe to go.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Intersections section

**Common Mistake:** Signaling too late because 3-4 seconds feels like an eternity at an intersection.

### Question 9
**Correct Answer:** C) They forgot to turn their head and check the blind spot. Mirrors alone do not show all areas around the vehicle.

**Explanation:** The manual specifically requires turning your head to check blind spots before changing lanes. Mirrors have inherent blind spots that can hide vehicles.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Both mirrors are enough" — mirrors are not enough. Head turn is required.

### Question 10
**Correct Answer:** D) Yellow lights mean the bus is preparing to stop. Slow down and be prepared to stop, but do not stop yet. Only stop when the red lights activate and the stop arm extends.

**Explanation:** Yellow lights are a warning, not a legal trigger to stop. Red lights plus the stop arm are what require you to stop (assuming no physical divider).

**MVA Test Trap:** "Yellow means stop" — yellow means caution. Red plus stop arm means stop.

**Common Mistake:** Stopping prematurely for yellow lights, creating a rear-end collision risk.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, School Bus section

---

### Question 11
**Correct Answer:** B) Safe means not blocking traffic, not too close to intersections or driveways, not on a hill without wheel chocks. Legal means no parking restrictions, no fire hydrants, no bus stops, no handicapped zones without permits.

**Explanation:** Parking must be both safe (not creating hazards) and legal (not violating parking regulations). The manual says to "search ahead" — don't wait until the last second.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Any spot is fine" — many spots are illegal or dangerous. Planning ahead is required.

**Common Mistake:** Parking in restricted areas without noticing the signs.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Leaving Traffic section

### Question 12
**Correct Answer:** C) After completing a turn, you should steer into the proper lane for continuing travel. Staying in a turn-only lane can obstruct traffic and create a hazard.

**Explanation:** The manual says to "steer into the proper lane" after turning. This means transitioning to the correct travel lane promptly.

**MVA Test Trap:** "The turn is done, I'm fine" — you must complete the maneuver by entering the correct lane.

**Common Mistake:** Lingering in a turn lane after the turn is complete.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Turning at Intersection section

**MVA Test Trap:** "1 second is still signaling" — it is, but it's not enough. The law requires 3-4 seconds minimum.

**Common Mistake:** Signaling at the last moment because the turn feels "obvious."

### Question 20
**Correct Answer:** D) You must check to the rear, sides, and front of the vehicle to ensure the way is clear.

**Explanation:** All three directions must be checked — rear, sides, and front. Checking only one or two directions can miss approaching traffic.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Check forward and to the sides" — the rear check is specifically required and often forgotten.

**Common Mistake:** Focusing only on cross-traffic and not checking for vehicles approaching from behind.
