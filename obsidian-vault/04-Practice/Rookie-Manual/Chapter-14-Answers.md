# Chapter 14 — Rookie Manual — Answer Key

## Answers with Explanations

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** C) Identify, Predict, Decide, Execute.

**Explanation:** I.P.D.E. is the hazard perception system taught in the Maryland Driver's Manual. You identify the risk, predict what might happen, decide on the best course of action, then execute it.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often mix up the order. Identify is always first. Predict comes before Decide.

**Common Mistake:** Reversing Predict and Decide.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** A) 3 to 4 seconds under ideal conditions.

**Explanation:** At highway speeds, vehicles cover much more distance per second. Three to four seconds provides adequate reaction time.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may answer 2 seconds, which is for lower-speed roads. Expressways require 3 to 4 seconds.

**Common Mistake:** Using the wrong following distance for the road type.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** B) Accelerate to highway speed and signal to indicate you are merging.

**Explanation:** The entrance ramp is designed for acceleration. Matching highway speed before merging prevents dangerous speed differentials.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think you should slow down to look for a gap. You must accelerate first.

**Common Mistake:** Entering the expressway too slowly.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** B) The deceleration lane.

**Explanation:** The deceleration lane allows you to slow down after leaving the travel lanes, preventing rear-end collisions from through traffic.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think they can brake in the travel lane. You should use the deceleration lane whenever available.

**Common Mistake:** Not using the deceleration lane and braking in the travel lane.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 5
**Correct Answer:** D) Unless you are entering on a green signal, you must stop completely.

**Explanation:** A complete stop gives you time to assess high-speed traffic before entering. Only a green signal exempts you from stopping.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think they can slow down instead of stopping. A complete stop is required.

**Common Mistake:** Rolling through a stop sign at a highway entry point.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 6
**Correct Answer:** B) High beams reflect off fog droplets and actually reduce your visibility. Low beams direct light below the fog line and are more effective.

**Explanation:** Fog contains suspended water droplets that reflect light. High beams bounce light back at you, creating a wall of glare.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think more light equals better visibility. In fog, high beams make things worse.

**Common Mistake:** Using high beams in fog because they seem brighter.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 7
**Correct Answer:** B) Check that all exterior lights work, clean windows and headlights inside and out, and adjust the rear-view mirror to reduce glare.

**Explanation:** Dirty windows increase glare, dirty headlights reduce efficiency, and an unadjusted mirror reflects other vehicles' headlights into your eyes.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may skip the pre-drive check. The manual specifically lists these as requirements.

**Common Mistake:** Not cleaning headlights and windows before night driving.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 8
**Correct Answer:** B) A minimum of 10 hours.

**Explanation:** Many teen crashes occur at night because new drivers fail to make necessary adjustments. The 10-hour requirement builds the experience needed for safe night driving.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may not know the specific number. It is 10 hours, not 5 or 15.

**Common Mistake:** Not knowing the exact GDL night driving hour requirement.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 9
**Correct Answer:** D) Continue accelerating on the ramp to match highway speed before merging. If you cannot reach highway speed, wait for a larger gap in traffic.

**Explanation:** Merging at a 25 mph speed differential is extremely dangerous. The ramp is designed to give you space to accelerate. Use all of it.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think they should merge at whatever speed they are going. Speed matching is critical.

**Common Mistake:** Merging too slowly and creating a hazard.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 10
**Correct Answer:** A) Signal right, check mirrors, perform a head check, move into the right lane well before the exit, then use the exit ramp or deceleration lane.

**Explanation:** Identifying your exit early and moving to the right lane in advance prevents dangerous last-second lane changes across multiple lanes.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think they can cut across lanes at the last second. The manual says to identify exits in adequate time.

**Common Mistake:** Waiting too long to change lanes for an exit.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 11
**Correct Answer:** A) No, you should wait for a larger gap. Careful gap selection is critical at highway speeds.

**Explanation:** Highway traffic moves much faster than cross-street traffic. A gap that seems adequate may close quickly at highway speeds.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think any gap is sufficient. The manual emphasizes LARGE gap selection.

**Common Mistake:** Underestimating how fast highway traffic approaches.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 12
**Correct Answer:** C) Switch to high beams to low beams.

**Explanation:** High beams aimed at oncoming drivers can temporarily blind them. Switching to low beams prevents this danger.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think they should keep high beams on for maximum visibility. You must switch to low beams.

**Common Mistake:** Not dimming high beams when oncoming traffic approaches.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 13
**Correct Answer:** A) Stop completely at the stop line, wait for the car to pass, check again for a safe gap, then accelerate into the proper lane when clear.

**Explanation:** You must stop completely and select a large gap. Highway traffic moves fast and you need time to accelerate to match their speed.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may try to time their entry with the approaching car. You must wait for a clear gap.

**Common Mistake:** Entering the highway with an inadequate gap.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 14
**Correct Answer:** B) Increase your following distance beyond the normal 3 to 4 seconds, reduce your speed, and ensure your headlights are on.

**Explanation:** Rain reduces traction and visibility. The manual says to increase following distance in bad weather. This gives you more time to react.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may maintain normal speed and following distance. Bad weather requires adjustments.

**Common Mistake:** Not adjusting speed or following distance for weather conditions.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 15
**Correct Answer:** D) Signal right, check rear-view mirror, check side mirror, perform a head check, move into the right lane, then take the exit using the deceleration lane.

**Explanation:** Each step must be completed in order. Signal first, then check, then move. This is the same sequence for any lane change, but on the expressway it must be done with extra care due to high speeds.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may skip the head check or try to change lanes without signaling.

**Common Mistake:** Not performing all mirror checks and head checks before changing lanes on the expressway.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 16
**Correct Answer:** C) From an entrance ramp, you accelerate to highway speed on the ramp before merging. From a cross street, you must stop completely (unless on green), select a large gap, then accelerate to highway speed after entering.

**Explanation:** The entrance ramp provides a dedicated acceleration zone. A cross street does not. Both require gap selection and signaling, but the starting conditions differ.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think both procedures are the same. The stop requirement at cross streets is the key difference.

**Common Mistake:** Not stopping at a cross-street entry when required.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 17
**Correct Answer:** B) Identify: look for gaps in highway traffic. Predict: estimate when a gap will reach your merge point. Decide: choose the best gap to merge into. Execute: accelerate smoothly into the gap and merge.

**Explanation:** I.P.D.E. is not just for intersections. It applies to every driving decision, including expressway merging where timing and judgment are critical.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think I.P.D.E. only applies to intersections and pedestrians.

**Common Mistake:** Not applying I.P.D.E. to expressway merging scenarios.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 18
**Correct Answer:** D) Light traffic gives the new driver more time to make decisions and more space to correct mistakes. Good weather eliminates environmental hazards. Weekend mornings typically have the lowest traffic volumes.

**Explanation:** Starting in ideal conditions builds confidence and skills progressively. Heavy traffic and bad weather add complexity that new drivers are not ready for.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think practicing in heavy traffic is more realistic. The manual recommends starting in light traffic.

**Common Mistake:** Starting expressway practice in difficult conditions.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 19
**Correct Answer:** C) At night, you must Identify reduced visibility and potential hazards like pedestrians. Predict that they may be harder to see. Decide to slow down or increase following distance. Execute those adjustments consistently.

**Explanation:** I.P.D.E. at night requires extra vigilance because hazards are harder to identify in darkness. The system still applies, but the Identify step is more challenging.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think I.P.D.E. does not apply at night. It applies at all times.

**Common Mistake:** Not adjusting I.P.D.E. awareness for reduced nighttime visibility.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 20
**Correct Answer:** D) The deceleration lane isolates slowing vehicles from through traffic. Without it, a vehicle braking in the travel lane would force all vehicles behind to brake, creating a chain reaction. The deceleration lane prevents this disruption.

**Explanation:** Expressway safety depends on consistent speeds in travel lanes. The deceleration lane lets exiting vehicles slow down without affecting through traffic flow.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may think the deceleration lane is optional. Using it is a critical safety practice.

**Common Mistake:** Not understanding why the deceleration lane exists and braking in the travel lane.

**Reference:** See chapter
