# Chapter 17 — Rookie Manual — Answer Key

## Answers with Explanations

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** C) Ten times greater.

**Explanation:** Snow dramatically reduces tire grip, requiring significantly more distance to bring a vehicle to a complete stop. This is the single most important number to remember about winter driving.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often guess "twice" or "five times" — the actual multiplier is ten, which is much higher than most people expect.

**Common Mistake:** Underestimating stopping distance and driving at normal speeds on snow.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** D) Low beams only.

**Explanation:** High beams angle upward and reflect off fog particles, creating a wall of glare that reduces visibility even more. Low beams stay below the fog layer.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Use high beams for maximum visibility" — this is the most common wrong answer on MVA tests about fog.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking any light is better than no light — the direction and angle of the beam matter more than brightness.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** B) Hydroplaning is when water builds up between your tires and the road surface, causing you to lose contact with the road. It is caused by water deeper than the tire tread combined with excessive speed.

**Explanation:** When water cannot escape through the tire tread channels fast enough, a wedge of water lifts the tire off the pavement, eliminating all friction and steering control.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking only speed causes hydroplaning — water depth and tire tread condition are equally important factors.

**Common Mistake:** Believing hydroplaning only happens during heavy rain storms.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** A) On bridges, overpasses, and shaded areas.

**Explanation:** Bridges freeze first because cold air flows both above and below the bridge deck. Shaded areas retain less solar heat. These predictability patterns are frequently tested.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking ice forms randomly — the MVA test expects you to know the specific locations.

**Common Mistake:** Not adjusting speed when approaching bridges during cold weather.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 5
**Correct Answer:** D) No more than 12 inches from the curb.

**Explanation:** Maryland law requires vehicles parked parallel to the curb to be within 12 inches. Parking farther out can obstruct traffic and is a citation-worthy offense.

**MVA Test Trap:** Confusing the 12-inch curb distance with the 2-3 feet gap between vehicles during setup.

**Common Mistake:** Not checking the curb distance after completing the parallel park.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 6
**Correct Answer:** A) Two to three feet between vehicles.

**Explanation:** This gap gives you enough room to maneuver into the space without hitting either vehicle. Your front seat should be in line with the vehicle you are parking behind.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking you should be closer to the other vehicle — being too close limits your maneuvering room.

**Common Mistake:** Not aligning your front seat with the other vehicle's before beginning to back in.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 7
**Correct Answer:** D) Make sure your entire vehicle is free of snow and ice before driving.

**Explanation:** Snow left on the roof, hood, or trunk can fly off while driving and obstruct the view of other drivers, creating a hazard. All windows and mirrors must also be cleared.

**MVA Test Trap:** Only clearing the windshield and ignoring the roof and hood.

**Common Mistake:** Driving with snow on the roof because "it will blow off eventually."

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 8
**Correct Answer:** C) To keep your windows clear of condensation caused by moisture in the air during wet weather.

**Explanation:** Wet weather increases humidity inside the vehicle, causing windows to fog up. Defoggers clear the glass so you can see through all windows.

**MVA Test Trap:** Only using the defroster on the front windshield and forgetting the rear defogger.

**Common Mistake:** Using recirculated air mode instead of fresh air mode, which actually makes fogging worse.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 9
**Correct Answer:** A) Check for traffic in all directions, carefully back up, turn the steering wheel in the direction you want to travel, and when the car clears the parking area, stop and shift to drive.

**Explanation:** This sequence ensures you check for hazards, create space, orient your vehicle, and transition smoothly into traffic.

**MVA Test Trap:** Forgetting to check for traffic before backing — pedestrians and vehicles may be passing behind you.

**Common Mistake:** Turning the wheel before backing up, which can cause you to hit the adjacent vehicle.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 10
**Correct Answer:** D) Ease off the accelerator gently. Do not brake suddenly or make sharp steering movements. Allow the vehicle to slow down gradually until the tires regain contact with the road.

**Explanation:** Braking or sharp steering during hydroplaning can cause a spin because the tires have no grip. Lifting off the gas lets speed decrease naturally.

**MVA Test Trap:** Slamming the brakes — this is the most dangerous instinctive reaction.

**Common Mistake:** Turning the wheel sharply to try to regain control, which causes a spin when traction returns.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 11
**Correct Answer:** D) Turn on your headlights immediately. Maryland law requires headlights when visibility is reduced, and the rain combined with dusk conditions make it necessary for you to see and be seen.

**Explanation:** Headlights serve a dual purpose — they help you see the road AND they help other drivers see your vehicle. Light rain reduces visibility for everyone.

**MVA Test Trap:** Waiting until it gets fully dark — by then, other drivers may not see you in the rain.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking headlights are only needed when it is completely dark outside.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 12
**Correct Answer:** B) Slow down before reaching the bridge, avoid braking or accelerating while on the bridge, and maintain a steady, reduced speed across it.

**Explanation:** Bridges freeze before regular road surfaces because cold air circulates both above and below the deck. The road before the bridge may look fine, but the bridge itself can be icy.

**MVA Test Trap:** Maintaining normal speed because the approach road looks clear — the bridge is a different surface that freezes independently.

**Common Mistake:** Braking while on the bridge, which can cause a skid on the icy surface.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 13
**Correct Answer:** A) Use both your low-beam headlights and your fog lights. Reduce your speed significantly and increase following distance.

**Explanation:** Fog lights are specifically designed to cut through fog without reflecting back. Combined with low beams, they provide the best possible visibility. Reduced speed gives you more time to react.

**MVA Test Trap:** Turning on high beams because you "cannot see enough" — this will make visibility worse.

**Common Mistake:** Driving at normal speed with fog lights on, thinking the lights solve the visibility problem.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 14
**Correct Answer:** A) Yield to the cyclist. Wait until they pass before beginning your parking maneuver. The manual states you must check traffic in all directions and yield to pedestrians, bicyclists, and other vehicles.

**Explanation:** Bicyclists have the right of way and you must yield during any parking maneuver. Signaling your intentions helps, but waiting is the safest choice.

**MVA Test Trap:** Signaling and beginning the maneuver anyway — signaling does not give you the right of way.

**Common Mistake:** Only checking for cars and forgetting to look for cyclists and pedestrians.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 15
**Correct Answer:** D) Check for traffic in all directions — vehicles approaching from both sides, pedestrians walking behind your car, and cyclists. Only begin backing when it is clear.

**Explanation:** Angle parking requires you to back out blind in many cases. Checking all directions before moving is critical because other drivers and pedestrians may not see you either.

**MVA Test Trap:** Only looking in the rearview mirror — mirrors have blind spots, especially at the close range of a parking lot.

**Common Mistake:** Rushing the backup without thorough checking because "it is just a parking lot."

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 16
**Correct Answer:** D) Slow down before entering the curve, steer smoothly through it, and maintain a steady speed. Do not brake or accelerate while in the curve.

**Explanation:** Sudden inputs on snow while the vehicle is already stressed by turning forces can break tire traction. Smooth, gradual inputs maintain control.

**MVA Test Trap:** Braking while in the curve — this is a common cause of winter accidents.

**Common Mistake:** Entering the curve at normal speed and then trying to brake mid-curve.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 17
**Correct Answer:** D) Both require headlights on, increased following distance, and smoother driving inputs. Rain additionally requires defoggers and awareness of hydroplaning. Snow requires clearing the entire vehicle of ice/snow, and stopping distances are 10 times greater versus the moderately increased distances in rain. Both conditions call for earlier braking, but snow requires even more gentleness with all controls.

**Explanation:** The fundamental principle for both is that reduced traction demands more space, less speed, and smoother inputs. The specific hazards differ — hydroplaning for rain, ice and snow accumulation for winter.

**MVA Test Trap:** Treating rain and snow as requiring the same response — snow is significantly more dangerous and requires greater caution.

**Common Mistake:** Not recognizing that snow demands even more caution than rain in every category.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 18
**Correct Answer:** D) Setting up (parallel to the front vehicle, 2-3 feet apart) establishes your starting position. Backing in with sharp steering gets you into the space at an angle. Adjusting (shifting to drive, steering opposite) straightens you out and positions you within 12 inches of the curb. Each step creates the conditions for the next — skipping or reordering them makes the maneuver fail.

**Explanation:** Parallel parking is a sequential maneuver where each step builds on the previous one. The setup determines your angle of entry, the backing determines your depth in the space, and the adjustment determines your final position.

**MVA Test Trap:** Trying to go straight into the space without the proper setup — this almost always results in hitting a curb or adjacent vehicle.

**Common Mistake:** Not completing the backing step fully before shifting to drive for the adjustment.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 19
**Correct Answer:** B) You must contact law enforcement if someone is injured, a vehicle cannot be moved, a driver appears under the influence, a driver has no license, a driver tries to leave without giving information, or public property is damaged. They all involve situations beyond a simple fender bender where documentation, investigation, or medical response is needed.

**Explanation:** These situations all require official intervention — either for medical emergencies, legal documentation, or because one party cannot or will not cooperate responsibly.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking you only need to call police for injuries — property damage and uncooperative drivers also require law enforcement.

**Common Mistake:** Trying to handle situations privately when law enforcement involvement is legally required.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 20
**Correct Answer:** B) The manual states parallel parking is tested on the MVA Skills Test and requires practice to do without hitting curbs or vehicles. The GDL system is designed to build skills progressively through supervised practice. Parking is a low-speed skill that builds spatial awareness and vehicle control — foundations that transfer to all driving situations. Mastering parking under supervision before driving alone reduces the risk of minor collisions that escalate into dangerous situations.

**Explanation:** The GDL philosophy is that skills develop through practice. Parking is one of the most technically demanding maneuvers for new drivers and requires repeated supervised practice to develop muscle memory and spatial judgment.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking parking is not important for the knowledge test — the concepts and procedures are frequently tested.

**Common Mistake:** Rushing through parking practice without achieving competency before moving to more complex skills.

**Reference:** See chapter
