# Chapter 21 — Rookie Manual — Answer Key

## Answers with Explanations

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** D) Drivers under 21 receive a citation for consuming even one drink and driving — any amount of alcohol is illegal.

**Explanation:** Maryland enforces zero tolerance for drivers under 21 because they are not legally old enough to drink. Any detectable alcohol while driving results in a citation, regardless of BAC level.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse this with the .08 BAC threshold that applies to drivers 21 and over. Zero tolerance means **any** alcohol for under-21 drivers.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking under-21 drivers get the same .08 BAC threshold as adults.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** A) A BAC of .07 to .079 constitutes DWI for drivers 21 and over.

**Explanation:** Maryland law defines DWI as operating a vehicle with a BAC between .07 and .079 for drivers 21 and older. This is a lesser charge than DUI but still carries serious penalties.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse DWI (.07-.079) with DUI (.08+). The MVA frequently swaps these thresholds.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking DWI and DUI are the same charge or that .08 is the DWI threshold.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** D) A BAC of .08 or above constitutes DUI for drivers 21 and over.

**Explanation:** DUI is the more serious impaired driving charge in Maryland. A BAC of .08 or higher triggers DUI charges for drivers 21 and older, with penalties including potential license suspension and criminal record.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse DUI (.08+) with DWI (.07-.079). Remember: the higher number is the more serious charge.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting that both DUI and DWI are separate charges with different BAC thresholds.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** C) Implied Consent means that by signing and accepting any Maryland driver's license or permit, you agree to be tested for drugs and alcohol when requested by law enforcement.

**Explanation:** When you accept a Maryland Learner's Permit, Provisional License, or full Driver's License, you automatically consent to chemical testing. Refusing to comply may result in the loss of your driving privilege.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think Implied Consent only applies to full license holders — it applies to permits, provisional licenses, and full licenses.

**Common Mistake:** Believing you can refuse a breathalyzer without consequences.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 5
**Correct Answer:** D) No — DUI, DWI, and Refusal to Test cannot be expunged from your driving record.

**Explanation:** These charges remain permanently on your driving record. Unlike some other violations, there is no process to have them removed, which can affect insurance rates and employment opportunities.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume these charges can be erased after a certain period. They are permanent.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that completing a program or waiting a certain number of years will result in expungement.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 6
**Correct Answer:** D) A minimum of 60 hours, including at least 10 hours of nighttime driving.

**Explanation:** Maryland requires drivers under 25 to complete 60 hours of behind-the-wheel practice. At least 10 of those hours must be at night. These hours are in addition to the 6 hours from driver education.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't forget the 10 nighttime hours — they are part of the 60 total, not additional.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing the 60-hour requirement with the 14-hour requirement for drivers 25 and older.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 14
**Correct Answer:** B) DWI is .07-.079 BAC; DUI is .08 or higher. Both are serious charges, but DUI carries more severe penalties.

**Explanation:** Maryland distinguishes between two levels of impaired driving for adults. DWI is the lesser charge at .07-.079 BAC, while DUI at .08+ carries more severe consequences. Both can result in license suspension and criminal records.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't treat DWI and DUI as synonyms — they are separate legal charges with different BAC thresholds.

**Common Mistake:** Failing to recognize that impairment can begin before the legal .08 limit.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 15
**Correct Answer:** C) Under 25: 60 hours (10 night). 25+: 14 hours (3 night). The difference reflects that younger drivers need more supervised experience.

**Explanation:** Maryland requires significantly more practice hours for younger drivers because they have less overall driving experience. The 60-hour requirement for under-25 drivers ensures more supervised practice before independent driving.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't forget that both age groups must complete nighttime driving hours as part of their total.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the difference is only about total hours, not also about nighttime hours.

**Reference:** See chapter

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think refusal is consequence-free — it carries its own penalty separate from any DUI charge.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume there is a waiting period after which these charges disappear. They are permanent.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing expungement eligibility for other offenses with DUI/DWI charges.

### Question 19
**Correct Answer:** A) Maryland recognizes that impairment begins before the .08 BAC threshold. DWI (.07-.079) captures drivers who are impaired but below the DUI level, ensuring accountability at lower BAC levels.

**Explanation:** Having two levels allows Maryland to address a wider range of impaired driving. Drivers at .07 BAC are still impaired and dangerous, even if they haven't reached the .08 DUI threshold. This tiered approach promotes safer roads.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume .08 is the only number that matters — .07 is also a legal threshold.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that only DUI charges exist or that DWI is just a lesser version of DUI.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 20
**Correct Answer:** A) Signing a false certification is perjury — the parent may be prosecuted and the license may be canceled.

**Explanation:** The practice certification is signed under penalty of perjury. Falsely certifying that a driver has completed the required hours is illegal and can result in prosecution and license cancellation.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume the certification is just a formality — it carries legal weight.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that a small shortfall in hours is not a big deal. It is a legal violation.

**Reference:** See chapter
