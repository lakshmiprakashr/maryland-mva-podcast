# Chapter 02 — Rookie Manual — Answer Key

## Answers with Explanations

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** A) Licensed by the Maryland Motor Vehicle Administration (MVA).

**Explanation:** Only MVA-licensed schools are authorized to certify that a student has completed the driver education program. Unlicensed schools cannot provide official certification.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume any driving school is acceptable. Only MVA-licensed schools can certify completion.

**Common Mistake:** Enrolling in an unlicensed driving school and expecting it to count toward driver education requirements.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** C) 30 hours of classroom instruction and 6 hours of behind-the-wheel training.

**Explanation:** The Maryland Certified Driver Education Program requires a minimum of 30 classroom hours and 6 hours of actual driving instruction with a certified instructor.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't swap these numbers (6 classroom, 30 behind-the-wheel). The correct split is 30 classroom, 6 driving.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing the two numbers or thinking they are equal.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** B) The Orientation session for Driver Education, which coaches and new drivers attend together.

**Explanation:** Unit One is the first component of driver education, designed to orient both the coach and new driver to the program and expectations.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse Unit One with the first driving lesson. It's an orientation session, not a driving session.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking Unit One is a driving lesson rather than an orientation meeting.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 10
**Correct Answer:** C) Give feedback that focuses on improvement and how to learn from mistakes. Remain calm at all times. Yelling does not help.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume any feedback is good feedback. It must be constructive and calm.

### Question 11

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think your driving habits don't matter. The new driver is watching everything.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the new driver won't notice traffic violations or distracted driving.

**Reference:** See chapter
