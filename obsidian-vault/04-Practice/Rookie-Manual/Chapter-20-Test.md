**Q17. [SYNTHESIS]** Explain the difference between DUI and DWI in Maryland, including the BAC thresholds, who they apply to, and the consequences of each.

- A) DWI (Driving While Impaired) applies to drivers 21 and over with a BAC of .07 to .079. DUI (Driving Under the Influence) applies to drivers 21 and over with a BAC of .08 or above. Both are extremely serious charges that may result in loss of driving privilege for an extended period. For drivers under 21, zero tolerance applies — any alcohol while driving results in a citation regardless of BAC. Both DUI and DWI, along with Refusal to Test, cannot be expunged from your driving record.
- B) Only required for commercial vehicles
- C) Not specified in the manual
- D) Varies by county

**Q19. [SYNTHESIS]** Explain the relationship between Implied Consent, the right to refuse testing, and the consequences of refusal. Why does Maryland structure the law this way?

- A) Implied Consent means by accepting any Maryland license, you agree to chemical testing when requested. You technically can refuse, but refusal carries its own penalty — potential license loss. This is separate from any DUI or DWI charge. Maryland structures it this way because impaired driving is a leading cause of fatal crashes. By creating consequences for refusal, the law ensures that drivers cannot avoid accountability simply by declining the test.
- B) Varies by county
- C) Impaired driving is one of the six situations requiring mandatory law enforcement contact at a crash scene. If you witness a crash and the other driver appears impaired, you must call 911. Additionally, impaired driving is a leading cause of crashes. The manual connects the cause (impairment) with the effect (crashes) and the legal response (reporting requirements). Understanding both topics together gives you a complete picture of your legal responsibilities.
- D) Only required for commercial vehicles
