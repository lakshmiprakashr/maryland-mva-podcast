# Chapter 08 — Rookie Manual — Answer Key

## Answers with Explanations

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** A) Center position (1) is most commonly used. Left position (2) is for hills, curves, and driving next to parked cars. Right position (3) is for preparing to make a right turn.

**Explanation:** Center is the default. You shift to left or right only when specific conditions warrant it. All positions must keep the vehicle entirely within the lane lines.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Left position is for passing" — the manual says left is for hills, curves, and parked cars, not for passing.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking lane positions are only relevant on highways; they apply to all roads.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 8, Position Within Lane section

---

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** A) Check to the rear, sides, and front of the vehicle.

**Explanation:** A full 360-degree check is essential before pulling into traffic. You need to know what's approaching from all directions.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Signal first" — checking comes before signaling. You need to know the situation before indicating your intent.

**Common Mistake:** Only looking forward or only looking in the rearview mirror.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 8, Entering Traffic section

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** B) Come to a full and complete stop.

**Explanation:** Maryland law requires a full stop at all stop signs. Even if the road is empty, you must stop completely. A rolling stop is a violation.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Slow down and look" — you must actually stop, not just slow down.

**Common Mistake:** Performing a rolling stop instead of coming to a complete halt.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 8, Intersections section

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** B) When there is no roadway divider between your vehicle and the bus AND the bus has flashing red lights on and the stop arm is extended.

**Explanation:** Both conditions must be met: no physical divider AND red lights with stop arm. If a physical divider exists, you do not need to stop on that side.

**MVA Test Trap:** "A painted median counts as a divider" — only a physical roadway divider exempts you. Painted lines do not count.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing a painted median with a physical divider.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 8, School Bus section

---

### Question 5
**Correct Answer:** C) Tap the brake to alert drivers behind you that you may be slowing down.

**Explanation:** Tapping the brake activates your brake lights, warning following drivers that you're adjusting speed. This prevents rear-end collisions.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Only brake when you need to stop" — the manual says to tap the brake when approaching intersections even if not stopping.

**Common Mistake:** Not tapping the brake because you plan to go through the intersection.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 8, Intersections section

### Question 6
**Correct Answer:** A) No. They must first make sure the intersection is clear before proceeding.

**Explanation:** Other vehicles may still be in the intersection from the previous signal phase. Pedestrians may be crossing. A green light means you have permission to go, not that it's automatically safe.

### Question 7
**Correct Answer:** A) They need to turn off the signal and accelerate to the appropriate speed after entering the lane.

**Explanation:** The complete sequence includes six steps: check, signal, select gap, enter lane, cancel signal, and accelerate. Forgetting to cancel the signal or accelerate creates confusion for other drivers.

**MVA Test Trap:** "The job is done after entering the lane" — you must also cancel the signal and match traffic speed.

**Common Mistake:** Leaving the turn signal on after completing the maneuver.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 8, Entering Traffic section

### Question 8
**Correct Answer:** A) No. You must stop only when the red lights are flashing AND the stop arm is extended. Yellow lights mean the bus is preparing to stop, so slow down and be ready.

**Explanation:** Yellow lights are a warning that the bus will soon stop. Red lights plus the stop arm are the legal trigger for stopping.

**Common Mistake:** Stopping for yellow lights, which can cause rear-end collisions.

### Question 10
**Correct Answer:** A) No. The concrete median is a physical roadway divider, so you do not need to stop for a bus on the opposite side.

**Explanation:** Maryland law exempts drivers from stopping when a physical roadway divider separates them from the bus. A concrete median qualifies as a divider.

**MVA Test Trap:** "All divided highways are exempt" — only physical dividers count, not painted medians or turn lanes.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming any painted center line acts as a divider.

### Question 11
**Correct Answer:** B) Maryland law requires a full and complete stop at all stop signs. A rolling stop is a violation and a dangerous habit.

**Explanation:** Even in a quiet neighborhood, rolling through a stop sign is illegal. The habit of rolling stops carries over to busier intersections where it's extremely dangerous.

**MVA Test Trap:** "In a quiet neighborhood, rolling is fine" — the law applies everywhere, regardless of traffic.

**Common Mistake:** Treating stop signs as yield signs in low-traffic areas.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 8, Intersections section

### Question 12
**Correct Answer:** A) A vehicle approaching from behind may be too close or moving too fast for you to safely enter. Without checking the rear, you might pull out in front of them.

**Explanation:** The manual specifies checking rear, sides, AND front. All three directions must be clear before entering traffic.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Checking forward and to the sides is enough" — the rear check is critical for vehicles approaching from behind.

**Common Mistake:** Focusing only on cross-traffic and forgetting about traffic from behind.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 8, Entering Traffic section

### Question 13

**MVA Test Trap:** "They're completely different" — they share the core check-signal-proceed pattern.

### Question 14
**Correct Answer:** C) It activates your brake lights to alert drivers behind you that you may be slowing down, preventing rear-end collisions.

**Explanation:** Following drivers need advance warning that you're adjusting speed. Brake lights are the most effective signal. Without tapping, a following driver may not realize you're slowing.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Brake tapping is only for stopping" — it's a communication tool, not just a stopping action.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the car behind you is paying attention without providing an explicit brake light signal.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 8, Intersections section

### Question 15
**Correct Answer:** D) (1) Check rear, sides, and front, (2) use turn signal, (3) select a safe gap, (4) drive into the appropriate lane, (5) turn off signal, (6) accelerate to appropriate speed.

**Explanation:** This is a complete sequence. Skipping any step — like forgetting to cancel the signal or not accelerating — creates hazards.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Four steps" — there are six, including canceling the signal and accelerating to speed.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to cancel the signal after entering traffic.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 8, Entering Traffic section

### Question 16
**Correct Answer:** B) The driver did not check ALL directions before proceeding. They only looked left and missed the pedestrian approaching from the right.

**Explanation:** The manual says to make sure the intersection is clear before proceeding. "Clear" means checking all directions, not just one.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Looking one way is enough" — you must check left, right, and ahead before proceeding on green.

**Common Mistake:** Checking only the direction of expected cross-traffic.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 8, Intersections section

### Question 17
**Correct Answer:** D) The four items are: (1) knows all vehicle controls, (2) can do a pre-entry check, (3) can brake smoothly and stop completely, (4) can back up correctly. If any answer is No, stop and practice more.

**Explanation:** The checklist is a safety gate. All four foundational skills must be confirmed before adding complexity. "If you don't answer yes to all of the questions, STOP and practice more."

**MVA Test Trap:** "Three out of four is good enough" — the manual is absolute: ALL four must be "Yes."

**Common Mistake:** Rushing to progress without fully mastering all foundational skills.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 8, Coach's Checklist section

### Question 18
**Correct Answer:** A) Both require you to assess the full traffic situation before making a decision. Just as you check all directions at an intersection, you must evaluate whether a divider exists before deciding whether to stop for a bus.

**Explanation:** Safe driving is fundamentally about gathering complete information before acting. The school bus rule adds a legal dimension, but the principle is the same: know the full situation.

**MVA Test Trap:** "School bus rules are totally different" — they share the principle of complete situational awareness.

**Common Mistake:** Memorizing the school bus rule in isolation without connecting it to general traffic awareness principles.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 8, School Bus and Intersections sections

### Question 19
**Correct Answer:** A) Yes, if the car was too close for a safe gap. The manual says to "select a safe gap in traffic" — if no safe gap exists, you wait.

**Explanation:** Entering traffic requires patience. Forcing a merge when traffic is too close is a leading cause of side-impact collisions.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Just go quickly" — the manual says to select a safe gap. If no gap exists, wait.

**Common Mistake:** Feeling pressured to enter traffic and forcing an unsafe merge.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 8, Entering Traffic section

---

### Question 20
**Correct Answer:** A) Turn off your turn signal and accelerate to the appropriate speed.

**Explanation:** Canceling the signal prevents confusion for other drivers, and accelerating to traffic speed prevents you from becoming a slow-moving hazard.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Just drive normally" — specifically, cancel the signal AND accelerate to appropriate speed.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to cancel the signal and driving at a slow speed that disrupts traffic flow.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 8, Entering Traffic section
