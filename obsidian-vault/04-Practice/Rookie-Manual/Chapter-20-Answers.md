### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** A) .08 or above.

**Explanation:** A BAC of .08 or higher is legally considered DUI for drivers age 21 and over in Maryland. This is the standard threshold used nationwide.

**MVA Test Trap:** Confusing the DUI threshold with the DWI threshold — DWI is .07 to .079, DUI is .08 and above.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking DWI and DUI are interchangeable terms with the same BAC threshold.

### Question 17
**Correct Answer:** B) DWI (Driving While Impaired) applies to drivers 21 and over with a BAC of .07 to .079. DUI (Driving Under the Influence) applies to drivers 21 and over with a BAC of .08 or above. Both are extremely serious charges that may result in loss of driving privilege for an extended period. For drivers under 21, zero tolerance applies — any alcohol while driving results in a citation regardless of BAC. Both DUI and DWI, along with Refusal to Test, cannot be expunged from your driving record.

**Explanation:** Maryland's tiered system means there are actually three levels of impaired driving consequences based on age and BAC: zero tolerance (under 21), DWI (.07-.079), and DUI (.08+).

**MVA Test Trap:** Treating DUI and DWI as the same charge — they have different BAC thresholds and are distinct legal charges.

**Common Mistake:** Not knowing about the DWI threshold at .07.

### Question 19
**Correct Answer:** D) Implied Consent means by accepting any Maryland license, you agree to chemical testing when requested. You technically can refuse, but refusal carries its own penalty — potential license loss. This is separate from any DUI or DWI charge. Maryland structures it this way because impaired driving is a leading cause of fatal crashes. By creating consequences for refusal, the law ensures that drivers cannot avoid accountability simply by declining the test.

**Explanation:** The law balances your theoretical right to refuse with practical consequences that discourage refusal. This maximizes the likelihood of detecting impaired drivers.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking refusal is a loophole to avoid DUI charges — it is not and adds penalties.

**Common Mistake:** Not understanding that refusal is a separate legal consequence, not a way to avoid the DUI charge.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 20
**Correct Answer:** B) Impaired driving is one of the six situations requiring mandatory law enforcement contact at a crash scene. If you witness a crash and the other driver appears impaired, you must call 911. Additionally, impaired driving is a leading cause of crashes. The manual connects the cause (impairment) with the effect (crashes) and the legal response (reporting requirements). Understanding both topics together gives you a complete picture of your legal responsibilities.

**Explanation:** The manual groups these topics because they overlap — impaired driving causes crashes, and crash reporting procedures must account for impaired drivers at the scene.

**MVA Test Trap:** Seeing these as unrelated topics — they are directly connected through crash causation and reporting requirements.
