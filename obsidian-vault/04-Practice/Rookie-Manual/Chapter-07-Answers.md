# Chapter 07 — Rookie Manual — Answer Key

## Answers with Explanations

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** B) Foot on the brake, start the engine, shift into Drive, then slowly release the brake.

**Explanation:** This sequence ensures the vehicle is under control at every step. The brake is depressed to prevent rolling, and releasing the brake slowly allows gentle movement.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Shift to Drive and press the gas" — the manual says to release the brake slowly, not press the gas.

**Common Mistake:** Pressing the gas immediately after shifting instead of letting idle speed move the car.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Moving the Vehicle section

---

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** D) Center position (1) is the most commonly used. Left position (2) is used on hills, curves, or next to parked cars. Right position (3) is used to prepare for right turns.

**Explanation:** Center is the default. You shift left or right only when conditions warrant it — never stay in an extreme position longer than necessary.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Left position is for passing" — the manual says left is for hills, curves, and parked cars, not passing.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking lane positions are only about highways. They apply to all roads.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Position Within Lane section

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** B) Before turning, signal your intention. Maryland law requires signaling before every turn and lane change.

**Explanation:** Signaling gives other drivers time to react to your maneuver. Without signaling, other drivers cannot anticipate your turn and may not yield or slow down.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Only signal on busy roads" — signaling is required in ALL situations, including parking lots and residential streets.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to signal during low-speed practice or in empty parking lots.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Turns section

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** D) Press the brake and shift into reverse.

**Explanation:** The brake must be engaged first to maintain control. Only after shifting to reverse do you begin the visual search and body repositioning.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Look behind first" — the brake and gear shift come first, then the visual search.

**Common Mistake:** Trying to look behind before securing the vehicle in reverse.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Backing section

---

### Question 5
**Correct Answer:** B) Do not use it. Allow the vehicle to move at a slow walking pace using only idle speed.

**Explanation:** Gas pedal use while backing removes control. Idle speed provides slow, manageable movement that gives you time to react to hazards.

**MVA Test Trap:** "A little gas is needed to move backward" — idle creep is sufficient and safer.

**Common Mistake:** Pressing the gas because the car feels like it's not moving fast enough.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Backing section

### Question 6
**Correct Answer:** C) Left hand at 9 o'clock and right hand at 3 o'clock. This position maintains good steering control and minimizes airbag injury risk.

**Explanation:** The 9 and 3 position gives you maximum leverage for turning and keeps your arms away from the airbag deployment zone.

**MVA Test Trap:** "10 and 2" is the old position. The manual now recommends 9 and 3.

**Common Mistake:** Using 10 and 2 out of habit from older driving instruction.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Steering Positions section

---

### Question 7
**Correct Answer:** C) Simply release the brake slowly. The vehicle will begin to move on its own at idle speed without any gas.

**Explanation:** Pressing the gas in a parking lot or driveway is unnecessary and dangerous for a new driver. Idle speed provides enough movement to begin practicing.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Gas is needed to get the car moving" — automatic transmissions move at idle. Gas is for acceleration after you're already moving.

**Common Mistake:** Pressing the gas because it feels like the car won't move without it.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Moving the Vehicle section

---

### Question 8
**Correct Answer:** A) The steering wheel must be turned smoothly. Jerky steering can cause loss of control and is a bad habit that becomes dangerous at higher speeds.

**Explanation:** The manual specifically states to "turn the steering wheel smoothly." Smooth inputs keep the vehicle stable. Jerky inputs unsettle the car.

**MVA Test Trap:** "In a parking lot it doesn't matter" — smooth steering is a habit to build from day one.

**Common Mistake:** Nervous drivers tend to grip the wheel too tightly and make jerky movements.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Turns section

---

### Question 9
**Correct Answer:** B) Enter the nearest lane for the desired direction. For a left turn, that means the left-most lane available in the direction you're turning.

**Explanation:** Entering the correct lane prevents conflicts with other traffic. The manual specifies entering the nearest lane for your desired direction.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Any lane is fine" — you must enter the nearest lane for your direction of travel.

**Common Mistake:** Drifting into the far lane after a turn instead of staying in the nearest lane.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Turns section

---

### Question 10
**Correct Answer:** C) Mirrors create blind spots directly behind and beside the vehicle. You must turn your body to the right and look through the rear window.

**Explanation:** The manual explicitly warns against using only or mostly mirrors during backing. Turning your body provides a direct view of the area behind the car.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Mirrors show everything you need" — they don't. Blind spots exist between mirror coverage areas.

**Common Mistake:** Relying on mirrors because it feels more comfortable than turning around.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Common Errors with Backing section

---

### Question 11
**Correct Answer:** C) (1) Know where all vehicle controls are, (2) complete a pre-entry check, (3) brake smoothly and come to a complete stop, (4) back up using correct procedure.

**Explanation:** The coach's checklist ensures foundational skills are mastered before adding complexity. If any answer is "No," the coach must stop and practice more.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Three out of four is fine" — ALL four must be confirmed. The manual says if you don't answer yes to all, STOP and practice more.

**Common Mistake:** Rushing to the next lesson without fully mastering the current one.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Coach's Checklist section

---

### Question 12
**Correct Answer:** C) Shift to the left lane position. The manual says left position is best used when driving next to parked cars.

**Explanation:** Shifting left gives you more space between your vehicle and parked cars, protecting you from doors opening or people stepping out.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Stay center" — the manual specifically recommends left position next to parked cars.

**Common Mistake:** Staying center because it "feels normal" even when conditions call for a different position.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Position Within Lane section

---

### Question 13
**Correct Answer:** A) Both require checking the surroundings first and using proper steering technique. Turning requires signaling and entering the correct lane. Backing requires shifting to reverse, turning the body, hand at 12 o'clock, and using idle speed only.

**Explanation:** Both maneuvers demand awareness and preparation, but the specific steps differ. Turning is forward-moving with signals; backing is rearward-moving with body repositioning.

**MVA Test Trap:** "They're basically the same" — they share principles (check, steer) but have very different procedures.

**Common Mistake:** Treating backing as just "driving in reverse" without the specific body and hand position changes.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Turns and Backing sections

---

### Question 14
**Correct Answer:** C) Different driving conditions require different positions within the lane. Center is default, but left provides safety from parked cars and better visibility on curves, and right prepares for right turns.

**Explanation:** Lane positioning is a dynamic skill. Rigidly staying center ignores the need to adapt to changing road conditions and upcoming maneuvers.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Center is always correct" — center is default, but left and right positions serve important specific purposes.

**Common Mistake:** Never adjusting lane position, which reduces safety margins in certain situations.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Position Within Lane section

---

### Question 15
**Correct Answer:** C) (1) Improper body position, (2) moving too fast, (3) failing to check behind, (4) not looking behind during the whole maneuver, (5) using only mirrors, (6) not steering in the intended direction.

**Explanation:** These errors are the most frequent causes of backing accidents. Each one represents a failure in awareness or control.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Only three errors" — the manual lists six. Students often miss "not steering in the direction you want to go."

**Common Mistake:** Memorizing only the most obvious errors and forgetting about steering direction.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Common Errors with Backing section

---

### Question 16
**Correct Answer:** B) Progress to the next set of skills in a more challenging environment, such as a lightly traveled residential road.

**Explanation:** The checklist is a gate — all four items must be confirmed before moving forward. This prevents new drivers from advancing before they're ready.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Move to the highway" — the manual recommends progressing gradually, starting with residential roads.

**Common Mistake:** Skipping steps and going straight to busy roads after parking lot practice.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Coach's Checklist and Practice Lessons sections

### Question 17
**Correct Answer:** D) To develop a feel for both pedals and build muscle memory, reducing the risk of pressing the wrong pedal while driving.

**Explanation:** Pedal confusion (pressing gas instead of brake) is a leading cause of crashes for new drivers. Familiarization while stationary prevents this.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Just one press of each is enough" — the manual says "several times" to build proper feel.

**Common Mistake:** Skipping the practice because the pedals "look different" — feel is what matters, not appearance.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Moving the Vehicle section

### Question 18
**Correct Answer:** B) Normal driving uses 9 and 3 for control and airbag safety. Backing uses 12 o'clock because you need one hand to support your turned body while looking through the rear window.

**Explanation:** Backing requires a completely different body orientation than forward driving. The 12 o'clock position allows one-handed steering while your body is turned to the right.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Use 9 and 3 for everything" — backing is a special case that requires 12 o'clock.

**Common Mistake:** Trying to use the normal hand position while backing, which makes it impossible to turn your body properly.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 7, Steering and Backing sections

---

### Question 19

**Explanation:** Lane positions are situational, not permanent. Center is the default; you only shift for a reason.

### Question 20
**Correct Answer:** B) Right position (3) — positioned to the right side of the lane, still entirely within the lines.

**Explanation:** The right lane position prepares you for a right turn. Being in the correct position before the turn ensures you enter the proper lane after the turn.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Any lane position is fine" — the manual specifically assigns right position for right turn preparation.

**Common Mistake:** Staying center when preparing for a right turn, which forces an awkward angle.
