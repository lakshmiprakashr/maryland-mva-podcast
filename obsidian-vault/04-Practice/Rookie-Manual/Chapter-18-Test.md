**Q17. [SYNTHESIS]** Compare the procedures for entering and exiting a parallel parking space. How do the steps mirror each other?

- A) Varies by county
- B) Entering involves: signal, align parallel, 2-3 feet gap, brake, reverse, check traffic, back with sharp turn, turn opposite, adjust. Exiting involves: check traffic, signal, brake, reverse toward car behind, drive forward with turn toward travel direction, check front bumper, steer into traffic. The steps essentially reverse — entering goes back into the space, exiting pulls forward out of it, and both require checking all directions.
- C) Both require signaling intentions, moving slowly, checking for traffic, and centering the vehicle. Angle parking is simpler — you pull forward into the space and back out. Parallel parking is more complex — you must align parallel to another vehicle, back in with specific steering inputs, and end up within 12 inches of the curb. Exiting angle parking requires backing up; exiting parallel parking requires a combination of reversing and moving forward.
- D) Only required for commercial vehicles

**Q18. [SYNTHESIS]** Explain the chain of consequences that begins with a single moving violation for a provisional license holder in Maryland.

- A) Only required for commercial vehicles
- B) The statistics provide context for why the rules exist. More than 60 people die and 7,800 are injured annually in Maryland crashes involving young drivers. The citation consequences, Driver Improvement Program, and GDL requirements are all designed to reduce these numbers. The manual connects individual responsibility (obeying traffic laws) to the broader public safety goal.
- C) Varies by county
- D) A moving violation leads to a conviction (or PBJ), which triggers three things: (1) the eighteen-month provisional waiting period restarts from zero, (2) mandatory attendance at the Driver Improvement Program, and (3) if you fail to attend the program, your license is suspended. This chain means one ticket can set back your driving privilege progression significantly.
