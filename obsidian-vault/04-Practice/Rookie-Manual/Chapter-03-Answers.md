# Chapter 03 — Rookie Manual — Answer Key

## Answers with Explanations

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** C) 30 hours of classroom instruction and 6 hours of behind-the-wheel training.

**Explanation:** This is a mandatory requirement for all new drivers, regardless of age. Both components must be completed at an MVA-licensed driving school.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't swap these numbers. The correct split is 30 classroom, 6 driving.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing the two numbers or thinking they are equal.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** B) 9 months.

**Explanation:** Drivers 18 or under without a high school diploma must hold their permit for 9 months before being eligible for a provisional license.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse with 3 months (for 18-year-olds with a diploma). The diploma makes a significant difference.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming all 18-year-olds have the same holding period.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 3

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse with 9 months (for 18-year-olds without a diploma). The diploma is the key difference.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting that the diploma affects the holding period.

### Question 6

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse with 60 hours (for under 25). The age-based difference is significant.

### Question 7
**Correct Answer:** B) 60 hours of practice with a supervising driver, including 10 hours at night.

**Explanation:** Drivers under 25 need 60 total practice hours, with 10 of those hours occurring during nighttime (defined as ½ hour before sunset to ½ hour after sunrise).

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse with 14 hours (for 25+). The MVA loves to swap these numbers.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting the nighttime hour requirement.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 8
**Correct Answer:** B) Your holding period extends to 9 months, regardless of your age.

**Explanation:** A moving violation or PBJ (probation before judgment) triggers the maximum 9-month holding period for all age groups.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think that being over 25 exempts you from the 9-month extension. It applies to everyone.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the 45-day period for 25+ is immune to extensions.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 9
**Correct Answer:** A) 2 years (24 months) from the date it is issued.

**Explanation:** The permit has a strict expiration date. If it expires before you get your provisional license, you must retake the law test.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse the 2-year validity with the holding periods. The permit can be valid longer than the holding period.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting the expiration date.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 15
**Correct Answer:** B) A 17-year-old needs 9 months holding, 60 practice hours (10 night), and driver education. A 26-year-old needs 45 days holding, 14 practice hours (3 night), and driver education. Both require the same driver education (30 classroom + 6 behind-the-wheel).

**Explanation:** Maryland reduces requirements for older drivers, recognizing they may have more maturity. However, driver education is required for all ages.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume the requirements are the same for all ages. The age-based differences are significant.

**Common Mistake:** Focusing only on practice hours and forgetting the different holding periods.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 16
**Correct Answer:** D) The varying holding periods reflect Maryland's assessment of maturity and responsibility. Younger drivers and those without a high school diploma need more time to develop safe driving habits.

**Explanation:** The graduated system is designed to provide more supervision for less experienced drivers while allowing more mature drivers to progress faster.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think the differences are arbitrary. They are based on maturity assessments.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the system is unfair without understanding the reasoning.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 17
**Correct Answer:** B) ½ hour before sunset until ½ hour after sunrise.

**Explanation:** Nighttime includes the twilight periods around sunset and sunrise, not just dark hours. This captures the most challenging visibility conditions.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse with "after dark" or "sunset to sunrise." The definition includes the half-hour transitions.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking nighttime starts at sunset.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 18
**Correct Answer:** B) At least 21 years old and must have held a driver's license for at least 3 years.

**Explanation:** Both requirements must be met: age 21+ AND 3+ years of licensed driving experience.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think being licensed is enough. Both age AND experience requirements must be met.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming any licensed adult can supervise.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 19
**Correct Answer:** C) Completed skills practice log, completed certification page, and valid Maryland learner's instructional permit.

**Explanation:** All three documents are required. The log documents your practice hours, the certification page certifies completion, and the permit proves you are authorized to practice.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think you only need the permit. The log and certification page are mandatory.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to bring the signed practice log.

**Reference:** See chapter

---

### Question 20
**Correct Answer:** D) PBJ stands for Probation Before Judgment. If convicted or granted PBJ for a moving violation, your holding period extends to 9 months.

**Explanation:** PBJ is a legal disposition that can affect your driving privileges. It triggers the maximum holding period regardless of your age.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse PBJ with a regular conviction. Both trigger the same 9-month extension.

**Common Mistake:** Not understanding what PBJ means and its consequences.

**Reference:** See chapter
