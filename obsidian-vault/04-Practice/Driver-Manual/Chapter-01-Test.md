**Q18. [SYNTHESIS]** Explain the relationship between the knowledge test and the driving skills test. Can you take them in any order, and what does each test specifically evaluate?

- A) The knowledge test evaluates your understanding of motor vehicle laws, safe driving practices, and traffic signs — it is a written exam at the MVA. The driving skills test evaluates your ability to apply that knowledge on real roads and a test course. You must pass the knowledge test (and vision screening) first before taking the driving skills test.
- B) Only required for commercial vehicles
- C) The vision screening sets the baseline: you must meet 20/40 acuity and 140-degree field of vision. If you need glasses or contacts, a restriction is added to your license. Separately, state law requires you to report any medical condition that could affect driving ability. These systems work together to ensure only safe drivers are licensed.
- D) At the MVA, you need age proof, SSN, lawful status (if applicable), and two Maryland residency documents. If under 18, a parent must co-sign. If under 16, you also need the DL-300 form. While holding the Permit, if you are under 25, you must complete the Driver Education Program. These requirements layer on top of each other based on your age and situation.
