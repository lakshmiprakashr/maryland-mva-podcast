# Chapter 02 — Driver's Manual — Answer Key

## Answers with Explanations

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** D) The Rookie Driver/Graduated Driver Licensing System, abbreviated as GDL (or GLS in the manual).

**Explanation:** Maryland's GDL system is a staged process that all first-time drivers must complete regardless of age. It progresses from Learner's Instructional Permit to Provisional License to full Driver's License, building experience at each stage.

**MVA Test Trap:** Do not confuse "GDL" with "GLS." Both are used in the manual. The test may use either form. Know that they refer to the same system.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking the GDL only applies to teenagers. It applies to people of all ages who have never held a driver's license.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** B) A Type 1 Learner's Instructional Permit, valid for up to two years.

**Explanation:** The Type 1 permit is specifically for first-time applicants. To get it, you must pass a vision screening and a knowledge test. Its two-year validity gives new drivers ample time to complete the required practice hours and driver education.

**MVA Test Trap:** Do not confuse with the Type 2 permit. Type 2 is for existing license holders wanting a different class, is valid for only 180 days, and requires the applicant to be at least 16 years and 6 months old.

**Common Mistake:** Mixing up the two-year validity of Type 1 with the 180-day validity of Type 2.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** C) Sixty total practice hours, with at least ten hours during nighttime (beginning 30 minutes before sunset through 30 minutes after sunrise).

**Common Mistake:** Thinking night hours are a separate requirement on top of the 60 hours. They are included within the 60.

### Question 6

### Question 10
**Correct Answer:** D) He must complete a knowledge test and a driving skills test. Since he already has a U.S. license, he does not need the three-hour roadway safety education program.

**Explanation:** Holders of existing U.S. licenses do not need to go through the full GDL system. They apply for a Type 2 Learner's Instructional Permit and can test for a full license after holding it for at least 14 days. **MVA Test Trap:** Do not confuse James with an out-of-country license holder. He has a Virginia license, so the out-of-country requirements (including the 3-hour program) do not apply.

**Common Mistake:** Applying GDL requirements to existing out-of-state license holders. The GDL only applies to those who have never held a license.

### Question 11

### Question 17
**Correct Answer:** B) Type 1 is for first-time license applicants with no prior license. It is valid for up to two years and requires passing a vision screening and knowledge test. Type 2 is for existing license holders who want a different class of license. It requires the applicant to be at least 16 years and 6 months old, is valid for up to 180 days, and allows the holder to take a driving skills test for a full license after 14 days. Type 1 leads to a Provisional License; Type 2 leads directly to a full license.

**Explanation:** The two permit types serve fundamentally different populations. Type 1 is the starting point for the GDL system. Type 2 is a shortcut for experienced drivers who already know how to drive but need a different license class. This is a high-frequency test topic.

**MVA Test Trap:** Do not mix up eligibility ages or validity periods between the two types. The MVA may present answers that swap details between Type 1 and Type 2.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting that Type 2 holders can go directly to a full license, bypassing the Provisional stage entirely.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 18
**Correct Answer:** C) The base minimum hold time is 45 days for all applicants. However, three factors modify this: (1) If you are under 18, you must hold the permit for at least 9 months without violations. (2) If you are 18 with a diploma/GED or ages 19-24, you must hold it for at least 3 months without violations. (3) At any age, if you are convicted of or receive a PBJ for a moving violation, you must hold the permit for at least 9 months from the most recent violation. The longest applicable hold time controls.

**Explanation:** Maryland uses a layered system where age and violations both affect hold time. The 45-day minimum is the floor. Being under 18 raises it to 9 months. A moving violation at any age raises it to 9 months from the violation date. These rules can interact, and the most restrictive one applies.

**MVA Test Trap:** Do not assume only one rule applies. The MVA may present scenarios where multiple rules overlap, and you need to identify the longest hold time as the controlling factor.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting that the violation hold (9 months from conviction/PBJ) applies at any age, even if the general clean-record hold would be shorter.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 19
**Correct Answer:** B) Step 1: Apply for a Type 1 Learner's Instructional Permit at age 15 years 9 months or older. Must bring required documents, have a parent/guardian co-sign, provide a completed DL-300 form (under 16), and pass vision screening and knowledge test. Step 2: While holding the permit, complete the Maryland Driver Education Program, practice driving at least 60 hours (10 at night) with a supervising driver, and keep a Practice and Skills Log. Step 3: Hold the permit for at least 9 months without violations. Step 4: Pass the driving skills test to receive a Provisional License. Step 5: Drive under Provisional restrictions for at least 18 months with no moving violations. Step 6: The MVA automatically converts the Provisional License to a full Driver's License at age 18 or older.

**Explanation:** This comprehensive scenario touches every stage of the GDL and tests knowledge of all requirements in sequence. Understanding the full timeline helps students grasp how the stages build on each other.

**MVA Test Trap:** Do not forget the DL-300 form requirement for under-16 applicants, or the 9-month hold that applies specifically to under-18 applicants with clean records.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking the driver education program and practice hours happen at different stages. They both occur during the Permit stage, before the driving skills test.

**Reference:** See chapter

### Question 20
**Correct Answer:** A) The co-signer must submit a written request to the MVA's Administrative Adjudication Division. The minor's license is immediately cancelled and remains cancelled until the minor reaches age 18, unless a new co-signer co-signs a new application. The new co-signer must meet the same qualifications as the original co-signer. Once the minor turns 18, they can apply independently without needing a co-signer.

**Explanation:** This tests understanding of the co-signer's power and the minor's options. The cancellation is permanent until either the minor turns 18 or a new qualified co-signer is found. This is an important protection mechanism Maryland provides to parents and guardians.

**MVA Test Trap:** Do not think the minor can simply re-apply immediately. The license stays cancelled until 18 or until a new co-signer signs. There is no way around this.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting that the new co-signer must meet the same qualification requirements as the original. Not just anyone can co-sign.

**Reference:** See chapter
