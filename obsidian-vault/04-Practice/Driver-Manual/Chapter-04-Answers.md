# Chapter 04 — Driver's Manual — Answer Key

## Answers with Explanations

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** A) Come to a complete stop at the stop line. Yield to all other traffic and pedestrians. Proceed when the way is clear.

**Explanation:** A flashing red signal functions exactly like a stop sign. You must come to a complete stop, yield, and proceed only when it is safe.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse flashing red with steady red. Both require a stop, but flashing red treats the intersection like a stop sign, meaning you can proceed after yielding without waiting for a signal change.

**Common Mistake:** Slowing down instead of coming to a complete stop. Flashing red always means full stop.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.A

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** C) Orange.

**Explanation:** Orange is the standard color for construction and maintenance work zone warning signs. When you see orange signs, be prepared for unexpected conditions.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse orange with yellow. Yellow is for general warnings. Orange is specifically for construction zones.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking all warning signs are yellow. Construction warnings specifically use orange.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.B

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** D) An octagon, which is an eight-sided shape. It is the only eight-sided sign on the road, and it always means stop.

**Explanation:** The octagonal shape is unique to stop signs. Even if you cannot read the word 'STOP,' the shape alone tells you what to do.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students sometimes confuse the octagon with the diamond shape. Octagon = 8 sides = stop. Diamond = warning.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking all red signs are stop signs. The shape is what makes it unique, not just the color.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.B

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** A) Treat it as a four-way stop. Stop at the stop line, or if none, before the crosswalk or intersection. Yield to vehicles and pedestrians in the intersection and proceed when safe.

**Explanation:** A non-functioning traffic signal means all drivers must stop and follow four-way stop rules. This prevents confusion and crashes.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume you can drive through just because the light is dark. You must stop and treat it as a four-way stop.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to yield to vehicles and pedestrians already in the intersection.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.A

### Question 5
**Correct Answer:** A) School zones and school crossings.

**Explanation:** The pentagon, or five-sided shape, is specifically used for school zone and school crossing warnings. These signs alert you to areas where children may be crossing.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse the pentagon with the diamond. Both are warning signs, but pentagon is specifically for school zones.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking all five-sided signs are school signs. The pentagon shape is specifically reserved for school zones.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.B

### Question 6
**Correct Answer:** B) Permission to turn in the direction the arrow points is ending. You should slow down and proceed with caution.

**Explanation:** A steady yellow arrow means the protected turn phase is ending. You should slow down and be prepared to stop if you cannot safely complete the turn.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse steady yellow arrow with flashing yellow arrow. Steady means permission is ending. Flashing means caution, you may turn but must yield.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking a yellow arrow means you must stop immediately. You should stop if safe, but if you are already in the intersection, complete the turn carefully.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.A

### Question 7
**Correct Answer:** B) Blue.

**Explanation:** Blue signs indicate services available to travelers. This includes food, gas, hospitals, lodging, airports, and rest areas.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse blue with green. Green is for destinations and directions. Blue is specifically for services.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking green signs indicate services. Green is for general guidance and destinations.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.B

### Question 8
**Correct Answer:** B) Come to a complete stop even if you do not see a train. Wait until the flashing red signal stops and the way is clear.

**Explanation:** At a railroad crossing with a flashing red signal, you must always stop. Trains can come from either direction and may be closer and faster than they appear.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume you can proceed if you don't see a train. Always stop at a flashing red at a railroad crossing.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking you can proceed if the tracks look clear. You must stop and wait until the signal stops flashing.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.A

### Question 9
**Correct Answer:** B) A warning of special conditions or hazards ahead. You may need to slow down and be ready to take appropriate action.

**Explanation:** Diamond-shaped yellow signs are warning signs. They alert you to conditions like curves, lane ends, merging traffic, or other hazards that require attention.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse a diamond warning sign with a regulatory sign. Diamond means warning and potential hazard ahead.

**Common Mistake:** Ignoring diamond signs because they don't say 'stop' or 'yield.' Warning signs require you to be alert and prepared.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.B

### Question 10
**Correct Answer:** A) You may turn left in the direction of the arrow, but you must still yield to pedestrians and any vehicles already in the intersection.

**Explanation:** A green arrow gives you a protected turn, but you must still yield to pedestrians and vehicles already in the intersection. The arrow does not eliminate all yielding requirements.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often think a green arrow means you can go without checking. You must still yield to pedestrians and vehicles already in the intersection.

**Common Mistake:** Turning without checking for pedestrians in the crosswalk. The green arrow protects you from oncoming traffic, not pedestrians.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.A

### Question 11
**Correct Answer:** A) This is a regulatory sign. It tells you the maximum legal speed on this road is 55 miles per hour under ideal conditions.

**Explanation:** Rectangular white signs with black text are regulatory signs. Speed limit signs tell you the maximum legal speed. You must obey these signs.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse regulatory signs with warning signs. White rectangular signs are regulatory and must be obeyed. Yellow diamond signs are warnings.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking the speed limit is a target speed rather than a maximum. You may drive slower but never faster.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.B

### Question 12
**Correct Answer:** B) Move out of the lane as soon as you safely can.

**Explanation:** A steady yellow X means the lane use is about to change. You should safely merge out of that lane as soon as possible.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse steady yellow X with flashing yellow X. Steady yellow X means exit the lane. Flashing yellow X means left turns only.

**Common Mistake:** Stopping in the lane instead of safely merging out. You must exit the lane when you see a steady yellow X.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.A

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse fluorescent pink with fluorescent yellow-green. Pink is for emergency incidents. Yellow-green is for schools, pedestrians, and bicycles.

### Question 14
**Correct Answer:** A) The pedestrian signal tells drivers to stop. You must stop and wait for pedestrians to cross safely.

**Explanation:** A solid red hand on the pedestrian signal means stop. Drivers must stop to allow pedestrians to cross safely before proceeding.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume you can proceed if no pedestrians are visible. The signal tells you to stop regardless.

### Question 16

### Question 17
**Correct Answer:** C) Steady green: proceed with caution, yield to pedestrians and vehicles in intersection. Green arrow: proceed in arrow direction with protected turn, still yield to pedestrians and vehicles in intersection. Flashing yellow: slow down and proceed with caution, do not stop. Only flashing red requires a complete stop.

**Explanation:** Each signal has different rules. Green means go with caution. Green arrow means protected turn. Flashing yellow means caution only. Only flashing red requires a full stop.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often confuse flashing yellow with flashing red. Flashing yellow means slow and proceed. Flashing red means stop.

**Common Mistake:** Stopping at a flashing yellow. Flashing yellow does not require a stop, only caution.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.A

---

### Question 18
**Correct Answer:** C) Regulatory signs tell you laws you must obey, typically white or red with black text, rectangular shape. Warning signs alert you to hazards ahead, typically yellow diamond-shaped. Guide signs provide directions and information, typically green or blue rectangular.

**Explanation:** Each type of sign serves a different purpose and has distinct colors and shapes. Regulatory = must obey. Warning = be alert. Guide = direction and information.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often confuse regulatory signs with warning signs. Remember: rectangular white = regulatory; diamond yellow = warning.

**Common Mistake:** Treating warning signs as if they are regulatory. Warning signs do not require specific actions, just awareness.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.B

---

### Question 19
**Correct Answer:** C) Colors and shapes work together to communicate meaning. Red octagon = stop. Yellow diamond = general warning. Green rectangle = destination and direction. The combination of color and shape allows instant recognition.

**Explanation:** Traffic signs use both color and shape to communicate. The color tells you the type of information (regulatory, warning, guide), and the shape reinforces that meaning.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students may remember colors but forget shapes, or vice versa. Both must be memorized together.

**Common Mistake:** Relying only on text to identify signs. You must recognize signs by color and shape for the exam.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.B

---

### Question 20
**Correct Answer:** A) Pedestrian sequence: Walking person = go; Flashing hand = don't start crossing; Solid hand = stop. Driver sequence: Solid red = stop; Flashing red = stop then proceed with caution; Solid yellow = get ready to stop. Drivers must yield to pedestrians in the crosswalk.

**Explanation:** Crosswalk signals coordinate pedestrian crossing with driver stopping. The pedestrian sees a walking person when it is safe to cross, and a flashing or solid hand when to stop. The driver sees corresponding signals to know when to stop and when to proceed.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often only think about what pedestrians see, not what drivers must do. Drivers must also obey crosswalk signals.

**Common Mistake:** Focusing only on pedestrian signals without considering what drivers are required to do at those signals.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 4.A
