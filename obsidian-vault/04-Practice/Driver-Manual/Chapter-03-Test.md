**Q20. [SYNTHESIS]** How do the rules for following distance, stopping distance, and speed work together to keep you safe? Give an example.

- A) Following distance gives you time to react. Stopping distance determines how much space you need to actually stop. Speed affects both: higher speed means longer stopping distance. Example: at 3-4 seconds following distance on a wet road with worn tires, you need to reduce speed because stopping distance increases and your safe following distance should increase to 4-5 seconds or more.
