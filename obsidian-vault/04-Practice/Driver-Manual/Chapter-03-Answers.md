# Chapter 03 — Driver's Manual — Answer Key

## Answers with Explanations

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** A) 3 to 4 seconds.

**Explanation:** The MVA recommends a minimum following distance of 3 to 4 seconds under ideal conditions. You measure this by counting 'one-one thousand, two-one thousand, three-one thousand' after the car ahead passes a reference point.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse with 2 seconds, which is too close. Some students also confuse this with the 4-5 second rule for buses and delivery vans.

**Common Mistake:** Using a time-based measurement instead of a distance measurement. Always use the seconds method with a reference point.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.4

---

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** C) The driver on the left must yield to the driver on the right.

**Explanation:** At a four-way stop, if both drivers arrive simultaneously, the driver on the left yields to the driver on the right. This rule prevents confusion and keeps traffic flowing safely.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often think the driver who arrived first always goes. The simultaneous arrival rule specifically assigns right-of-way to the driver on the right.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing the 'first to arrive' rule with the 'driver on the right' rule. They apply in different situations.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.1

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** D) After coming to a complete stop, you may turn right on red unless a sign specifically prohibits it. You must yield to pedestrians and all other traffic.

**Explanation:** Maryland law permits right turns on red after a complete stop unless a sign prohibits it. You must yield the right-of-way to pedestrians and all other traffic before making the turn.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume you can always turn right on red. Always check for 'No Turn On Red' signs first.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to yield to pedestrians when turning right on red. You must yield to everyone.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.A

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** B) Reaction time, type and condition of the road, tire tread condition, and brake condition.

**Explanation:** Stopping distance depends on how quickly you see and react to a hazard, the road surface, your tires' grip, and how well your brakes work. All four factors must be considered for safe driving.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often only mention speed as a factor. While speed matters, the manual specifically lists these four factors.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting tire tread condition as a factor in stopping distance.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.5

---

### Question 5
**Correct Answer:** A) Constantly observing your surroundings and searching ahead to identify risks, being ready to change speed or lane as needed.

**Explanation:** Defensive driving means being aware of what is happening around your vehicle at all times, scanning front, sides, and rear, and anticipating problems before they become emergencies.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse defensive driving with slow driving. It's about awareness and anticipation, not speed.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking defensive driving is only about avoiding other bad drivers. It includes constant scanning and risk assessment.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.A

---

### Question 6
**Correct Answer:** D) 100 feet.

**Explanation:** You may not pass when crossing or within 100 feet of an intersection or railroad grade crossing. This ensures you have adequate visibility and space to complete the pass safely.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often confuse the 100-foot rule for intersections with the 500-foot rule for oncoming vehicles with high beams.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing the 100-foot no-passing zone with other distance measurements in the manual.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.9

---

### Question 7
**Correct Answer:** C) Check if U-turns are allowed in that location, then turn on your left turn signal, stop and yield to oncoming traffic, and turn into the outside or right-hand lane in the opposite direction.

**Explanation:** U-turns are not legal everywhere. You must first verify they are permitted, then follow the proper procedure of signaling, yielding, and turning into the correct lane.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often forget that U-turns are not legal everywhere and that you must yield to oncoming traffic.

**Common Mistake:** Turning into the inside lane instead of the outside or right-hand lane when completing a U-turn.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.8

---

### Question 8
**Correct Answer:** D) Treat it as a four-way stop. Stop at the stop line, or if none, before the crosswalk or intersection. Yield to vehicles and pedestrians in the intersection and proceed when safe.

**Explanation:** A non-functioning traffic signal means all drivers must stop and treat the intersection as a four-way stop. You must yield to anyone already in the intersection.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume you can just drive through. You must come to a complete stop first.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to yield to vehicles and pedestrians already in the intersection before proceeding.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.A

---

### Question 9
**Correct Answer:** A) Stay behind the slow vehicle. You may not pass where the solid yellow line is on your side.

**Explanation:** A solid yellow line on your side means you are in a no-passing zone. You must wait until the line changes to a broken yellow or until you are in a legal passing zone.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume you can pass if the car is moving slowly. The no-passing zone rule applies regardless of the other vehicle's speed.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that a solid yellow line means you can pass with extra caution. It means no passing is allowed.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.9

---

### Question 10
**Correct Answer:** C) Slow down, yield to the pedestrian and all vehicles in the intersection, and proceed only when the way is clear and safe.

**Explanation:** A yield sign means you must slow down, look both ways, and yield to pedestrians, bicyclists, and vehicles. You may only proceed when it is safe to do so.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume you have the right-of-way just because you arrived at the yield sign first. Yield means give way to everyone already in or approaching the intersection.

**Common Mistake:** Slowing down but not actually stopping when pedestrians or vehicles are present. You must yield, which may require a complete stop.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.1

---

### Question 11
**Correct Answer:** C) The oncoming traffic has the right-of-way. You must yield to drivers in the opposing traffic lane when making a left turn.

**Explanation:** When making a left turn, you must yield to drivers in the opposing traffic lane. You can only turn when there is a safe gap in oncoming traffic.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often think they can turn as soon as the light turns green. You must still yield to oncoming traffic even with a green light.

**Common Mistake:** Turning too early and cutting off oncoming traffic. Wait for a safe gap.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.1

---

### Question 12
**Correct Answer:** D) Wait for the pedestrian to completely clear the intersection before proceeding, even though you have a green light.

**Explanation:** A green light means you may proceed, but you must yield to pedestrians and vehicles already in the intersection. Safety comes first.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume green means go immediately. Always check for pedestrians and other vehicles first.

**Common Mistake:** Starting to move as soon as the light turns green without checking the intersection. Always scan first.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.A

---

### Question 13
**Correct Answer:** B) Switch to low beams at least 500 feet before reaching the oncoming vehicle.

**Explanation:** Maryland law requires you to dim your high beams at least 500 feet before reaching oncoming vehicles. This prevents blinding the other driver.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often confuse the 500-foot rule for oncoming vehicles with the 300-foot rule for following vehicles.

**Common Mistake:** Waiting too long to switch to low beams. Remember: 500 feet for oncoming, 300 feet for following.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.A (Traffic Signals)

---

### Question 14
**Correct Answer:** C) No. Double solid yellow lines mean passing is not allowed in either direction. You must wait until the lines change.

**Explanation:** Double solid yellow lines separate traffic going in opposite directions and passing is not allowed from either side. You may make a left turn across them, but not pass.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students think you can pass if no cars are coming. Double solid yellow lines prohibit passing regardless of traffic conditions.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing double solid yellow with solid-plus-broken yellow. Double solid means no passing from either side.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.9

---

### Question 15
**Correct Answer:** D) Use the acceleration lane to match the speed of traffic on the highway, and yield to drivers already on the highway.

**Explanation:** When entering a highway from an entrance ramp, you must yield to drivers already on the road. Use the acceleration lane to get up to highway speed.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume you have the right-of-way just because you're on the ramp. Highway traffic has priority.

**Common Mistake:** Entering the highway too slowly without using the acceleration lane properly.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.1

---

### Question 16
**Correct Answer:** A) Come to a complete stop at the stop line. Yield to all other traffic and pedestrians. Proceed when the way is clear.

**Explanation:** A flashing red signal means the same as a stop sign. You must come to a complete stop, yield to all traffic and pedestrians, and proceed only when it is safe.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't treat a flashing red the same as a steady yellow. Flashing red requires a complete stop.

**Common Mistake:** Slowing down instead of coming to a complete stop. A flashing red signal requires a full stop.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.A

---

### Question 17
**Correct Answer:** C) Right turns on red are allowed after a complete stop unless a sign prohibits them, from any lane. Left turns on red are only allowed from a one-way street to another one-way street. Both require yielding to pedestrians and all traffic.

**Explanation:** The key difference is that right turns on red are broadly permitted (unless prohibited), while left turns on red are restricted to one-way-to-one-way situations. Both require a complete stop and yielding.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often assume left turns on red are completely prohibited. They are allowed in one specific situation: one-way street to one-way street.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking you can turn left on red from any lane. This is only allowed from a one-way street to another one-way street.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.A

---

### Question 18
**Correct Answer:** A) The posted speed limit is the maximum under ideal conditions. You may drive slower based on conditions, but it is illegal to drive faster. Maryland Vehicle Law requires driving at a reasonable speed and being aware of hazards. You generally cannot receive a ticket for driving under the limit unless you are driving so slowly that you impede traffic.

**Explanation:** The posted limit is a maximum, not a target. You must adjust speed for conditions. Maryland law requires reasonable and prudent driving. Driving below the limit is generally legal unless it creates a hazard.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often think the posted speed is the required speed. It is the maximum, and you may need to drive slower.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking you must always drive at the posted speed limit. The manual says to match traffic speed up to the limit.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.C

---

### Question 19
**Correct Answer:** B) Solid yellow on your side: no passing for you. Double solid yellow: no passing for either direction. Solid-plus-broken yellow: passing allowed only for traffic on the side of the broken line; not allowed on the side of the solid line.

**Explanation:** Each line configuration communicates different passing rules. Solid yellow restricts passing for one side. Double solid restricts both sides. Solid-plus-broken allows passing for one direction only.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often forget that solid-plus-broken yellow allows passing for one direction. They may also forget that you CAN make a left turn across double solid yellow lines.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that solid-plus-broken yellow means no passing for either direction. It means passing is allowed for one direction.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 3.9

---

### Question 20
**Correct Answer:** A) Following distance gives you time to react. Stopping distance determines how much space you need to actually stop. Speed affects both: higher speed means longer stopping distance. Example: at 3-4 seconds following distance on a wet road with worn tires, you need to reduce speed because stopping distance increases and your safe following distance should increase to 4-5 seconds or more.

**Explanation:** These three concepts are interconnected. Speed increases stopping distance. Following distance provides reaction time. Both must be adjusted for conditions like wet roads or worn tires.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often treat these as separate topics. They are deeply connected and must be considered together.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to increase following distance in adverse conditions. The 3-4 second rule is for ideal conditions only.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Sections 3.C, 3.D, 3.E
