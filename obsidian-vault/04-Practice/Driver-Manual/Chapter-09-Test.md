**Q17. [SYNTHESIS]** Compare and contrast suspension, revocation, and cancellation of a Maryland driver's license in terms of severity, duration, and possibility of reinstatement.

- A) Varies by county
- B) Only required for commercial vehicles
- C) Suspension is the least severe — it is temporary, may allow a restricted license for work/school, and you can regain full driving privileges after the suspension period. Revocation is more severe — there is a minimum waiting period before you can request reinstatement, and the MVA can deny your request. Cancellation is the most severe — your driving privilege is permanently terminated and cannot be reinstated. The MVA may cancel if you obtained the license through fraud, provided false information, or should not have been given a license.
- D) Refusing a test triggers immediate administrative suspension through the MVA, based on the officer's certified refusal statement. This happens regardless of court outcomes. If you are later convicted in court, you face additional court penalties (fines, possible jail, etc.) AND the MVA imposes its own penalties (suspension, possible revocation). The administrative action and court penalties are independent — one does not replace the other. This is why Maryland law states that MVA penalties are imposed "in addition to" court penalties.

**Q20. [SYNTHESIS]** Explain how the wireless device restriction for provisional drivers relates to the broader graduated driver licensing philosophy. Why might Maryland apply stricter rules to new drivers?

- A) The wireless device restriction — which bans ALL wireless communication including hands-free for provisional and permit holders — reflects the graduated licensing philosophy that new drivers need fewer distractions while they develop driving skills. The MVA applies stricter rules to new drivers because: (1) novice drivers are statistically more crash-prone, (2) they have less experience managing multiple tasks while driving, and (3) research shows that any phone use (even hands-free) significantly impairs a new driver's attention. The wireless device ban is part of a broader set of restrictions — along with passenger, nighttime, and seat belt restrictions — designed to reduce risk during the most vulnerable period of driving.
- B) Varies by county
- C) Only required for commercial vehicles
- D) Not specified in the manual
