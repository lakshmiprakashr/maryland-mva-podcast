# Chapter 05 — Driver's Manual — Answer Key

## Answers with Explanations

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** C) .08 or higher.

**Explanation:** Maryland considers drivers to be driving under the influence when their blood alcohol concentration is .08 or higher. Even below this limit, any amount of alcohol can affect your ability to drive safely.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think .08 is the only number that matters. Any amount of alcohol can impair your driving and result in criminal charges.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that if you are below .08, you are safe to drive. Alcohol affects judgment and coordination at much lower levels.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 6.A

---

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** B) Passing is not allowed in either direction. However, you may make a left turn across double solid yellow lines, such as when turning into a driveway.

**Explanation:** Double solid yellow lines separate traffic going in opposite directions and passing is prohibited from both sides. Left turns across them are permitted.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often think you cannot turn across double solid yellow lines. You can make left turns into driveways or side streets.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing double solid yellow with double solid white. Yellow separates opposite directions. White separates same direction.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.C

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** D) Anytime you cannot clearly see at least one thousand feet ahead of your vehicle, and when using your windshield wipers in inclement weather.

**Explanation:** Maryland law requires headlights when visibility is reduced to less than one thousand feet and whenever you use windshield wipers in bad weather.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't forget the windshield wiper rule. Many students only remember the one thousand feet rule.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking headlights are only required at night. They are also required during rain, fog, and any time visibility is low.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.A

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** C) At least five hundred feet.

**Explanation:** Maryland law requires you to switch to low beams at least five hundred feet before reaching oncoming vehicles. This prevents blinding the other driver.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse the five hundred foot rule for oncoming vehicles with the three hundred foot rule for following vehicles.

**Common Mistake:** Using the wrong distance. Remember: five hundred feet for oncoming, three hundred feet when following.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.A

---

### Question 5
**Correct Answer:** C) Release the accelerator or brake pedal, steer in the direction you are skidding, look where you want to go, and press the brake only after regaining traction.

**Explanation:** When skidding, the key is to release the gas or brake, steer into the skid, and look where you want to go. Do not brake until your tires grip the road again.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't slam on the brakes during a skid. This makes the skid worse. Release the pedal and steer.

**Common Mistake:** Steering away from the skid instead of into it. Always steer in the direction the vehicle is skidding.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.E

---

### Question 6
**Correct Answer:** A) When your tires lose contact with the road and ride on a sheet of water, causing you to lose some or all control of your vehicle.

**Explanation:** Hydroplaning happens when water builds up between your tires and the road surface. Your tires literally ride on water instead of the road.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think hydroplaning only happens at high speeds. It can happen at lower speeds with enough water on the road.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking you can brake normally during hydroplaning. You must take your foot off the gas and not apply brakes.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.F

---

### Question 7
**Correct Answer:** A) Buses, school buses, and trucks carrying hazardous materials.

**Explanation:** These vehicles are required by law to stop at every railroad crossing, regardless of whether a train is visible. This is a critical safety rule.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think all vehicles must stop at railroad crossings. Only buses, school buses, and hazmat trucks are required to stop.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing the requirement for specific vehicles with a general requirement for all vehicles.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.G

---

### Question 8
**Correct Answer:** B) Active speed cameras are operating in the work zone.

**Explanation:** Flashing blue lights specifically indicate that speed cameras are active and recording. Flashing amber or yellow means workers are present.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse blue with amber. Blue means speed cameras. Amber means workers present.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking all flashing lights in work zones mean the same thing. Each color has a specific meaning.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.H

---

### Question 9
**Correct Answer:** D) The broken white line separates you from traffic going in the same direction, and you may change lanes. The solid yellow line marks the left edge of the road on a divided highway.

**Explanation:** Broken white lines separate same-direction traffic and allow lane changes. Solid yellow lines mark the left edge on divided highways, one-way roads, and ramps.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse solid yellow with broken yellow. Solid yellow marks the left edge. Broken yellow is the center line on 2-lane roads.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking solid yellow means no passing on a two-way road. Solid yellow on the left edge marks divided highways.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.C

---

### Question 10
**Correct Answer:** D) Switch to low beams at least five hundred feet before reaching the oncoming vehicle. Do not stare into their headlights. Instead, look at the right edge of your lane.

**Explanation:** You must dim high beams five hundred feet before oncoming vehicles. Looking at the right edge of your lane prevents temporary blindness from oncoming headlights.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't wait until the vehicle is close to switch. Five hundred feet is about one block.

**Common Mistake:** Staring directly into oncoming headlights. Always look to the right edge of your lane.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.A, 5.B

---

### Question 11
**Correct Answer:** B) Reduce speed, use low beam headlights only, and if the fog is too thick to drive safely, pull completely off the road to a safe location and turn on your emergency flashers.

**Explanation:** In heavy fog, use low beams only because high beams reflect back. If you cannot see safely, pull completely off the road and use flashers.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't use high beams in fog. They reflect off the water droplets and make visibility worse.

**Common Mistake:** Stopping on the road instead of pulling completely off. You must get completely off the road if fog is too thick.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.C

---

### Question 12
**Correct Answer:** D) Start slowing down long before you reach the intersection. On ice and snow, you need much more distance to stop safely.

**Explanation:** On icy roads, braking distance is significantly increased. Start slowing well in advance of any intersection or turn to avoid skidding.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't wait until you are at the intersection to start braking. On ice, you need much more stopping distance.

**Common Mistake:** Braking too late and then slamming on the brakes, which causes skidding.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.D

---

### Question 13
**Correct Answer:** D) Stop and wait until the gates go up and the warning lights stop flashing. Never try to go around the gates.

**Explanation:** When gates are down or lights are flashing, the road is temporarily closed. You must stop and wait. Trains can come from either direction.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume you can sneak around the gates. The road is closed and trains can be closer than they appear.

**Common Mistake:** Trying to beat the train by going around the gates. Always stop and wait.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.G

---

### Question 14
**Correct Answer:** B) Workers are present. You should slow down, obey work zone signs, and watch for workers and equipment.

**Explanation:** Flashing amber or yellow lights in a work zone indicate workers are present. This means reduced speed, increased alertness, and higher fines.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse amber lights with blue lights. Amber means workers. Blue means speed cameras.

**Common Mistake:** Maintaining normal speed through work zones when workers are present.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.H

---

### Question 15
**Correct Answer:** A) Yield to the pedestrian in the crosswalk first, then yield to traffic already in the circle. Enter only when there is a safe gap.

**Explanation:** At a roundabout, you must yield to both pedestrians in the crosswalk and traffic already in the circle. You do not need to wait if the circle is empty.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't forget about pedestrians. Many students focus only on traffic in the circle and forget the crosswalk.

**Common Mistake:** Entering the roundabout without yielding to traffic already in the circle.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.I

---

### Question 16
**Correct Answer:** B) Take your foot off the gas, do not apply brakes, keep your vehicle going straight, and slow down gradually until you regain control.

**Explanation:** During hydroplaning, your tires lose contact with the road. Braking or turning can make it worse. Keep straight and let the vehicle slow down naturally.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't brake during hydroplaning. This causes loss of control. Release the gas and keep straight.

**Common Mistake:** Panicking and slamming on the brakes. This is the worst thing you can do during hydroplaning.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.F

---

### Question 17
**Correct Answer:** A) Both require headlights. Night driving: use high beams on dark roads, switch to low beams 500 feet before oncoming vehicles and 300 feet when following. Fog: use low beams only, never high beams. Both: increase following distance and reduce speed. Fog is more dangerous because visibility can change rapidly.

**Explanation:** Night and fog driving share some rules but differ in headlight use. Night allows high beams on dark roads. Fog requires low beams only because high beams reflect back off water droplets.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often think the same headlight rules apply to both. Fog specifically requires low beams only.

**Common Mistake:** Using high beams in fog because it seems like it would help. It actually makes visibility worse.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Sections 5.A, 5.B, 5.C

---

### Question 18
**Correct Answer:** D) Broken lines allow passing/lane changes. Solid lines discourage or prohibit them. Double solid lines prohibit passing. Color indicates direction: white = same direction, yellow = opposite direction.

**Explanation:** Pavement markings use line type and color to communicate lane change rules. Broken means permitted, solid means restricted. White for same direction, yellow for opposite direction.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often forget that you CAN turn left across double solid yellow lines. They also confuse white and yellow line rules.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking all solid lines mean the same thing. Solid yellow and solid white have different meanings.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 5.C

---

### Question 19
**Correct Answer:** C) All three involve loss of traction. The common principle is: reduce speed, avoid sudden braking or steering, and regain control gradually. For skidding: release gas/brake, steer in skid direction. For hydroplaning: foot off gas, do not brake, keep straight. For snow/ice: slow down, start braking early, test brakes gently.

**Explanation:** These are all traction-loss scenarios. The key principle is to avoid sudden inputs and let the vehicle regain traction gradually. Speed reduction is the most important preventive measure.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often treat these as completely separate topics. They share the same core principle of managing traction loss.

**Common Mistake:** Reacting with sudden braking or steering in any of these situations, which makes all of them worse.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Sections 5.D, 5.E, 5.F

---

### Question 20
**Correct Answer:** B) Maryland BAC limit is .08 or higher. Under 21 has zero tolerance. Open containers are illegal in passenger area. Penalties include license suspension or revocation. Any amount of alcohol can affect driving and result in criminal charges. Drugs, including some prescription medications, can also result in charges.

**Explanation:** Maryland has strict DUI laws. The legal limit is .08, but any amount can impair driving. Under 21 drivers face zero tolerance. Open containers are prohibited. Drug impairment is also illegal.

**MVA Test Trap:** Students often think .08 is the only number that matters. Under 21 has zero tolerance, and any amount can result in charges.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that prescription drugs are always legal to drive with. If the label warns against driving, you can be charged.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Section 6.A
