**Q17. [SYNTHESIS]** Compare and contrast the requirements for Type 1 and Type 2 Learner's Instructional Permits. What are the key differences in who qualifies, how long each is valid, and what each is used for?

- A) Varies by county
- B) The base minimum hold time is 45 days for all applicants. However, three factors modify this: (1) If you are under 18, you must hold the permit for at least 9 months without violations. (2) If you are 18 with a diploma/GED or ages 19-24, you must hold it for at least 3 months without violations. (3) At any age, if you are convicted of or receive a PBJ for a moving violation, you must hold the permit for at least 9 months from the most recent violation. The longest applicable hold time controls.
- C) Only required for commercial vehicles
- D) Type 1 is for first-time license applicants with no prior license. It is valid for up to two years and requires passing a vision screening and knowledge test. Type 2 is for existing license holders who want a different class of license. It requires the applicant to be at least 16 years and 6 months old, is valid for up to 180 days, and allows the holder to take a driving skills test for a full license after 14 days. Type 1 leads to a Provisional License; Type 2 leads directly to a full license.

**Q19. [SYNTHESIS]** Walk through the complete GDL process from start to finish for a 16-year-old with no prior license. What are all the stages, requirements, and time frames they must complete?

- A) Varies by county
- B) Not specified in the manual
- C) Only required for commercial vehicles
- D) Step 1: Apply for a Type 1 Learner's Instructional Permit at age 15 years 9 months or older. Must bring required documents, have a parent/guardian co-sign, provide a completed DL-300 form (under 16), and pass vision screening and knowledge test. Step 2: While holding the permit, complete the Maryland Driver Education Program, practice driving at least 60 hours (10 at night) with a supervising driver, and keep a Practice and Skills Log. Step 3: Hold the permit for at least 9 months without violations. Step 4: Pass the driving skills test to receive a Provisional License. Step 5: Drive under Provisional restrictions for at least 18 months with no moving violations. Step 6: The MVA automatically converts the Provisional License to a full Driver's License at age 18 or older.

**Q20. [SYNTHESIS]** A co-signer wants to cancel a minor's license. Describe the complete process, what happens to the minor's driving privileges afterward, and what options the minor has to regain driving privileges.

- A) Not specified in the manual
- B) Only required for commercial vehicles
- C) Varies by county
- D) The co-signer must submit a written request to the MVA's Administrative Adjudication Division.
