**Q16. [SYNTHESIS]** Compare the right-of-way rules for pedestrians, bicyclists, and motorcycles. How do they differ?

- A) Pedestrians have right-of-way at crosswalks (marked and unmarked) and must be yielded to when turning. Bicyclists have right-of-way when a motor vehicle is turning, and when riding in bike lanes or shoulders — they are treated as vehicles. Motorcyclists have the same rights as any driver but require extra space and attention due to their vulnerability. The key difference is that pedestrians win at crosswalks, bicyclists win at turns and in bike lanes, and motorcyclists have full lane rights.
- B) Varies by county
- C) ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance Systems) includes features like Automatic Emergency Braking, Lane Keeping Assistance, and Blind Spot Warning. When used properly, they prevent 40% of crashes. However, the driver is ALWAYS responsible for safe operation. ADAS has limitations in rain, snow, fog, on hills, and around curves. Technology names vary by vehicle and don't always describe what it does. You must keep sensors clean, software updated, and be familiar with your specific vehicle's systems.
- D) Only required for commercial vehicles

**Q18. [SYNTHESIS]** How do truck No-Zones compare to car blind spots, and why are they more dangerous?
