# Chapter 10 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Lesson 5 covers **turning at intersections** (full 9-step procedure), **leaving traffic** (parking within 12 inches of curb), **blind spot awareness**, **changing lanes** (7-step procedure), and **risk awareness** — recognizing and managing the constant danger on the road. This is the most comprehensive lesson, combining all previous skills into real-world driving on busier roads (35-50 mph).

## Why It Matters

Turning, changing lanes, and managing risk are the daily skills of driving. The lane-change procedure and the risk awareness section are heavily tested on the MVA exam. The 12-inch curb rule and 3-4 second signal requirement are specific numbers the exam loves.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Turning at intersections (9 steps):** Check for vehicles/pedestrians/signs/devices, turn head for blind spots, signal 3-4 seconds in advance, position vehicle correctly, adjust speed/stop if required, identify safe gap, check mirrors for hazards, steer into proper lane, adjust speed
- **Leaving traffic/parking:** Search ahead, check mirrors, signal, slow down, steer within 12 inches of curb
- **Blind spots:** Mirrors cannot see all areas; MUST turn head before lane changes and turns
- **Changing lanes (7 steps):** Maintain space cushion, check traffic all directions, select safe gap, signal intentions, recheck in direction of lane change, adjust speed and steer smoothly, cancel signal once established
- **Risk is everywhere:** Children may run into traffic, drivers may stop suddenly, someone may swerve into your lane from phone distraction
- **Experienced vs. novice:** Experienced drivers anticipate hazards; novice drivers often don't recognize risky situations until it's too late
- **Learning to recognize and manage risk is critical to safe driving**
- **Busy road practice:** After neighborhood practice, move to roads with 35-50 mph speeds; focus on looking ahead and watching for hazards

## MVA Test Traps

| Trap | Correct Answer |
|------|---------------|
| "Mirrors are enough for lane changes" | Must turn head to check blind spots; mirrors have gaps |
| "Signal and change lanes immediately" | Must signal, recheck, then change when safe |
| "Risk only exists in heavy traffic" | Risk is constant — even on empty roads, unexpected hazards can appear |
| "Novice drivers are safer because they're cautious" | Novice drivers are actually more likely to miss risks because they haven't learned to recognize them |
| "Parking within 2 feet of curb is fine" | Must be within 12 inches (1 foot) of curb |

## Common Mistakes

- Not rechecking in the direction of the lane change before moving
- Failing to maintain a space cushion before changing lanes
- Not canceling the signal after completing a lane change
- Underestimating risk because traffic seems light
- Focusing only on the car ahead and not scanning for developing hazards
- Not adjusting speed smoothly when entering a new lane
- Forgetting to check blind spots before changing lanes

## Memory Tricks

- **"Space, Check, Gap, Signal, Recheck, Go, Cancel"** — Lane change sequence: **S**pace cushion, **C**heck traffic, **G**ap selection, **S**ignal, **R**echeck, **G**o, **C**ancel
- **"12 inches, not 12 feet"** — Parking curb distance: within 1 foot, not several feet
- **"3 to 4 seconds signal"** — Count it out: one-one-thousand, two-one-thousand, three-one-thousand, four-one-thousand
- **"Head turn every time"** — Before any lane change or turn, physically turn your head
- **"Assume the worst"** — A child could run out, a driver could swerve, someone could be texting. Always expect the unexpected.
- **"Scan ahead, not just ahead"** — Look far down the road, not just at the car in front of you

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

- **Turn signals** — Required 3-4 seconds before lane changes and turns
- **Lane markings** — Dashed white = lane change permitted; solid white = do not cross; yellow = opposing traffic
- **Speed advisory signs** — Recommend safe speeds for curves and exits
- **Road hazard signs** — Warn of potential dangers ahead

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **Signal requirement:** At least 3-4 seconds before turning or changing lanes (MD Transportation Article 21-604)
- **Parking distance:** Within 12 inches of curb when leaving traffic
- **Right turn on red:** Full stop required, then proceed when safe
- **Lane change signaling:** Signal must be active before and during lane change; cancel after
- **Speed on busy roads:** Practice on 35-50 mph roads after mastering neighborhood driving
