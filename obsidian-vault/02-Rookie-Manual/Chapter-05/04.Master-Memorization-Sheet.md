# Chapter-05 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Chapter 5 covers two main topics: requirements for drivers over 25 and beginning to drive. It explains the reduced requirements for older drivers, special situations (driving in other states, expired permits, technology during skills tests), and the initial practice lessons including pre-entry checks, getting ready to drive, and steering basics. Seatbelt laws are also covered.

## Why It Matters

The MVA tests on the specific requirements for drivers over 25, the technology rules for skills tests, and the fundamental pre-driving procedures. Understanding these concepts is essential for both the knowledge test and for safe driving practices.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Drivers 25+**: 14 practice hours (3 at night), 45-day holding period
- **Still required**: MVA-approved driver education course and supervising driver
- **Moving violation/PBJ**: Extends holding period to 9 months
- **Permit required**: Must have permit every time you drive, even in parking lots
- **Out-of-state driving**: Check with other states' MVA first
- **Permit expired**: Must retake law test
- **Skills test technology**: Backup camera YES, parking assist NO
- **Seatbelt law**: Everyone must wear a seatbelt, no exceptions
- **Pre-entry check**: Check for hazards (broken glass, body damage, tires, lights, fluid leaks, people/objects)
- **Getting ready**: Adjust seat, safety belts, mirrors; know all controls; turn off electronic devices

## MVA Test Traps

- **"14 hours vs 60 hours" trap**: 14 hours for 25+, 60 hours for under 25. Don't swap these.
- **"45 days vs 9 months" trap**: 45 days for clean record, 9 months if violation. Don't assume 45 days is guaranteed.
- **"Backup camera vs parking assist" trap**: Camera is okay, assist is not. Don't confuse these.
- **"Everyone must wear seatbelt" trap**: No exceptions for age or seating position.

## Common Mistakes

- Forgetting that drivers 25+ still need driver education and a supervising driver
- Not knowing that parking assist is prohibited during skills tests
- Assuming seatbelts are only required for front seat passengers
- Skipping the pre-entry check before driving

## Memory Tricks

- **"14 for you"** — drivers 25+ need only 14 practice hours
- **"45 days clean"** — 45 days if no violations, 9 months if you have one
- **"Camera yes, assist no"** — backup camera allowed, parking assist prohibited
- **"Click it every time"** — seatbelts required every trip, no exceptions

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

No road signs or signals are covered in this chapter. Focus on permit requirements, technology rules, and pre-driving procedures.

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **Seatbelt Law**: Everyone must wear a seatbelt, regardless of age or seating position. Driver can be ticketed if anyone in vehicle is unbuckled.
- **Learner's Permit**: Must be in possession every time you drive
- **Skills Test Technology**: Backup camera and onboard radar allowed; parking assist prohibited
- **Driver Education**: Required for all ages, including 25+
- **Supervising Driver**: Required for all practice driving
