## [1:00 - 3:30] Requirements for Drivers Over 25

What if you are 25 years of age or older? The requirements change significantly.

[PAUSE]

You still need to complete a **Maryland MVA approved driver education course**. This is required for all ages.

[PAUSE]

You still need a **supervising driver or coach** to help you practice. Supervision is still required.

[PAUSE]

But here is the difference: you only need **fourteen hours of supervised practice** with a minimum of **three practice hours at night**.

[PAUSE]

Compare that to the **sixty hours** required for drivers under 25. That is a significant reduction.

[PAUSE]

And you only need to wait **forty five days** before being allowed to take the skills test and receive a driver's license, if you do not receive any moving violation citations or PBJ, which stands for **Probation Before Judgment**.

[PAUSE]

If you do receive a moving violation citation or a PBJ, you will be required to hold the learner's instructional permit for **nine months** before being allowed to take the tests.

[PAUSE]

---

## [3:30 - 5:30] Special Situations

Let us cover some common questions about special situations.

[PAUSE]

**Do I need a learner's permit to start practicing?**

[PAUSE]

Before you start driving, you must have a learner's instructional permit. You must also have your learner's permit with you every time you drive, even in an empty parking lot.

[PAUSE]

**My family is going on vacation. What if I want to drive in another state?**

[PAUSE]

You should check with the Motor Vehicle Administration in those states before driving there. A state may not accept a Maryland learner's permit.

[PAUSE]

**I just got my receipt from the MVA. Can I start driving?**

[PAUSE]

You can drive but you must keep your receipt with you when you drive.

[PAUSE]

**What if my learner's permit expires before I get my provisional license?**

[PAUSE]

You will need to **retake the law test** and get a new learner's permit before you will be allowed to take the driving skills test.

[PAUSE]

---

## [5:30 - 7:00] Technology During Skills Test

Here is a critical fact the MVA loves to test.

[PAUSE]

**Can I use parking assist, backup cameras, and other technologies when I take my skills test?**

[PAUSE]

You may use your **backup camera** and the vehicle's **onboard radar system**.

[PAUSE]

But you may **not** use the **parking assist** feature.

[PAUSE]

The distinction is about driver control. Backup cameras help you see, but parking assist takes over steering. The MVA wants to test your ability to steer and park.

[PAUSE]

Remember: **Camera yes, assist no**.

[PAUSE]

---

## [7:00 - 9:30] Beginning to Drive

Now let us cover the essential steps for beginning to drive.

[PAUSE]

This lesson should be completed in a driveway, parking lot, or a lightly traveled residential road.

[PAUSE]

**Pre-entry Check**

[PAUSE]

Have the driver check all around the vehicle for hazards. Look for broken glass on or around the vehicle. Check for body damage. Check the condition of tires and lights. Look for fluid leaks under the vehicle. Watch for people or objects.

[PAUSE]

**Getting Ready to Drive**

[PAUSE]

Be sure to help your new driver adjust seat, safety belts, and mirrors appropriately. Be sure the new driver knows where all the controls are: headlights, turn signal, hazard lights, horn, heater and defroster and air conditioning, parking brake, windshield wipers, door locks, gear shift, gas pedal, and brake pedal.

[PAUSE]

Fasten seat belts securely. Turn off all electronic devices.

[PAUSE]

**Steering**

[PAUSE]

Have the new driver turn the wheel slightly to the right and to the left to develop a feel for how the vehicle will move when the wheel turns.

[PAUSE]

## [9:30 - 10:30] Seatbelt Law

Let us talk about seatbelts.

[PAUSE]

**Who has to wear a safety belt in Maryland?**

[PAUSE]

**Everybody**, no matter where they are seated in the vehicle and no matter what their age.

[PAUSE]

Seatbelts are your best protection in a crash. You can be ticketed if everyone in the vehicle is not buckled up.

[PAUSE]

**Click it every time** you drive or ride, even on short trips.

[PAUSE]

Wear your lap belt low on your hips, under your stomach.

[PAUSE]

The MVA exam loves to ask: Do back seat passengers need seatbelts? The answer is **yes**. Everyone must buckle up.

[PAUSE]

## [10:30 - 12:00] Quick Review

Let us quickly review the key facts.

[PAUSE]

Drivers over 25: fourteen practice hours, three at night, forty five day holding period.

[PAUSE]

Still need driver education and supervising driver.

[PAUSE]

Moving violation or PBJ extends to nine months.

[PAUSE]

Backup camera: yes. Parking assist: no.

[PAUSE]

Permit required every time you drive.

[PAUSE]

Pre-entry check: hazards, tires, lights, leaks, people.

[PAUSE]

Adjust seat, belts, mirrors. Know all controls. Turn off electronics.

[PAUSE]

Seatbelts: everyone, every time.
