## [1:00 - 3:00] Choosing a Driving School

How do you choose a driving school for your Rookie Driver?

[PAUSE]

First and most important: **Make sure the school is licensed by the Maryland Motor Vehicle Administration**. Only licensed schools can certify completion of the driver education program.

[PAUSE]

A complete listing of driving schools in your area can be found at **www dot mva dot maryland dot gov**.

[PAUSE]

Ask other parents about their experiences. Check with several schools to see which ones have a convenient location and schedule.

[PAUSE]

Remember your new driver must attend **thirty hours of classroom** and **six hours of behind the wheel training**.

[PAUSE]

Check with the driving school about their policies. Ask about the Unit One Orientation schedule. How much does it cost? Is a payment plan available? What is the refund policy?

[PAUSE]

## [3:00 - 6:30] Graduated Licensing Requirements by Age

Now let us cover the specific requirements of Maryland's Graduated Licensing System.

[PAUSE]

The MVA exam loves to ask about these numbers. Listen carefully.

[PAUSE]

**Age 18 or under without a high school diploma**: You must hold your permit for **nine months**. You need **sixty hours** of practice, including **ten hours at night**.

[PAUSE]

**Age 18 with a high school diploma**: Your holding period drops to **three months**. You still need **sixty hours** of practice with **ten hours at night**.

[PAUSE]

**Ages 19 through 24**: Also **three months** holding period. Also **sixty hours** of practice with **ten hours at night**.

[PAUSE]

**Age 25 and older**: Only **forty five days** holding period. Only **fourteen hours** of practice with **three hours at night**.

[PAUSE]

Notice the pattern: the first three groups need sixty hours, the last group needs only fourteen.

[PAUSE]

---

## [6:30 - 9:00] Supervisor and Documentation Requirements

Let us cover the supervisor and documentation requirements.

[PAUSE]

Your supervising driver must be at least **twenty one years old** and must have held a license for at least **three years**.

[PAUSE]

Nighttime practice hours are defined as **one half hour before sunset until one half hour after sunrise**.

[PAUSE]

You must complete the Maryland Certified Driver Education Program: **thirty hours of classroom instruction** and **six hours of behind the wheel training**.

[PAUSE]

Your learner's instructional permit is good for **two years, or twenty four months**, from the date it is issued.

[PAUSE]

For the skills test, you must present your completed skills practice log, completed certification page, and valid Maryland learner's instructional permit.

[PAUSE]

The practice driving skills log must be signed by an individual certifying the practice driving requirements were fulfilled.

[PAUSE]

---

## [9:00 - 10:30] Moving Violations and PBJ

Here is a critical fact the MVA loves to test.

[PAUSE]

If you are convicted or granted **Probation Before Judgment, also known as PBJ**, for any moving violation, your time will be **restarted and extended to nine months**.

[PAUSE]

This applies to **all ages**. Even if you are twenty five or older and would normally only wait forty five days, a moving violation or PBJ extends your holding period to nine months.

[PAUSE]

**Probation Before Judgment, or PBJ** (pronunciation: pro-BAY-shun be-FORE JUDGE-ment) is a legal disposition that can affect your driving privileges.

[PAUSE]

The MVA exam loves to ask: Does being over 25 exempt you from the nine month extension? The answer is **no**.

[PAUSE]

## [10:30 - 12:00] Quick Review

Let us quickly review the key facts.

[PAUSE]

Driving school must be MVA licensed. Thirty hours classroom, six hours behind the wheel.

[PAUSE]

Holding periods: nine months for 18 without diploma, three months for 18 with diploma or ages 19 through 24, forty five days for 25 plus.

[PAUSE]

Practice hours: sixty hours for under 25, fourteen hours for 25 plus.

[PAUSE]

Supervisor: twenty one plus with three plus years licensed.

[PAUSE]

Moving violation or PBJ extends holding period to nine months for all ages.

[PAUSE]

Permit valid for two years.

[PAUSE]
