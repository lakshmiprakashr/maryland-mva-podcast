# Chapter-03 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Chapter 3 covers two critical topics: choosing a licensed driving school and understanding Maryland's Graduated Licensing System requirements. This chapter provides the specific holding periods, practice hours, and education requirements based on age groups.

## Why It Matters

The MVA tests extensively on the graduated licensing requirements. Knowing the exact holding periods, practice hours, and age-based differences is essential for both the knowledge test and for planning your licensing timeline.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Driver education**: 30 hours classroom + 6 hours behind-the-wheel (required for all ages)
- **Age 18 or under (no HS diploma)**: 9-month holding period, 60 practice hours (10 at night)
- **Age 18 (with HS diploma)**: 3-month holding period, 60 practice hours (10 at night)
- **Ages 19-24**: 3-month holding period, 60 practice hours (10 at night)
- **Age 25+**: 45-day holding period, 14 practice hours (3 at night)
- **Moving violation/PBJ**: Extends holding period to 9 months for all ages
- **Supervising driver**: Must be 21+ with 3+ years licensed
- **Permit validity**: 2 years (24 months)

## MVA Test Traps

- **"9 months vs 3 months" trap**: Age 18 without HS diploma = 9 months; with HS diploma = 3 months. The MVA loves to ask about this difference.
- **"45 days" trap**: Only drivers 25+ get the 45-day period. Don't assume all adults get this.
- **"Moving violation extends to 9 months" trap**: This applies to ALL ages, not just younger drivers.
- **"60 vs 14 hours" trap**: 60 hours for under 25, 14 hours for 25+. Don't swap these.

## Common Mistakes

- Confusing the holding periods for different age groups
- Forgetting that a moving violation extends the holding period to 9 months
- Not knowing that nighttime hours are defined as ½ hour before sunset to ½ hour after sunrise
- Assuming driver education is optional for drivers 25+

## Memory Tricks

- **"9-3-3-45"** — holding periods: 9 months (18 no diploma), 3 months (18 with diploma), 3 months (19-24), 45 days (25+)
- **"60-60-60-14"** — practice hours: 60 for first three groups, 14 for 25+
- **"10-10-10-3"** — night hours: 10 for first three groups, 3 for 25+

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

No road signs or signals are covered in this chapter. Focus on the graduated licensing requirements and holding periods.

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **Graduated Licensing System**: Three-step system with specific requirements per age group
- **Holding Period**: Varies by age (9 months, 3 months, or 45 days)
- **Practice Hours**: 60 hours (under 25) or 14 hours (25+), with required night hours
- **Driver Education**: 30 classroom + 6 behind-the-wheel (required for all)
- **Moving Violation/PBJ**: Extends holding period to 9 months
- **Supervisor Requirements**: 21+ years old, 3+ years licensed
