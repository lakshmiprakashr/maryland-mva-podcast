# Chapter-01 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Welcome to Maryland's Graduated Licensing System. Chapter 1 introduces the three-step process to becoming a fully licensed driver in Maryland: Learner's Instructional Permit → Provisional License → Full Driver's License. This chapter also covers the coach's role and the practice requirements.

## Why It Matters

Understanding Maryland's graduated licensing structure is foundational. The MVA tests your knowledge of this system extensively. Missing key requirements (like practice hours or supervisor qualifications) can cost you your permit or delay your license.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Three-step system**: Learner's Permit → Provisional License → Driver's License
- **Minimum practice hours**: 60 hours for drivers under 25; 14 hours for drivers 25+
- **Supervisor requirements**: Must be 21+ years old AND hold a license for 3+ years
- **Night practice hours**: Defined as ½ hour before sunset to ½ hour after sunrise
- **Manual sections**: Coach tips, suggested routes/skills, coach-new driver agreement, practice log, certification page, laws chart

## MVA Test Traps

- **"18" vs "21"**: Supervisors must be 21+, not 18+. Turning 18 does NOT qualify you to supervise.
- **Practice hours confusion**: 60 hours for under 25, 14 hours for 25+. The MVA loves to swap these numbers.
- **"Three years" trap**: Supervisor must have held a license for 3+ years, not just be 3+ years old.

## Common Mistakes

- Forgetting that practice must happen in **varied conditions** (different roads, weather, times)
- Thinking the coach agreement is optional — it's **required** before starting practice
- Assuming the certification page is optional for the skills test — it's **mandatory**

## Memory Tricks

- **"60 for youth, 14 for you"** — under 25 needs 60 hours, 25+ needs 14 hours
- **"21 and 3"** — supervisor must be 21+ with 3+ years licensed
- **"3 steps to drive"** — permit, provisional, full license

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

No road signs or signals are covered in this introductory chapter. Focus on the licensing system and requirements.

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **Practice Driving Log**: Must be signed by a certifying individual; presented at MVA with valid permit
- **Certification Page**: Must bring to MVA for skills test
- **Supervisor Rule**: Only licensed drivers 21+ with 3+ years experience can supervise practice driving
- **Graduated Licensing**: Maryland law requires the three-step system for all new drivers
