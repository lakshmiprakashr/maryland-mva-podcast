## [1:00 - 3:30] The Three Step System

Maryland uses a three step system called the **Graduated Licensing System**.

[PAUSE]

The first step is your **Learner's Instructional Permit**. This is where you start. You can practice driving with a supervising driver.

[PAUSE]

The second step is your **Provisional License**. This gives you more freedom, but still has restrictions.

[PAUSE]

The third step is your **Full Driver's License**. This is the goal. You earn this after demonstrating safe driving habits.

[PAUSE]

The MVA exam loves to ask about these three steps. Remember: **Permit, Provisional, Full License**. That is the order.

[PAUSE]

Now let us talk about what you need to do during the permit phase.

[PAUSE]

---

## [3:30 - 6:00] Practice Requirements

Here is where the numbers get important. The MVA will test you on these.

[PAUSE]

If you are **under twenty five years old**, you need **sixty hours** of supervised practice. That includes **ten hours at night**.

[PAUSE]

If you are **twenty five or older**, you only need **fourteen hours** of supervised practice. That includes **three hours at night**.

[PAUSE]

Nighttime is defined as **one half hour before sunset until one half hour after sunrise**. Do not confuse this with just after dark.

[PAUSE]

Your supervising driver must be at least **twenty one years old** and must have held a license for at least **three years**. Both requirements must be met.

[PAUSE]

The MVA loves to ask: Can a twenty year old with two years of experience supervise you? The answer is **no**. They need to be twenty one with three years.

[PAUSE]

---

## [6:00 - 9:00] Documentation and Requirements

You will need several documents when you go to the MVA for your skills test.

[PAUSE]

First, you need a **practice driving skills log**. This records all your practice hours. It must be signed by someone certifying you completed the requirements.

[PAUSE]

Second, you need a **certification page**. This certifies that you have completed the required practice time. You **must** bring this to the MVA.

[PAUSE]

Third, you need your valid **Maryland learner's instructional permit**.

[PAUSE]

If your permit expires before you get your provisional license, you will need to **retake the law test** and get a new permit.

[PAUSE]

Your learner's permit is valid for **two years** from the date it is issued.

[PAUSE]

The Maryland Certified Driver Education Program requires **thirty hours of classroom instruction** and **six hours of behind the wheel training**.

[PAUSE]

---

## [9:00 - 11:00] Coach's Role

The coach plays a vital role in this process.

[PAUSE]

A coach is someone helping a Rookie Driver complete their required practice time. The coach should be a good role model.

[PAUSE]

Before you start driving, you and your coach should complete the **Rookie Driver Coach Driving Agreement**. This is not optional.

[PAUSE]

The agreement helps you and your coach develop a contract to encourage safe driving throughout the dangerous first years of driving.

[PAUSE]

If your new driver does not obey your rules or Maryland law, you can withdraw consent and cancel the license. The new driver may not be able to get a new license until turning eighteen.

[PAUSE]

The best coach is a good role model. Responsible driving is one of the best things you can do to keep your new driver safe.

[PAUSE]

Let us quickly recap the key facts.

Three steps: **Permit, Provisional, Full License**.

[PAUSE]

Sixty hours for under twenty five. Fourteen hours for twenty five and older.

[PAUSE]

Supervisor must be twenty one plus with three plus years licensed.

[PAUSE]

Night hours: one half hour before sunset to one half hour after sunrise.

[PAUSE]

Permit is valid for two years. Bring certification page and practice log to skills test.

[PAUSE]

Maryland Certified Driver Education: thirty classroom hours, six behind the wheel hours.
