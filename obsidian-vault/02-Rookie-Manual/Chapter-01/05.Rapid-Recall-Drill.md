## Questions

**Q1. [Type: RECALL]** What are the three steps in Maryland's Graduated Licensing System?

**Answer:** Learner's Instructional Permit, Provisional License, and Full Driver's License.

**Explanation:** Maryland uses a three-step graduated system to gradually increase driving privileges as new drivers gain experience and demonstrate safe driving habits.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse the order or think there are only two steps (permit and license). The provisional license is the middle step.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting that the provisional license is a separate step, not just an extension of the permit.

---

**Q2. [Type: RECALL]** What is the minimum number of supervised practice hours required for a driver under 25 years old?

**Answer:** 60 hours of practice with a supervising driver, including 10 hours at night.

**Explanation:** Maryland law requires 60 total practice hours for drivers under 25, with 10 of those hours occurring during nighttime (defined as ½ hour before sunset to ½ hour after sunrise).

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse with 14 hours (which is for drivers 25 and older). The MVA loves to swap these numbers.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting the nighttime hour requirement — it's not just 60 hours total, but 10 must be at night.

---

**Q3. [Type: RECALL]** How old must a supervising driver be, and how long must they have held their license?

**Answer:** At least 21 years old and must have held a driver's license for at least 3 years.

**Explanation:** Maryland requires supervisors to be both age 21+ AND have 3+ years of licensed driving experience to ensure adequate experience mentoring new drivers.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse with 18 years old (the age you can get a permit) or 2 years (permit validity period). The supervisor requirements are specifically 21 and 3.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking the supervisor only needs to be 21, forgetting the 3-year license requirement.

---

**Q4. [Type: RECALL]** How are nighttime practice hours defined in Maryland?

**Answer:** ½ hour before sunset until ½ hour after sunrise.

**Explanation:** Nighttime driving practice is specifically defined by the twilight periods around sunset and sunrise, not just "dark hours." This captures the most challenging visibility conditions.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse with "after dark" or "sunset to sunrise." The definition includes the half-hour transitions.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking nighttime starts at sunset, forgetting the ½ hour before sunset counts.

---

**Q5. [Type: RECALL]** What document must you bring to the MVA when taking your skills test?

**Answer:** The completed certification page, along with your practice driving skills log and valid Maryland learner's instructional permit.

**Explanation:** The certification page certifies that you have completed the required practice time. Without it, you cannot take the skills test.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think you only need the permit — the certification page is mandatory for the skills test.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to bring the signed practice log along with the certification page.

---

**Q6. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are 17 years old and want to practice driving. Your older sister is 20 and has had her license for 2 years. Can she supervise you?

**Answer:** No. The supervising driver must be at least 21 years old and have held a license for at least 3 years.

**Explanation:** Even though your sister is licensed, she doesn't meet either requirement: she's under 21 and has only held her license for 2 years (needs 3).

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume any licensed adult can supervise. Both the age (21+) and experience (3+ years) requirements must be met.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that being licensed is enough, without checking both age AND years of experience.

---

**Q7. [Type: APPLICATION]** You have completed 55 hours of practice driving, but only 5 of those hours were at night. Are you ready to take the skills test if you're under 25?

**Answer:** No. You need 60 total hours with 10 hours at night. You're short 5 hours total and 5 nighttime hours.

**Explanation:** The requirements are 60 total hours AND 10 nighttime hours. Meeting one requirement but not the other means you're not eligible.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think that completing 60 hours automatically includes the nighttime requirement. The 10 nighttime hours are a separate requirement within the 60.

**Common Mistake:** Counting nighttime hours toward the total without realizing they must be specifically designated as nighttime practice.

---

**Q8. [Type: APPLICATION]** Your learner's permit is about to expire before you've completed your practice hours. What should you do?

**Answer:** You must retake the law test and get a new learner's permit before you can take the driving skills test.

**Explanation:** An expired permit means you lose your driving privileges. You must go through the permit process again, including passing the law test.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume you can just renew the permit — you must retake the law test.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking you can continue practicing with an expired permit, which is illegal.

---

**Q9. [Type: APPLICATION]** You just received your learner's permit receipt from the MVA. Can you start practicing driving today?

**Answer:** Yes, but you must keep the receipt with you when you drive.

**Explanation:** The receipt serves as proof of your permit while you wait for the actual permit card. You can drive with it as long as it's in your possession.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think you must wait for the physical card — the receipt is valid for driving.

**Common Mistake:** Waiting to practice until you receive the physical permit card, which could take weeks.

---

**Q10. [Type: APPLICATION]** You want to drive in Virginia while on vacation with your family. You have a Maryland learner's permit and your parent (licensed for 10 years) is with you. Can you drive in Virginia?

**Answer:** Not necessarily. You should check with Virginia's DMV first, as other states may not accept a Maryland learner's permit.

**Explanation:** Each state has its own rules about recognizing out-of-state learner's permits. Virginia may not accept Maryland's permit, even with a qualifying supervisor.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume your Maryland permit works in all states. You must verify with each state's motor vehicle authority.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that a Maryland permit automatically allows driving in any state.

---

**Q11. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are 26 years old and received a speeding ticket while practicing with your learner's permit. How does this affect your licensing timeline?

**Answer:** If convicted or granted PBJ for a moving violation, your holding period extends to 9 months before you can take the skills test.

**Explanation:** A moving violation or PBJ (probation before judgment) triggers the 9-month extended holding period, regardless of your age. Without a violation, drivers 25+ only wait 45 days.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think that being over 25 exempts you from the 9-month extension. The extension applies to all ages if there's a violation.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming that age 25+ means you avoid all penalties for violations during the permit phase.

---

**Q12. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Compare the practice requirements for a 17-year-old versus a 26-year-old applying for a provisional license in Maryland.

**Answer:** A 17-year-old needs 60 practice hours (10 at night) and a 9-month holding period. A 26-year-old needs only 14 practice hours (3 at night) and a 45-day holding period.

**Explanation:** Maryland reduces requirements for older drivers, recognizing they may have more maturity and life experience. However, both must complete the Maryland Certified Driver Education Program.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume the requirements are the same for all ages. The age-based differences are significant.

**Common Mistake:** Focusing only on practice hours and forgetting the different holding periods (9 months vs 45 days).

---

**Q13. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Why does Maryland require both a minimum number of practice hours AND a minimum holding period for the learner's permit?

**Answer:** The practice hours ensure sufficient driving experience, while the holding period ensures that experience is gained over time, not crammed into a short period.

**Explanation:** Driving skill develops through repeated practice over time. The holding period prevents "cramming" all practice hours into a few days, ensuring gradual skill development.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think the holding period is just a waiting period — it's designed to spread practice over time.

**Common Mistake:** Focusing on hours alone without understanding that the time component is equally important for skill development.

---

**Q14. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** What happens if a coach fails to complete the Rookie Driver - Coach Driving Agreement?

**Answer:** The agreement is required to start practice driving. Without it, the new driver cannot legally begin supervised practice.

**Explanation:** The coach-new driver agreement is a contractual commitment to safe driving practices. It's not optional — it must be completed before any practice driving begins.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think the agreement is just a recommendation. It's a legal requirement that must be completed.

**Common Mistake:** Treating the agreement as optional paperwork rather than a required legal document.

---

**Q15. [Type: RECALL]** What does the Maryland Driver's Manual include to help new drivers?

**Answer:** Tips for effective coaching, suggested routes and skills, a coach-new driver agreement, a practice log, a certification page, and a chart of laws and regulations.

**Explanation:** The manual provides comprehensive resources to guide both the coach and new driver through the graduated licensing process.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't forget the specific components. The MVA may ask about any of these resources.

**Common Mistake:** Remembering only some components and forgetting others, like the certification page.

---

**Q16. [Type: RECALL]** What is the purpose of the practice driving skills log?

**Answer:** To record practice driving hours under the required supervision of an experienced driver.

**Explanation:** The log serves as documentation that you've completed the required practice hours. It must be signed and presented at the MVA.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse the log with the certification page. The log tracks hours; the certification page certifies completion.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking the log is optional documentation rather than a required record.

---

**Q17. [Type: APPLICATION]** You're practicing driving and your coach tells you to stop at a stop sign. What should you do?

**Answer:** Come to a complete stop at the stop line or before entering the crosswalk, then proceed when safe.

**Explanation:** This is a fundamental driving skill that should be practiced from the very first lesson. The coach should be guiding you through proper stopping procedures.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse a stop sign with a yield sign. Stop signs require a complete stop.

**Common Mistake:** Performing a "rolling stop" instead of coming to a complete stop.

---

**Q18. [Type: APPLICATION]** Your coach is teaching you to use the turn signal. When should you signal before making a turn?

**Answer:** At least 100 feet before making a turn on a highway, or 50 feet before turning on other roads.

**Explanation:** Signaling early gives other drivers time to react to your intended maneuver. The specific distance depends on the type of road.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think you can signal at the last minute. Early signaling is a legal requirement.

**Common Mistake:** Signaling too late, which can surprise other drivers and cause accidents.

---

**Q19. [Type: RECALL]** What is the Maryland Certified Driver Education Program?

**Answer:** A program consisting of a minimum of 30 hours of classroom instruction and 6 hours of behind-the-wheel training.

**Explanation:** This program is required for all new drivers, regardless of age. It provides both theoretical knowledge and practical driving experience.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse the education requirements with practice hours. The program is separate from the 60/14 practice hours.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking the education program replaces the need for supervised practice hours.

---

**Q20. [Type: RECALL]** How long is a Maryland learner's instructional permit valid?

**Answer:** 2 years (24 months) from the date it is issued.

**Explanation:** The permit has a strict expiration date. If it expires before you get your provisional license, you must retake the law test.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse the 2-year validity with the 9-month or 45-day holding periods. The permit can be valid longer than the holding period.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting the expiration date and letting the permit lapse.

## Quick Reference

- **Three steps**: Permit → Provisional → Full License
- **60 hours** for under 25; **14 hours** for 25+
- **Supervisor**: 21+ years old, 3+ years licensed
- **Night hours**: ½ hr before sunset to ½ hr after sunrise
- **Permit validity**: 2 years (24 months)
- **Education**: 30 classroom + 6 behind-the-wheel hours
- **Certification page** required at skills test
- **Practice log** must be signed and presented

**Test Day Tip:** Always verify you meet ALL requirements (hours, holding period, education) before scheduling your skills test. The MVA will check your certification page and practice log.
