## [1:00 - 4:30] Passing Another Vehicle

Let us start with passing. This is a high-risk maneuver, so the steps matter.

[PAUSE]

Step one. Maintain a proper interval between you and the vehicle to be passed.

Step two. Select an adequate gap in oncoming traffic. Do not force it.

Step three. Signal your intentions.

Step four. Check your mirrors and perform a head check before changing lanes.

[PAUSE]

Step five. Steer smoothly into the passing lane and accelerate.

And here is the big rule on the test. Even when passing, you are NOT allowed to exceed the posted speed limit.

[EMPHASIS: not allowed]

[PAUSE]

Step six. When the entire front of the passed vehicle can be seen in your rear-view mirror, signal and steer smoothly back into your original lane.

[PAUSE]

Notice that last rule. The ENTIRE front of the vehicle. Not just the front bumper. You must see the whole front before returning to your lane.

[PAUSE]

Now, being passed. If someone is passing you, maintain your speed and lane position. Check for oncoming traffic that may present a hazard to the passing vehicle. And slow down if necessary to help them reenter the lane.

[PAUSE]

Do NOT speed up when being passed. That traps the passing vehicle in the oncoming lane longer and creates a dangerous situation.

## [4:30 - 9:00] Following Distance and Night Driving

Now let us talk about following distance. The proper distance under ideal conditions is three to four seconds.

[PAUSE]

Here is how to measure it. Start counting when the vehicle in front of you passes a stationary object. A lamp post or a road marker works great. Stop counting when you reach the same object. You should count three to four seconds.

[PAUSE]

Increase this distance in bad weather or at night. The manual is very clear about this.

[PAUSE]

Now night driving. This is a big topic and it is critically important.

Before you drive at night, check that all exterior lights work properly. Front and rear lights, brake lights, turn signals, low beams and high beams.

Make sure your windows and headlights are clean, inside and outside. Dirty windows add to glare. Dirty headlights reduce efficiency.

[PAUSE]

When driving at night, avoid using high beams in fog. They will reduce your own ability to see and may temporarily blind other drivers. Switch to low beams.

[EMPHASIS: low beams]

When you see the headlights of an oncoming vehicle, switch to low beams.

[PAUSE]

Adjust your rear-view mirror to avoid the reflection of other vehicles' headlights. Most cars have a day-night rear-view mirror that can be tilted easily to reduce glare.

Avoid using your vehicle's interior light while driving. If you need to check for something, safely pull over to the side of the road first.

[PAUSE]

Keep your eyes moving. Watch for flashes of light at the top of hills, at road bends, and at intersections. Those flashes may indicate the headlights of other cars.

Increase your following distance to make sure you have time to adjust your speed or brake as needed.

[PAUSE]

Night driving requires lots of concentration, which can be tiring. To prevent fatigue, take frequent breaks to give your eyes and mind a rest.

And remember, the GDL system requires ten hours of night driving practice. Many teen crashes occur at night because new drivers fail to make these adjustments.

## [9:00 - 10:30] Quick Review and Closing

Here is your quick review of the must-know facts.

[PAUSE]

Passing. Maintain interval, select gap, signal, mirrors, head check, accelerate. Return to lane when entire front is visible in rear-view mirror.

[PAUSE]

Speed limit. You CANNOT exceed it even when passing. This is a guaranteed test question.

[PAUSE]

Being passed. Maintain speed and lane. Slow if necessary to help. Do NOT speed up.

[PAUSE]

Following distance. Three to four seconds. Measure with a stationary object. Increase at night and in bad weather.

[PAUSE]

Night driving. Low beams in fog and with oncoming vehicles. Check all lights. Clean windows and headlights. Avoid interior lights while driving. Keep eyes moving. Watch for light flashes. Take breaks.

[PAUSE]

GDL requires ten hours of night driving practice.

[PAUSE]

You are making excellent progress. Replay this audio whenever you need a refresher. On test day, these facts will be second nature. You have got this.
