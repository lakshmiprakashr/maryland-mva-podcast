## Questions

**Q1. [Type: RECALL]** What is the minimum age for a Learner's Permit in Maryland?

**Answer:** 15 years and 9 months.

**Explanation:** Maryland's Graduated Licensing System sets the minimum age for a Learner's Permit at 15 years and 9 months. This is the first stage of the three-stage licensing process.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse this with the provisional license age (16 years, 6 months) or full license age (18 years).

**Common Mistake:** Mixing up the three minimum ages: 15y9m (permit), 16y6m (provisional), 18 (full).

---

**Q2. [Type: RECALL]** What is the minimum age for a Provisional License in Maryland?

**Answer:** 16 years and 6 months.

**Explanation:** The Provisional License is the second stage of Maryland's Graduated Licensing System, available at 16 years and 6 months. It comes with specific restrictions for drivers under 18.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse this with the Learner's Permit age (15 years, 9 months) or full license age (18 years).

**Common Mistake:** Assuming provisional license is available at 16 years old exactly.

---

**Q3. [Type: RECALL]** What is the minimum age for a full Driver License in Maryland?

**Answer:** 18 years.

**Explanation:** A full, unrestricted Driver License is available at age 18. This is the third and final stage of Maryland's Graduated Licensing System.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse this with the provisional license age (16 years, 6 months).

**Common Mistake:** Thinking the full license is available at 17 or 16.

---

**Q4. [Type: RECALL]** Under what circumstances must a provisional license holder have a cosigner?

**Answer:** If the provisional license holder is under 18, they must have a cosigner who can request MVA cancel the license for any reason.

**Explanation:** The cosigner requirement for provisional licenses only applies to drivers under 18. Once a driver turns 18, no cosigner is needed.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think all provisional license holders need a cosigner — only those under 18.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the cosigner requirement applies to all provisional license holders regardless of age.

---

**Q5. [Type: RECALL]** What is the nighttime restriction for provisional license holders under 18?

**Answer:** They may not drive between 12:00 AM and 5:00 AM unless for school, sports, job, or volunteer work.

**Explanation:** Maryland imposes a nighttime curfew on provisional license holders under 18. The only exceptions are for school, sports, employment, or volunteer activities.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think the curfew applies to all provisional license holders — only those under 18.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting the specific exceptions that allow nighttime driving.

---

**Q6. [Type: RECALL]** What is the passenger restriction for provisional license holders under 18?

**Answer:** For the first 151 days, they may not carry passengers other than immediate family members.

**Explanation:** During the first 151 days of holding a provisional license, drivers under 18 cannot carry non-family passengers. This restriction is designed to reduce distractions from peer passengers.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse 151 days with other time periods. It is specifically the first 151 days.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking the restriction applies for the entire provisional license period, not just the first 151 days.

---

**Q7. [Type: RECALL]** Can provisional license holders text while driving?

**Answer:** No — texting is prohibited at all license stages except for emergency 911 texts.

**Explanation:** Maryland's texting ban applies to all drivers regardless of license type. The only exception is for emergency 911 texts.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think the provisional license texting ban is different from other stages — it is the same across all stages.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming that texting becomes legal at some license stage. It never does (except 911).

---

**Q8. [Type: RECALL]** Can a provisional license holder under 18 use a cell phone while driving?

**Answer:** No — no cell phone use is allowed under the age of 18 except for emergency 911 calls.

**Explanation:** Drivers under 18 with a provisional license may not use a cell phone while driving in any capacity, except to make emergency 911 calls.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse cell phone rules with texting rules. Cell phones are banned for under-18 provisional holders; hands-free is allowed for 18+ provisional holders.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking hands-free phone use is allowed for all provisional license holders.

---

**Q9. [Type: APPLICATION]** A 17-year-old provisional license holder is driving home from a sports practice at 11 PM. Is this legal?

**Answer:** Yes — the nighttime restriction (12 AM to 5 AM) does not apply when driving for school, sports, job, or volunteer work. However, if driving home from a friend's house at 11 PM, it would still be legal since 11 PM is before the 12 AM curfew.

**Explanation:** The nighttime curfew runs from 12 AM to 5 AM. At 11 PM, the driver is still within legal driving hours. If it were after midnight, the sports exception would apply.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think the curfew starts at 11 PM — it starts at 12 AM (midnight).

**Common Mistake:** Confusing 11 PM with 12 AM when thinking about the curfew.

---

**Q10. [Type: APPLICATION]** A 16-year-old provisional license holder wants to drive two friends to a movie. They received their license 3 months ago. Is this legal?

**Answer:** No — for the first 151 days (approximately 5 months), they cannot carry non-family passengers. Three months is within the restriction period.

**Explanation:** The passenger restriction applies for the first 151 days of the provisional license. Three months is about 90 days, which is within the restriction period. The friends are not immediate family, so they cannot be passengers.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume 3 months is enough time for the restriction to expire — 151 days is about 5 months.

**Common Mistake:** Not counting the days from license issuance to determine if the restriction still applies.

---

**Q11. [Type: APPLICATION]** A provisional license holder who is 18 years old is pulled over while talking on a handheld cell phone. Will they receive a citation?

**Answer:** No — provisional license holders 18 and over may use hands-free devices. However, a handheld phone is not hands-free, so they could receive a citation.

**Explanation:** The law allows provisional license holders 18+ to use hands-free phone devices. A handheld phone is not hands-free, so using one would be a violation.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think all phone use is banned for provisional holders 18+ — hands-free is permitted.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing "no cell phone use under 18" with "hands-free allowed at 18+."

---

**Q12. [Type: APPLICATION]** What happens to a provisional license holder on their second offense?

**Answer:** 30 days of license suspension and 90 days of work/school restriction.

**Explanation:** The second offense carries both a license suspension (30 days) and a restriction that limits driving to work and school only (90 days). This escalates from the first offense, which only requires DIP class.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse second offense consequences with first (DIP class only) or third (180 days suspension + YDIP).

**Common Mistake:** Mixing up the consequences for different offense levels.

---

**Q13. [Type: APPLICATION]** At what point total does a Maryland driver face license suspension?

**Answer:** 8 points on the driving record.

**Explanation:** Maryland's point system triggers license suspension at 8 points, with possible additional penalties. This is separate from the offense-based consequences for provisional license holders.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse point-based suspension (8 points) with offense-based suspension (30 days for 2nd offense).

**Common Mistake:** Thinking the point system and the offense consequences are the same thing.

---

**Q14. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Compare the cosigner requirements for a Learner's Permit versus a Provisional License.

**Answer:** Learner's Permit always requires a cosigner. Provisional License requires a cosigner only if under 18.

**Explanation:** The Learner's Permit always requires a cosigner who can request MVA cancel for any reason. The Provisional License only requires a cosigner if the driver is under 18 — once they turn 18, no cosigner is needed.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think the cosigner requirements are the same for both license types.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming all provisional license holders need a cosigner regardless of age.

---

**Q15. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** How do the consequences escalate from first to fourth offense for provisional license holders?

**Answer:** 1st: DIP class. 2nd: 30-day suspension + 90-day restriction. 3rd: 180-day suspension + YDIP. 4th: 180-day revocation.

**Explanation:** Consequences escalate significantly with each offense. The first offense is educational (DIP class). The second adds suspension and restriction. The third doubles the suspension and adds the Young Driver Improvement Program. The fourth results in full revocation.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse suspension (temporary) with revocation (permanent loss). Revocation is more severe.

**Common Mistake:** Not knowing the specific consequences for each offense level.

---

**Q16. [Type: RECALL]** What must a supervising driver have to supervise practice sessions?

**Answer:** A valid license, must be at least 21 years old, and must have been licensed for at least 3 years.

**Explanation:** The supervising driver must meet all three criteria simultaneously: valid license, age 21+, and at least 3 years of licensure.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't forget any of the three requirements — all must be met.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming any licensed adult can supervise without checking the 3-year requirement.

---

**Q17. [Type: RECALL]** What is the practice hour requirement for drivers 25 and older?

**Answer:** 14 hours total, including 3 hours at night.

**Explanation:** Drivers 25 and older need 14 hours of supervised practice with 3 hours at night. These hours are in addition to the 6 hours from driver education.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't apply the under-25 requirement (60 hours) to this age group.

**Common Mistake:** Using the wrong age threshold when determining practice requirements.

---

**Q18. [Type: APPLICATION]** A driver accumulates 6 points on their Maryland driving record. What happens?

**Answer:** They are required to attend DIP (Driver Improvement Program) class.

**Explanation:** Maryland's point system requires DIP class at 5-7 points. At 6 points, this driver falls within that range and must complete the program.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse 5-7 points (DIP class) with 8 points (suspension) or 12 points (revocation).

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that any point accumulation results in suspension. Suspension starts at 8 points.

---

**Q19. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Why does Maryland's Graduated Licensing System have three stages instead of just one?

**Answer:** The three stages allow new drivers to gain experience progressively, with restrictions that reduce risk during the most dangerous learning period.

**Explanation:** Each stage of the GDL system provides progressively more freedom as the driver demonstrates competence and maturity. The Learner's Permit stage provides maximum supervision, the Provisional stage adds independence with restrictions, and the full license reflects demonstrated readiness.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think the stages are arbitrary — each serves a specific safety purpose.

**Common Mistake:** Not understanding the purpose behind the graduated system.

---

**Q20. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Compare the seat belt requirements across all three license stages.

**Answer:** Seat belt requirements are identical across all three stages — driver and all passengers must wear seat belts or be in age-appropriate car seats/boosters.

**Explanation:** Unlike other restrictions that change between stages, the seat belt requirement is constant. Every license stage requires the driver and all passengers to wear seat belts or use appropriate child safety seats.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume seat belt rules change between stages — they are the same for all.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking seat belt requirements become less strict with a full license.

## Quick Reference

- Minimum ages: 15y9m (permit), 16y6m (provisional), 18 (full)
- Cosigner: always (permit), under 18 only (provisional), none (full)
- Nighttime curfew: 12am-5am (provisional, under 18)
- Passenger restriction: 151 days, no non-family (provisional, under 18)
- Texting: banned at all stages (911 only)
- Cell phone: no use (permit), no under 18 / hands-free 18+ (provisional), hands-free (full)
- Consequences: 1st DIP, 2nd 30-day suspension, 3rd 180-day suspension + YDIP, 4th revocation
- Point thresholds: 5-7 DIP, 8 suspension, 12 revocation

**Test Day Tip:** When the MVA exam asks about restrictions, always identify the license type AND the driver's age. Many restrictions only apply if the driver is under 18 — knowing this eliminates wrong answers quickly.
