## [1:30] Section 1: Certification Requirements

The certification for rookie drivers is a legal document signed by a parent, guardian, mentor, or coach. It verifies that the new driver has completed the required practice hours.

[PAUSE]

If the new driver is under twenty-five, the certifier states that the driver has had a minimum of **sixty hours** of behind-the-wheel practice, including **ten hours** of nighttime driving. These hours are in addition to the **six hours** from driver education.

[PAUSE]

If the new driver is twenty-five or older, the certifier states that the driver has had a minimum of **fourteen hours** of practice, including **three hours** at night. Again, these are in addition to driver education hours.

[PAUSE]

The supervising driver must be a valid license holder who is at least **twenty-one years old** and has been licensed for at least **three years**.

[PAUSE]

Here is the critical detail. The certification is signed under **penalty of perjury**. It is considered part of the license application. Falsifying information can result in prosecution and the cancellation of the driver's license.

[PAUSE]

Memory trick. Think "perjury means prison." Okay, that is an exaggeration, but it emphasizes that this is a legal obligation, not just a formality.

## [4:30] Section 2: The Driver-Coach Agreement

The Driver-Coach Agreement sets ground rules for both the new driver and the coach. Let us start with the rookie driver's commitments.

[PAUSE]

The rookie driver agrees to drive only at specified times and on specified roads. They agree to **no texting or phone use** while driving. They agree to **always wear their safety belt** and make sure all passengers do too. They agree to **no alcohol or drugs** and drive. And they agree to **obey all provisional license restrictions**.

[PAUSE]

The agreement also includes consequences for violations. First offense, second offense, third offense. These escalating consequences are important to remember.

[PAUSE]

Now for the coach's commitments. The coach agrees to supervise the completion of sixty hours of supervised driving. They agree to be a **model driver** and follow all Maryland driving laws. They agree to be **supportive and provide constructive, helpful comments**. And they agree to set limits and enforce Maryland driving law.

[PAUSE]

Here is what the MVA exam might ask. The coach's role does not end when the new driver gets their provisional license. The manual explicitly states that coaching should continue. This includes periodic ride-alongs and updating the agreement as the driver gains experience.

## [8:00] Section 3: The License Comparison Chart

Now let us walk through the three stages of Maryland's Graduated Licensing System and how restrictions change at each stage.

[PAUSE]

**Learner's Permit.** Minimum age is fifteen years and nine months. Must have a cosigner who can request MVA cancel for any reason. No cell phone use except 911. No texting except 911. Seat belts required for all.

[PAUSE]

**Provisional License.** Minimum age is sixteen years and six months. If under eighteen, must have a cosigner. No cell phone use under eighteen except 911. Hands-free allowed if over eighteen. No texting except 911. Nighttime restriction: no driving between midnight and 5 AM if under eighteen, with exceptions for school, sports, job, and volunteer work. Passenger restriction: no non-family passengers for the first one hundred fifty-one days if under eighteen. Seat belts required for all.

[PAUSE]

**Full Driver License.** Minimum age is eighteen. No cosigner required. Hands-free phone allowed. No texting except 911. No nighttime restrictions. No passenger restrictions. Seat belts required for all.

[PAUSE]

Here is a key comparison. Texting is banned at **all three stages**. This is the one rule that never changes. The MVA exam may ask about this, and the answer is always no texting except for 911.

[PAUSE]

Another key comparison. Seat belt requirements are **identical** across all three stages. Driver and all passengers must wear seat belts or be in appropriate car seats. No exceptions, no changes.
