# Chapter 24 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

The License Comparison Chart displays the three stages of Maryland's Graduated Licensing System side by side: Learner's Permit, Provisional License, and full Driver License. Each stage has different minimum ages, cosigner requirements, phone/texting rules, nighttime restrictions, passenger restrictions, alcohol policies, and escalating consequences for violations. The MVA exam heavily tests these differences.

## Why It Matters

Every restriction and age threshold in this chart is testable. The MVA loves to ask "which license type allows..." or "what is the minimum age for..." questions. Knowing the exact boundaries of each stage is essential for passing the knowledge test and for legal compliance.

## High-Yield Facts

### Minimum Ages
- Learner's Permit: **15 years, 9 months**
- Provisional License: **16 years, 6 months**
- Full Driver License: **18 years**

### Cosigner Requirements
- Learner's Permit: **Must have** cosigner who can request MVA cancel for any reason
- Provisional License: Required **only if under 18** — cosigner can request MVA cancel for any reason
- Full License: **No cosigner** required

### Seat Belt Use
- **All three stages**: driver and all passengers must wear seat belts or be in age-appropriate car seats/boosters

### Cellphone Use
- Learner's Permit: **No cell phone use** except emergency 911 calls
- Provisional License: **No cell phone use under 18** except 911; may use **hands-free if over 18**
- Full License: May use **hands-free** device

### Texting
- **All three stages**: No texting except for emergency 911 texts

### Nighttime Restrictions (under 18)
- Provisional License: May not drive **12:00 AM to 5:00 AM** unless for school, sports, job, or volunteer work
- Full License: **No restrictions**

### Passenger Restrictions (under 18)
- Provisional License: For **first 151 days**, may not carry passengers other than **immediate family members**
- Full License: **No restrictions**

### Alcohol Restrictions
- Learner's Permit: **Zero tolerance**
- Provisional License: **Zero tolerance**
- Full License: Under 21 = **zero tolerance**; 21+ = .07 BAC = DWI, .08+ = DUI

### Consequences for Under 18 (Learner's Permit & Provisional)
- 1st offense: **DIP Class**
- 2nd offense: **30 days suspension** + **90 days work/school restriction**
- 3rd offense: **180 days suspension/revocation** + **180 days restriction** + Young Driver Improvement Program
- 4th offense: **180 day revocation**

### Point System (Full License)
- 5-7 points: **DIP class**
- 8 points: **Suspension** (possible additional penalties)
- 12 points: **Revocation** (possible additional penalties)

## MVA Test Traps

- **Trap:** Thinking texting is allowed at any stage. Texting is banned at ALL stages — only 911 texts are permitted.
- **Trap:** Confusing cellphone and texting rules. Cellphone use has age-based exceptions for provisional holders (hands-free at 18+); texting does not.
- **Trap:** Believing nighttime restrictions apply to all provisional license holders. They only apply if **under 18**.
- **Trap:** Thinking the cosigner requirement exists for provisional license holders over 18. It only applies if **under 18**.
- **Trap:** Confusing the passenger restriction period. It is the **first 151 days** of the provisional license, not the entire provisional period.

## Common Mistakes

- Mixing up minimum ages: 15 years 9 months (permit), 16 years 6 months (provisional), 18 years (full)
- Forgetting that seat belt rules are identical across all three stages
- Assuming hands-free phone use is allowed for all provisional license holders — only those **18+**
- Not knowing the escalating consequences: DIP → suspension → revocation

## Memory Tricks

- **"15-9, 16-6, 18"** — the three minimum ages in order
- **"No text ever"** — texting ban applies to all license types
- **"151 days of family"** — first 151 days, only family passengers allowed
- **"12 to 5"** — nighttime curfew (under 18)
- **"5-7-8-12"** — point thresholds: DIP at 5-7, suspension at 8, revocation at 12

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- Graduated Licensing System: Learner's Permit → Provisional License → Full License
- Minimum ages: 15y9m (permit), 16y6m (provisional), 18 (full)
- Cosigner can request MVA cancel permit/license for any reason
- Nighttime restriction: 12am-5am (provisional, under 18)
- Passenger restriction: 151 days, no non-family (provisional, under 18)
- Point system: 5-7 = DIP, 8 = suspension, 12 = revocation
- Under 21: zero tolerance for alcohol at all license stages
