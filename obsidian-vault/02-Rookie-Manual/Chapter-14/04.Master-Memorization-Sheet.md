# Chapter 14 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Expressway driving is the most demanding environment for new drivers. This chapter covers entering the expressway via ramps and cross streets, maintaining proper lanes and following distance, exiting safely, using I.P.D.E. for hazard perception, and practicing night driving including high beam and low beam rules.

## Why It Matters

Expressway crashes are often severe due to high speeds. New drivers must master the specific skills of merging, lane management, and exiting before driving independently. Night driving doubles the risk of fatal crashes for teens.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Entrance ramp:** Accelerate to highway speed, signal, select safe gap, merge into closest lane
- **Following distance:** minimum 3 to 4 seconds; increase in bad weather or at night
- **Lane changes on expressway:** signal, check mirrors, perform head checks
- **Exit procedure:** identify exit early, signal, use mirrors and head checks, use deceleration lane, adjust speed after exiting
- **Cross street entry:** stop completely unless entering on green signal, signal direction, select large gap, accelerate into proper lane
- **I.P.D.E.:** Identify, Predict, Decide, Execute
- **Night driving:** check all lights, clean windows and headlights, low beams in fog and with oncoming traffic, increase following distance, take breaks to prevent fatigue
- **GDL requires 10 hours of night driving practice**

## MVA Test Traps

- **Entering too slowly on the ramp:** You must accelerate to highway speed on the ramp, not enter slowly
- **Following distance:** 3 to 4 seconds, not 2 seconds
- **Deceleration lane is not optional:** You must use it to exit safely
- **Cross street stop:** A stop sign at a highway cross street requires a complete stop, even if you can see traffic
- **High beams in fog:** They reduce visibility instead of improving it
- **Day/night mirror:** Most cars have a day/night rear-view mirror that tilts to reduce glare

## Common Mistakes

- Not accelerating enough on the entrance ramp
- Forgetting to signal when exiting the expressway
- Not performing head checks before lane changes on the expressway
- Using high beams in fog
- Not cleaning windows and headlights before night driving
- Driving while fatigued at night without taking breaks
- Not adjusting following distance for night or bad weather

## Memory Tricks

- **"Ramp Up"** — accelerate on the entrance ramp
- **"3 to 4 on the floor"** — 3 to 4 seconds following distance
- **"Signal, Mirrors, Head, Steer"** — the four steps for any lane change on the expressway
- **"Low in Fog"** — use low beams in fog, not high beams
- **"I-P-D-E, before you see"** — use I.P.D.E. before you encounter a hazard

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

- **Expressway entrance sign** — green with highway number and direction
- **Expressway exit sign** — green with exit number and destination name
- **Merge warning sign** — yellow diamond indicating lanes will merge
- **Lane ends warning sign** — yellow diamond
- **Advisory speed sign** — yellow with recommended speed for exits or curves
- **Mile marker signs** — green with white numbers, used for exit identification

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **Expressway Following Distance:** Minimum 3 to 4 seconds under ideal conditions (MD Driver's Manual)
- **Night Driving Practice:** GDL requires minimum 10 hours of supervised night driving
- **High Beam Usage:** Must switch to low beams when approaching oncoming vehicles and in fog
- **Cross Street Highway Entry:** Must stop completely before entering unless on a green signal
- **I.P.D.E. System:** Required hazard perception framework in the Maryland Driver's Manual
