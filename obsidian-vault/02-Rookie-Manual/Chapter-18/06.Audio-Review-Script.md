# Chapter 18 - Audio Review Script

## [0:00] Introduction

Welcome to your audio review for Maryland Driver's Manual chapter eighteen, covering **parking skills** and what happens when you are pulled over by a police officer. [PAUSE]

This chapter covers two very practical topics. First, the step-by-step procedures for parallel and angle parking. Second, your rights and responsibilities during a traffic stop, including the consequences of citations on your provisional license. [PAUSE]

Let us start with the skill that trips up most new drivers. [LONG PAUSE]

## [1:30] Section 1: Parallel Parking

Parallel parking is one of the most tested skills on the Maryland MVA Skills Test. Let me walk you through it step by step. [PAUSE]

First, **signal your intentions**. Then stop your vehicle parallel to the car you are parking behind. Your front seat should be in line with that vehicle, and you need **two to three feet** between your vehicles. [PAUSE]

With your foot on the brake, shift to reverse. Now here is critical. Check traffic in **all directions**. That means behind you, in front of you, and in your mirrors. You must yield to any pedestrians, bicyclists, or other vehicles. [PAUSE]

Back slowly and turn the wheel sharply in the appropriate direction. Continue backing until you are in line with the rear bumper of the car behind you. [PAUSE]

Then turn the steering wheel the **opposite** direction and stop before you touch that car's bumper. Shift to drive and adjust your vehicle in the space. [PAUSE]

The key measurement to remember. Your vehicle must end up no more than **twelve inches** from the curb. [EMPHASIS: twelve inches] That is the number the MVA test will check. [LONG PAUSE]

## [4:00] Section 2: Angle Parking and Exiting

Angle parking is simpler than parallel parking, but it still requires following the correct steps. [PAUSE]

To enter an angle parking space, identify the space, **signal your intentions**, and move slowly while turning into the space. Center your vehicle and pull forward to the front of the space. Then secure your vehicle. [PAUSE]

To exit an angle parking space, check for traffic in **all directions**. This is where most parking lot fender benders happen. Back up carefully. Turn the steering wheel in the direction you want to travel. When your car clears the parking area, stop and shift to drive. [PAUSE]

For exiting a parallel parking space, check for traffic and signal. Press the brake, shift to reverse, and back close to the vehicle behind you without hitting its bumper. Then shift to drive, turn the wheel toward your travel direction, and move forward carefully. Always check your front bumper so you do not hit the car in front of you. [LONG PAUSE]

## [6:30] Section 3: Traffic Stops and Citations

Now let us talk about what happens when a police officer pulls you over. This is important for every driver, but especially for provisional license holders. [PAUSE]

When you see those lights behind you, **pull off to the side of the roadway** as far away from traffic as possible. Turn off your engine, your radio, and any other device. [PAUSE]

**Stay in your vehicle** and keep your seatbelt fastened. Keep your hands visible at all times, preferably on the steering wheel. Do not reach for your wallet or documents until the officer asks. [PAUSE]

If the officer issues you a citation, **sign it**. Do not argue with the officer. Here is the critical fact. Signing a citation is **not** an admission of guilt. You are simply acknowledging that you received it. You can contest it later in court. [EMPHASIS: not an admission of guilt] [LONG PAUSE]

## [9:00] Section 4: Consequences and Insurance

What happens after you get a citation depends on your license status. If you are holding a **provisional driver's license** and you are convicted of a moving violation, or if you receive a Probation Before Judgment, you will be required to **restart your eighteen-month waiting period**. [EMPHASIS: restart] [PAUSE]

You will also be required to attend the **Driver Improvement Program**. This program reviews safe driving concepts. If you fail to attend, your license will be suspended. [PAUSE]

Whether you hold a learner's permit or a provisional license, the Driver Improvement Program is mandatory for any violation. [PAUSE]

Now let us talk about insurance. Since October first, 2016, all Maryland drivers must have proof of insurance with them at all times while driving. This proof can be in electronic format, like on your phone. You must present it on request of a law enforcement officer. Failure to comply may result in fines. [LONG PAUSE]

Parallel parking setup: two to three feet between vehicles. [PAUSE]

Parallel parking final position: twelve inches maximum from the curb. [PAUSE]

Traffic stop: pull over, engine off, stay in car, hands on steering wheel. [PAUSE]

Signing a citation is NOT admitting guilt. [PAUSE]

Provisional license violation: restart the eighteen-month waiting period. [PAUSE]

Driver Improvement Program is mandatory for violations. [PAUSE]

Proof of insurance required at all times since October 2016. [PAUSE]

Electronic insurance proof is legally accepted. [LONG PAUSE]

## [12:30] Closing

You have just reviewed two practical topics that every Maryland driver needs to master. Parking skills protect your vehicle and others in the lot. Knowing what to do during a traffic stop keeps you safe and protects your driving privilege. [PAUSE]

Remember, more than 60 people are killed each year in Maryland crashes involving young drivers. Every rule in this manual exists to bring that number down. Your safe driving habits matter. [PAUSE]
