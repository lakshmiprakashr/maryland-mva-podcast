## [1:00 - 4:30] Using the Shared Left Turn Lane

The shared left turn lane is that lane in the middle of the road with solid yellow lines on both sides and dashed yellow lines inside. You see them on busy roads all the time.

[PAUSE]

Here is the most important rule. You can ONLY use this lane for turning left into or out of driveways, parking lots, and for entering traffic. You cannot drive in it. You cannot use it to pass anyone.

[PAUSE]

Now let us talk timing. Signal three to four seconds before you move into the shared left turn lane. Then enter the lane no more than two to three seconds before your turn.

[PAUSE]

If you are turning into a driveway, here is the sequence. Check traffic, signal left, check for drivers on both sides, enter the lane, wait for a safe gap in oncoming traffic, and complete your turn.

[PAUSE]

If you are entering traffic from a driveway, it is more complex. Signal left, stop at the edge of the roadway. Check for drivers on the opposite side who are also signaling left. Check for a gap to the left.

If traffic is clear in both directions, enter the first through lane to the left. But if the gap is only to the left and not to the right, move into the shared turn lane, stop, turn on your RIGHT signal, recheck oncoming traffic, and when it clears, accelerate into the nearest lane.

[PAUSE]

The MVA exam loves to test the signal switch. You start with a left signal to enter the shared lane, then switch to a right signal to merge into traffic. Remember that sequence.

## [4:30 - 8:00] Roundabouts

Roundabouts are becoming more common in Maryland. They are sometimes called traffic circles, but they work differently from old-style traffic circles.

[PAUSE]

A roundabout lets vehicles move more efficiently than a traffic light. Here is how to use one.

First, slow down as you approach. Obey all traffic signs and pavement markings.

[PAUSE]

Second, yield to pedestrians and bicyclists when entering and exiting. This is a point students often miss. You yield both ways.

Third, and this is the big one on the test. Yield to traffic on your LEFT that is already in the roundabout. You do NOT yield to the right.

[EMPHASIS: left]

[PAUSE]

Keep your speed low while you are in the roundabout. And here is the exit rule. As you approach your exit, turn your right turn signal on. This tells drivers behind you that you are leaving.

[PAUSE]

So to recap. Yield to pedestrians entering and exiting. Yield to traffic from the left. Keep speed low. Signal right at your exit.

[PAUSE]

The exam will almost certainly ask about the yielding direction. Remember, left yield, round, exit right. That is your mantra.

## [8:00 - 11:00] Bicyclists, Multiple Turn Lanes, and Moving Over

Maryland law requires you to maintain a three-foot space cushion between you and a bicyclist on the road. Some Maryland roads have bicycle lanes, but many do not. The three-foot rule applies regardless.

[PAUSE]

When you approach an intersection with multiple turn lanes, identify the correct lane one to two blocks ahead. Signal your intentions. Check the traffic control devices, oncoming traffic, and cross traffic. Then, when legal and safe, turn into the corresponding lane.

[PAUSE]

Stay alert for pedestrians crossing the street and for oncoming vehicles. And remember, trucks and buses need more room to turn. Give them extra space.

[PAUSE]

Before you move on to the next set of skills, the Coach's Checklist requires you to safely navigate complex intersections, properly use the shared left turn lane for both entering and exiting traffic, change lanes safely, obey all traffic signs and signals, observe all posted speed limits, and feel comfortable on large busy roads.

[PAUSE]

If you cannot answer yes to all of those, do not move on. Stop and practice more. Mastering these skills now will make you a safer driver and help you pass your Maryland knowledge test.

## [11:00 - 12:30] Quick Review and Closing

Here is your quick review.

[PAUSE]

Shared left turn lane. Signal three to four seconds before. Enter two to three seconds before your turn. Do not drive in it.

[PAUSE]

Entering traffic from shared lane. Left signal, stop, check, switch to right signal, recheck, merge.

[PAUSE]

Roundabouts. Yield to pedestrians entering and exiting. Yield to traffic from your left. Keep speed low. Signal right at your exit.

[PAUSE]

Bicyclists. Three feet minimum. Always. Even without a bicycle lane.

[PAUSE]

Multiple turn lanes. Identify your lane one to two blocks ahead.
