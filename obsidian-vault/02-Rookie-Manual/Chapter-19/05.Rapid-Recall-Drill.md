# Chapter 19 - Rapid Recall Drill

## Instructions
Answer all 20 questions. Check answers after completing. Target time: 25-30 minutes.

## Questions

**Q1. [Type: RECALL]** What should you do immediately when a police officer signals you to pull over?

**Answer:** Pull off to the side of the roadway as far away from traffic as possible.

**Explanation:** Getting as far off the road as you can protects both you and the officer from passing traffic. This is the first and most critical step in a traffic stop.

**MVA Test Trap:** Pulling over to the nearest spot without considering distance from traffic — "as far away from traffic as possible" is the key phrase.

**Common Mistake:** Stopping in the travel lane or only partially off the road.

---

**Q2. [Type: RECALL]** What must you do with your engine, radio, and other devices during a traffic stop?

**Answer:** Turn them all off.

**Explanation:** Turning off the engine, radio, and other devices eliminates distractions, reduces noise so you can hear the officer, and shows cooperation.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking you only need to turn off the engine — the manual specifically includes radio and other devices.

**Common Mistake:** Leaving the music playing, which makes communication difficult.

---

**Q3. [Type: RECALL]** Where should you keep your hands during a traffic stop?

**Answer:** Visible at all times, preferably on the steering wheel.

**Explanation:** Keeping your hands visible on the steering wheel reduces the officer's concern about potential threats and demonstrates cooperation.

**MVA Test Trap:** Reaching for your wallet or documents immediately — wait until the officer asks.

**Common Mistake:** Putting hands in your lap or reaching for the glove compartment without being asked.

---

**Q4. [Type: RECALL]** What are the six situations where you must always contact law enforcement after a crash?

**Answer:** Someone has been injured, a vehicle cannot be moved, a driver appears to be under the influence, a driver does not have a license, a driver tries to leave the scene without giving proper information, or public property has been damaged.

**Explanation:** These situations all require official intervention for medical, legal, or documentation purposes. They go beyond what drivers can handle privately.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking you only need to call police for injury crashes — the other five situations also require law enforcement.

**Common Mistake:** Trying to handle a situation privately when law involvement is legally required.

---

**Q5. [Type: RECALL]** What is the correct procedure when you are in a crash where someone is injured?

**Answer:** Immediately contact 911 and stay at the scene until help arrives. Do not move vehicles.

**Explanation:** Injured persons may have spinal or internal injuries that could be worsened by movement. Emergency responders need the scene preserved and must handle any movement of injured persons.

**MVA Test Trap:** Trying to move injured people or vehicles — this can worsen injuries and is against the manual's instructions.

**Common Mistake:** Leaving the scene before emergency responders arrive.

---

**Q6. [Type: RECALL]** When you are in a crash with no injuries and your vehicle cannot be moved, what steps should you take?

**Answer:** Contact 911 and seek assistance, move to a safe location away from traffic, and use emergency flashers or flares to warn other motorists.

**Explanation:** Even without injuries, a disabled vehicle on the road is a hazard. Warning other drivers prevents secondary crashes.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking you only need to call a tow truck — 911 should be contacted first for safety coordination.

**Common Mistake:** Standing near the disabled vehicle instead of moving to a safe location.

---

**Q7. [Type: RECALL]** What information must you collect after a crash with no injuries where both vehicles can move?

**Answer:** Name, address, phone number, insurance information, vehicle license plate number, make, model, and year of the other vehicle, and names and contact information of any witnesses.

**Explanation:** This complete information set is essential for insurance claims and any legal proceedings. Missing any piece can complicate the claims process.

**MVA Test Trap:** Only getting the license plate number and forgetting insurance and witness information.

**Common Mistake:** Not getting witness information, which can be critical if there is a dispute about fault.

---

**Q8. [Type: RECALL]** If you strike and injure a domestic animal, what are you required to do?

**Answer:** Notify law enforcement.

**Explanation:** Maryland law requires notification to law enforcement when you strike and injure a domestic animal. This is similar to the requirement for hitting an unattended vehicle.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking you only need to find the owner — law enforcement notification is also required.

**Common Mistake:** Driving away because "it was just an animal" — this is a legal violation.

---

**Q9. [Type: RECALL]** If you strike an unattended vehicle or property and cannot find the owner, what must you do?

**Answer:** Leave your contact information in a secure place and notify your insurance company of the incident.

**Explanation:** The manual requires you to attempt to find the owner first. If you cannot, leaving your contact information ensures the owner can reach you. Notifying your insurance company initiates the claims process.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking you can just drive away if you cannot find the owner — leaving contact information is required.

**Common Mistake:** Leaving contact information in an insecure location where it could fall or blow away.

---

**Q10. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are pulled over by a police officer. You believe you were driving correctly and the stop is unfair. What should you do?

**Answer:** Pull over safely, turn off engine and radio, stay in the vehicle with seatbelt on, keep hands on the steering wheel, and cooperate fully. If issued a citation, sign it without arguing. You can contest it in court later.

**Explanation:** The roadside is not the place to argue your case. Cooperating keeps you safe and preserves your right to contest the citation through proper legal channels.

**MVA Test Trap:** Refusing to sign the citation or arguing with the officer — this can lead to additional charges.

**Common Mistake:** Becoming confrontational instead of remaining calm and respectful.

---

**Q11. [Type: APPLICATION]** You witness a minor fender bender where both cars can move and no one appears injured. The drivers exchange information but neither calls the police. Is this appropriate?

**Answer:** Yes, for a minor crash with no injuries where both vehicles can move and all parties cooperate, calling law enforcement is not required. The drivers should move vehicles out of the travel lane and exchange the required information: name, address, phone, insurance, plate number, and vehicle details.

**Explanation:** The manual specifies when law enforcement must be called. Simple fender benders with cooperative parties and no injuries fall outside those mandatory reporting requirements.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking every crash requires a police report — only the six specific situations listed require law enforcement.

**Common Mistake:** Not moving vehicles out of the travel lane, creating a traffic hazard.

---

**Q12. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are in a crash and the other driver appears to be impaired. What should you do?

**Answer:** Contact 911 immediately. Stay at the scene. Do not let the other driver leave. The manual lists a driver appearing to be under the influence as one of the situations requiring law enforcement contact.

**Explanation:** Impaired driving is a criminal offense. Contacting 911 ensures law enforcement can respond, test the driver, and prevent them from driving while impaired.

**MVA Test Trap:** Trying to handle it privately because "nobody is hurt" — impaired driving always requires law enforcement.

**Common Mistake:** Letting the impaired driver leave the scene before police arrive.

---

**Q13. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are a provisional license holder and you get a speeding ticket. After paying the fine, you assume the matter is resolved. What have you overlooked?

**Answer:** You may still need to attend the Driver Improvement Program, which is mandatory for provisional and permit holders convicted of violations. Failing to attend results in license suspension. Additionally, the conviction restarts your eighteen-month provisional waiting period.

**Explanation:** Paying the fine does not resolve all consequences. The Driver Improvement Program and the provisional period restart are separate requirements triggered by the conviction.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking paying the fine is the only consequence — for provisional holders, the procedural consequences are more significant.

**Common Mistake:** Not checking with the MVA about required follow-up actions after a citation.

---

**Q14. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are driving and hit a parked car in a lot. No one is around. What should you do?

**Answer:** Attempt to find the owner of the vehicle and provide your contact information. If you cannot locate the owner, leave your contact information in a secure place on the vehicle. Then notify your insurance company of the incident.

**Explanation:** Maryland law requires you to make a good-faith effort to find the owner. Leaving your information is mandatory if you cannot find them. Driving away without leaving any information is a hit-and-run.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking you can leave because "it is just a scratch" — any contact with an unattended vehicle requires action.

**Common Mistake:** Not leaving contact information because you think the damage is minor.

---

**Q15. [Type: APPLICATION]** After a crash where your car is drivable and no one is hurt, you need to move your vehicle. What should you do first?

**Answer:** Move the vehicle out of the travel portion of the road to prevent blocking traffic and causing secondary crashes. Then begin collecting the required information from the other driver.

**Explanation:** The manual specifically states to move out of the travel portion of the road. Blocking traffic after a minor crash creates additional hazards.

**MVA Test Trap:** Leaving vehicles in the road while exchanging information — move first, then collect info.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to activate hazard flashers before moving the vehicle.

---

**Q16. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are asked to provide proof of insurance during a traffic stop. Your proof is on your phone. The officer says they need a physical card. What are your rights?

**Answer:** Maryland law since October 1, 2016 explicitly allows electronic proof of insurance. You have the legal right to present proof on your phone. You may respectfully inform the officer that electronic proof is accepted under Maryland law.

**Explanation:** The law is clear that insurance identification cards may be in electronic format. Officers should accept electronic proof, though some may be unfamiliar with the provision.

**MVA Test Trap:** Not knowing your rights regarding electronic proof — the MVA test may ask about this.

**Common Mistake:** Panicking and not being able to locate your electronic proof because you never set it up on your phone.

---

**Q17. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Compare the three different crash scenarios covered in the manual: injuries, no injuries with immovable vehicle, and no injuries with movable vehicle. What is the key decision point that determines which procedure to follow?

**Answer:** The key decision point is whether anyone is injured. If yes: call 911, stay at scene, do not move vehicles. If no injuries, the next question is whether the vehicle can move. If it cannot: call 911, move to safety, use flashers/flares. If it can: move out of travel lane and collect information. The decision tree branches based on injury status first, then vehicle mobility.

**Explanation:** The manual presents a clear decision tree: injuries first, then mobility. Each branch has distinct actions appropriate to the situation's severity.

**MVA Test Trap:** Treating all crashes the same — the procedures differ significantly based on these two factors.

**Common Mistake:** Not following the correct branch of the decision tree because of stress or confusion.

---

**Q18. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Explain the chain of events that occurs when a provisional license holder receives a moving violation, from the moment of the citation through all consequences.

**Answer:** The chain is: (1) officer issues citation and driver signs it (not admitting guilt), (2) driver is convicted or receives PBJ in court, (3) the eighteen-month provisional waiting period restarts from zero, (4) driver must attend Driver Improvement Program, (5) if driver fails to attend, license is suspended. Each step leads to the next and the consequences compound.

**Explanation:** The Maryland GDL system creates escalating consequences for violations. The purpose is to encourage safe driving by making the cost of violations very high for provisional drivers.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking only the court fine matters — the procedural consequences are more impactful for provisional holders.

**Common Mistake:** Not understanding that PBJ has the same GDL consequences as a conviction.

---

**Q19. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Why does the manual include a section about striking domestic animals and unattended property alongside crash reporting procedures?

**Answer:** These situations are included because they share the same legal principle: when you cause damage or injury, you have a legal obligation to take responsibility. Striking a domestic animal requires law enforcement notification. Striking unattended property requires attempting to find the owner or leaving your contact information. Both mirror the general crash reporting requirement of accountability and documentation.

**Explanation:** The manual groups these situations to teach the broader principle that any incident involving damage or injury to others requires responsible action and documentation.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking these are separate, unrelated rules — they all stem from the same legal principle.

**Common Mistake:** Not recognizing that hitting a parked car or a pet requires the same kind of responsible follow-up as any other crash.

---

**Q20. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Compare the information requirements for a crash with injuries versus a crash without injuries. What information is collected in each case, and why is the approach different?

**Answer:** For crashes with injuries: call 911, stay at scene, do not move vehicles. Information collection is handled by law enforcement and emergency responders. For crashes without injuries where vehicles can move: you personally collect name, address, phone, insurance, plate, make/model/year, and witness information. The approach differs because injury crashes require professional medical and legal response, while minor crashes put the documentation responsibility on the drivers.

**Explanation:** The manual distinguishes between scenarios requiring emergency services and those that drivers can manage. This ensures appropriate resources are deployed and documentation is complete.

**MVA Test Trap:** Trying to collect the same information during an injury crash that you would for a minor fender bender — in injury crashes, wait for authorities.

**Common Mistake:** Not understanding that the severity of the crash determines who handles documentation.

## Quick Reference

- **Traffic stop:** Pull over, engine off, stay in car, hands visible, sign citation
- **Signing citation:** NOT an admission of guilt
- **Provisional violation:** Restart 18-month waiting period
- **Driver Improvement:** Mandatory for violations; non-attendance = suspension
- **Insurance:** Required at all times since October 2016; electronic OK
- **Call 911 for six situations:** Injuries, immovable vehicle, DUI, no license, hit-and-run, public property damage
- **Injury crash:** Stay at scene, do not move vehicles
- **No injuries, can move:** Move out of travel lane, collect info
- **Info to collect:** Name, address, phone, insurance, plate, make/model/year, witnesses
- **Domestic animal strike:** Notify law enforcement
- **Unattended property:** Find owner or leave contact info, notify insurance

**Test Day Tip:** Memorize the six situations requiring law enforcement contact — they appear frequently on the Maryland knowledge test and are easy to confuse with situations that do not require police.
