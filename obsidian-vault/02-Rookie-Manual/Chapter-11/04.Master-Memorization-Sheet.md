# Chapter 11 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Changing lanes safely is one of the most common driving maneuvers and one of the top crash causes for new drivers. This chapter covers the seven-step lane change process, risk management, using shared left turn lanes, multiple turn lanes, and the Move Over law.

## Why It Matters

Lane change crashes are among the most preventable types of collisions. New drivers frequently fail to check blind spots, signal properly, or maintain a space cushion. Mastering these steps is critical for both the Maryland knowledge test and safe daily driving.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Seven-step lane change process:** Maintain space cushion, check all directions, select safe gap, signal intentions, recheck toward lane change direction, adjust speed and steer smoothly, cancel signal once established
- **Signal 3 to 4 seconds** before making a lane change or turn
- **Risk is constant** — anticipate hazards like children, sudden stops, distracted drivers
- **Shared left turn lane:** Enter no more than 2 to 3 seconds before your turn
- **Three-foot rule:** Maintain at least 3 feet of space when passing bicyclists
- **Move Over law:** Change lanes OR slow down for emergency vehicles, tow trucks, and service vehicles stopped on the shoulder
- **School buses:** STOP if no divider exists between you and a school bus with lights activated
- **Multiple turn lanes:** Identify your lane one to two blocks in advance

## MVA Test Traps

- **Confusing Move Over with Stop:** You must move over if possible; only if you cannot move over should you slow down. You do NOT always stop.
- **School bus vs. divider rule:** Many students forget that you only stop if there is NO divider between you and the bus.
- **Shared left turn lane timing:** Students often enter too early. The rule is no more than 2 to 3 seconds before the turn.
- **Signal cancellation:** Forgetting to cancel the turn signal after completing the lane change can confuse other drivers.

## Common Mistakes

- Only checking the mirror and not performing a head check for blind spots
- Not adjusting speed to match the new lane before merging
- Signaling too late or not at all
- Failing to recheck in the direction of the lane change before moving
- Entering the shared left turn lane too early or using it as a driving lane

## Memory Tricks

- **S-C-R-O-S-S-C:** Space, Check, Recheck, Own speed, Steer, Signal, Cancel — the seven steps of a lane change
- **"No Divider, You Decide"** — STOP for school buses only when there is no divider
- **"2 to 3 for me"** — enter the shared left turn lane 2 to 3 seconds before your turn
- **"3 feet on the street"** — maintain 3 feet when passing a bicyclist

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

- **Yellow diamond warning signs** for lane ends, merge areas, and shared turn lanes
- **White lane-use arrows** indicating mandatory direction
- **Broken white lines** — lane changes permitted
- **Solid white lines** — lane changes discouraged or prohibited
- **Two-way left turn lane markings** — solid yellow lines on both sides with dashed yellow inside

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **Move Over Law (MD Vehicle Law 21-706):** Drivers must move over one lane or slow down for stopped emergency, tow, or service vehicles
- **School Bus Stop Law:** Must stop when no physical divider separates you from a bus with flashing red lights
- **Three-Foot Passing Law:** Minimum 3 feet clearance when overtaking a bicycle
- **Signal Requirement:** Must signal at least 100 feet before a turn or lane change on highways; 2 to 3 seconds in urban areas
