# Chapter-02 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Chapter 2 covers getting started with driving education. It focuses on the coach's responsibilities, how to choose a driving school, and the key laws every new driver and coach must know. This chapter establishes the foundation for safe driving habits.

## Why It Matters

The MVA tests your knowledge of coach responsibilities and driver education requirements. Understanding these concepts is critical for both the knowledge test and for safe practice driving.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Coach tips**: Take learning seriously but have fun, research driving schools, attend Unit One orientation, review what driver is learning, schedule practice times
- **Know updated laws**: Texting and cell phone laws (Section VI), seatbelt laws (Section X), moving over for emergency vehicles, sharing the road
- **Driving school selection**: Must be MVA-licensed, check policies, cost, refund policy, Unit One schedule
- **Driver education requirements**: 30 hours classroom + 6 hours behind-the-wheel training

## MVA Test Traps

- **"Licensed school" trap**: Only MVA-licensed schools can certify completion of driver education. Don't assume any driving school is acceptable.
- **"30 and 6" trap**: 30 hours classroom, 6 hours behind-the-wheel. Don't swap these numbers.
- **"Section VI and X" trap**: Texting/cell phone laws are in Section VI; seatbelt laws are in Section X. The MVA may ask which section covers which topic.

## Common Mistakes

- Not researching driving schools before enrolling
- Skipping the Unit One orientation session
- Failing to review what the new driver is learning in each class
- Not discussing driving skills before starting practice

## Memory Tricks

- **"30 and 6"** — 30 classroom, 6 behind-the-wheel (rhymes with "thirty and six")
- **"Unit One is orientation"** — Unit One is the first step, orientation for coaches and new drivers
- **"Section VI = phones, Section X = seatbelts"** — VI looks like a phone, X looks like a seatbelt buckle

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

No road signs or signals are covered in this chapter. Focus on driver education requirements and coach responsibilities.

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **Texting and Cell Phone Laws**: Covered in Section VI of the Maryland Driver's Manual
- **Seatbelt Laws**: Covered in Section X of the Maryland Driver's Manual
- **Moving Over Law**: Must move over for emergency vehicles
- **Sharing the Road**: Laws about sharing the road with cyclists, pedestrians, and other vehicles
