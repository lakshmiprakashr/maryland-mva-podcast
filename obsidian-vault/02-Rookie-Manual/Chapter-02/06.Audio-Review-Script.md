## [1:00 - 3:30] Coach Responsibilities

If you are helping a Rookie Driver complete their required practice time, you are a **coach**.

[PAUSE]

Here are some simple tips to make learning to drive a better experience.

[PAUSE]

First, **take learning to drive seriously but try to have fun**.

[PAUSE]

Do your research and locate the best driving school for you and your new driver.

[PAUSE]

Attend the **Orientation session for Driver Education, also known as Unit One**. This shows your new driver how important driver education is.

[PAUSE]

Review and discuss what your new driver is learning in each class as they progress through the course.

[PAUSE]

Schedule times and prepare routes before beginning to practice with your new driver.

[PAUSE]

The MVA exam loves to ask about coach responsibilities. Remember: research, attend orientation, review lessons, schedule practice.

[PAUSE]

## [3:30 - 6:00] Key Laws to Know

As a coach, you must know the latest laws. Let us cover the most important ones.

[PAUSE]

First, **texting and cell phone use laws**. These are covered in **Section Six** of the Maryland Driver's Manual. Both you and your new driver must follow these laws.

[PAUSE]

Second, **seatbelt laws**. These are in **Section Ten** of the Maryland Driver's Manual. Remember: everybody must wear a seatbelt in Maryland, no matter where they are seated.

[PAUSE]

Third, the **moving over law**. You must move over for emergency vehicles on the side of the road.

[PAUSE]

Fourth, **sharing the road** laws. This includes sharing with cyclists, pedestrians, and other vehicles.

[PAUSE]

For more information and to obtain a copy of the Maryland Driver's Manual, visit **www dot mva dot maryland dot gov**.

[PAUSE]

## [6:00 - 8:30] Choosing a Driving School

How do you choose a driving school for your Rookie Driver?

[PAUSE]

First and most important: **Make sure the school is licensed by the Maryland Motor Vehicle Administration**. Only licensed schools can certify completion of the driver education program.

[PAUSE]

A complete listing of driving schools in your area can be found at **www dot mva dot maryland dot gov**.

[PAUSE]

Ask other parents about their experiences with driving schools. Check with several schools to see which ones have a convenient location and schedule.

[PAUSE]

Remember your new driver must attend **thirty hours of classroom** and **six hours of behind the wheel training**.

[PAUSE]

Check with the driving school about their policies. Ask about the Unit One Orientation schedule. How much does driver education cost? Is a payment plan available? What is the refund policy if your new driver is unable to finish?

[PAUSE]

The MVA exam loves to ask about these specific numbers: thirty hours classroom, six hours driving.

[PAUSE]

## [8:30 - 10:30] Talking with Your Rookie Driver

Communication is key to safe driving.

[PAUSE]

Define what safe driving is for you and your new driver.

[PAUSE]

Discuss what skills you and your new driver will be working on before you start to drive.

[PAUSE]

Make sure your instructions are simple and direct.

[PAUSE]

Give feedback that focuses on improvement and how to learn from mistakes.

[PAUSE]

**Remain calm with your new driver at all times.** Yelling does not help.

[PAUSE]

Set a good example with your safe driving. Follow all traffic laws. Avoid distractions. Always wear your seat belt.

[PAUSE]

What can you do if your Rookie Driver does not obey your rules or Maryland law?

[PAUSE]

When your new driver received a learner's permit or provisional license, you were required to **co sign**. This means you can withdraw consent and cancel the license. The new driver may not be able to obtain a new license until turning eighteen.

[PAUSE]
