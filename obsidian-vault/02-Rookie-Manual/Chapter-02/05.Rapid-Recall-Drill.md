# Chapter-02 - Rapid Recall Drill

## Instructions

## Questions

**Q1. [Type: RECALL]** What must a driving school be to certify completion of driver education?

**Answer:** Licensed by the Maryland Motor Vehicle Administration (MVA).

**Explanation:** Only MVA-licensed schools are authorized to certify that a student has completed the driver education program. Unlicensed schools cannot provide official certification.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume any driving school is acceptable. Only MVA-licensed schools can certify completion.

**Common Mistake:** Enrolling in an unlicensed driving school and expecting it to count toward driver education requirements.

---

**Q2. [Type: RECALL]** How many hours of classroom instruction and behind-the-wheel training are required?

**Answer:** 30 hours of classroom instruction and 6 hours of behind-the-wheel training.

**Explanation:** The Maryland Certified Driver Education Program requires a minimum of 30 classroom hours and 6 hours of actual driving instruction with a certified instructor.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't swap these numbers (6 classroom, 30 behind-the-wheel). The correct split is 30 classroom, 6 driving.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing the two numbers or thinking they are equal.

---

**Q3. [Type: RECALL]** What does "Unit One" refer to in driver education?

**Answer:** The Orientation session for Driver Education, which coaches and new drivers attend together.

**Explanation:** Unit One is the first component of driver education, designed to orient both the coach and new driver to the program and expectations.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse Unit One with the first driving lesson. It's an orientation session, not a driving session.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking Unit One is a driving lesson rather than an orientation meeting.

---

**Q4. [Type: RECALL]** Where can you find a complete listing of MVA-licensed driving schools?

**Answer:** At www.mva.maryland.gov.

**Explanation:** The MVA website maintains an official directory of all licensed driving schools in Maryland, organized by area.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think you need to call the MVA directly. The website has the complete listing.

**Common Mistake:** Relying on word-of-mouth instead of checking the official MVA directory.

---

**Q5. [Type: RECALL]** What section of the Maryland Driver's Manual covers texting and cell phone laws?

**Answer:** Section VI of the Maryland Driver's Manual.

**Explanation:** Section VI specifically addresses laws regarding texting and cell phone use for both new drivers and their coaches.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse with Section X (seatbelts). Texting laws are in Section VI.

**Common Mistake:** Mixing up which section covers which topic.

---

**Q6. [Type: RECALL]** What section of the Maryland Driver's Manual covers seatbelt laws?

**Answer:** Section X of the Maryland Driver's Manual.

**Explanation:** Section X provides detailed information about seatbelt laws, including who must wear them and the penalties for non-compliance.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse with Section VI (texting laws). Seatbelts are in Section X.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming seatbelt laws are covered in the same section as cell phone laws.

---

**Q7. [Type: APPLICATION]** You want to enroll your new driver in a driving school. What should you check first?

**Answer:** Verify that the school is licensed by the Maryland MVA. Only licensed schools can certify completion of driver education.

**Explanation:** The first and most critical step is confirming MVA licensure. Without it, the school cannot provide official certification.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't focus on cost or location first. MVA licensure is the primary requirement.

**Common Mistake:** Choosing a school based on price or convenience without verifying MVA licensure.

---

**Q8. [Type: APPLICATION]** What policies should you ask a driving school about before enrolling?

**Answer:** Ask about the Unit One orientation schedule, cost, payment plans, and refund policy if the new driver cannot finish.

**Explanation:** These policies affect your financial commitment and scheduling. Knowing them upfront prevents surprises later.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't forget to ask about the refund policy. It's specifically mentioned in the manual.

**Common Mistake:** Only asking about cost and forgetting about orientation schedule and refund policy.

---

**Q9. [Type: APPLICATION]** As a coach, what should you do before starting to practice driving with your new driver?

**Answer:** Discuss what skills you will be working on, define what safe driving means for you, and make sure your instructions are simple and direct.

**Explanation:** Pre-practice discussion sets expectations and ensures both coach and driver are on the same page about goals and safety standards.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't skip the discussion and just start driving. Preparation is key.

**Common Mistake:** Jumping into practice without discussing expectations and safety standards first.

---

**Q10. [Type: APPLICATION]** How should a coach give feedback to a new driver?

**Answer:** Give feedback that focuses on improvement and how to learn from mistakes. Remain calm at all times. Yelling does not help.

**Explanation:** Constructive feedback helps the new driver improve, while yelling creates anxiety and can lead to more mistakes.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume any feedback is good feedback. It must be constructive and calm.

**Common Mistake:** Criticizing without offering solutions or getting frustrated and yelling.

---

**Q11. [Type: APPLICATION]** What should a coach do to set a good example for a new driver?

**Answer:** Follow all traffic laws, avoid distractions, and always wear a seat belt.

**Explanation:** New drivers learn by observing their coaches. If the coach breaks traffic laws or drives distracted, the new driver will too.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think your driving habits don't matter. The new driver is watching everything.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the new driver won't notice traffic violations or distracted driving.

---

**Q12. [Type: APPLICATION]** A new driver is not obeying your rules or Maryland law. What can you do?

**Answer:** You can withdraw consent and cancel the learner's permit or provisional license. The new driver may not be able to obtain a new license until turning 18.

**Explanation:** As the co-signer, you have the authority to revoke the driving privileges if the new driver violates rules or laws.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think you have no recourse. The co-signer agreement gives you specific authority.

**Common Mistake:** Feeling powerless to address dangerous driving behavior.

---

**Q13. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Compare the roles of the coach and the driving school in the driver education process.

**Answer:** The coach provides ongoing practice and mentorship, while the driving school provides formal classroom instruction and behind-the-wheel training. Both are essential for the new driver's development.

**Explanation:** The coach handles daily practice and real-world driving experiences, while the school provides structured curriculum and professional instruction.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think one replaces the other. Both are required components.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming that completing driver education eliminates the need for coached practice.

---

**Q14. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Why is it important to review what the new driver is learning in each class?

**Answer:** Reviewing helps the coach understand what skills to practice, ensures consistency between classroom and practice, and allows the coach to reinforce lessons.

**Explanation:** When the coach knows what the driver is learning, they can provide targeted practice and reinforce classroom concepts during real driving.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume the school handles everything. The coach's involvement in reviewing lessons is explicitly recommended.

**Common Mistake:** Not staying involved in the educational process and letting the school handle everything.

---

**Q15. [Type: RECALL]** What laws should coaches know about regarding new drivers?

**Answer:** Texting and cell phone laws (Section VI), seatbelt laws (Section X), moving over for emergency vehicles, and sharing the road laws.

**Explanation:** These are the most critical safety laws that coaches must understand and enforce during practice driving.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't forget the moving over law. It's specifically mentioned alongside texting and seatbelt laws.

**Common Mistake:** Focusing only on texting laws and forgetting the other important laws.

---

**Q16. [Type: RECALL]** Where can you obtain a copy of the Maryland Driver's Manual?

**Answer:** At www.mva.maryland.gov.

**Explanation:** The MVA website provides the most current version of the Maryland Driver's Manual, which is the official source for driving laws and regulations.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think you need to visit an MVA office. The manual is available online.

**Common Mistake:** Using outdated versions or unofficial sources instead of the official MVA manual.

---

**Q17. [Type: APPLICATION]** What should you discuss with your new driver before starting to drive?

**Answer:** Define what safe driving means, discuss what skills you will be working on, and establish clear expectations.

**Explanation:** This pre-drive discussion ensures both coach and driver are aligned on goals and safety standards before getting behind the wheel.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't skip this step. It's specifically recommended in the manual.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the new driver already knows what is expected of them.

---

**Q18. [Type: APPLICATION]** Why is it important to schedule times and prepare routes before practicing?

**Answer:** Scheduling ensures consistent practice, and prepared routes provide appropriate learning environments for the new driver's skill level.

**Explanation:** Planning ahead maximizes practice efficiency and ensures the new driver is challenged appropriately without being overwhelmed.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think spontaneous practice is sufficient. Structured practice is more effective.

**Common Mistake:** Driving randomly without a plan or prepared routes.

---

**Q19. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** How does the coach's role change after the new driver obtains a provisional license?

**Answer:** The coach should continue to reinforce safe driving habits and serve as a role model, even though the new driver has more independence.

**Explanation:** Safe driving is a lifelong responsibility. The coach's influence doesn't end with the provisional license.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume the coach's job is done when the provisional license is issued.

**Common Mistake:** Reducing involvement after the new driver gets their provisional license.

---

**Q20. [Type: RECALL]** What is the goal of the Rookie Driver - Coach Driving Agreement?

**Answer:** To help the coach and new driver develop a contract to encourage safe driving throughout the dangerous first years of driving.

**Explanation:** The agreement formalizes the commitment to safe driving and establishes clear expectations between coach and driver.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think the agreement is optional. It's a required component of the graduated licensing process.

**Common Mistake:** Treating the agreement as just paperwork rather than a meaningful commitment.
