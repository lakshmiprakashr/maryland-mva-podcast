## [1:00 - 4:00] Permit Requirements by Age

The MVA exam loves to test on these specific numbers. Listen carefully.

[PAUSE]

You must have a learner's permit for the period of time specified based on your age.

[PAUSE]

**Age 18 or under without a high school diploma**: Nine months holding period.

[PAUSE]

**Age 18 with a high school diploma**: Three months.

[PAUSE]

**Ages 19 through 24**: Three months.

[PAUSE]

**Age 25 and older**: Only forty five days.

[PAUSE]

If you are convicted or granted **Probation Before Judgment, also known as PBJ**, for any moving violation, your time will be **restarted and extended to nine months**.

[PAUSE]

This applies to **all ages**. Even if you are twenty five or older.

[PAUSE]

## [4:00 - 6:30] Practice and Education Requirements

Let us cover the practice and education requirements.

[PAUSE]

Supervising drivers must be at least **twenty one years of age** and must have held a driver's license for at least **three years**.

[PAUSE]

Nighttime practice hours are defined as **one half hour before sunset until one half hour after sunrise**.

[PAUSE]

A practice driving skills log must be signed by an individual certifying the practice driving requirements were fulfilled.

[PAUSE]

You must complete the Maryland Certified Driver Education Program: **thirty hours of classroom instruction** and **six hours of behind the wheel training**.

[PAUSE]

Your Maryland learner's instructional permit is good for **two years, or twenty four months**, from the date it is issued.

[PAUSE]

## [6:30 - 9:30] Special Situations

Now let us answer some common questions about special situations.

[PAUSE]

**Do I need a learner's permit to start practicing?**

[PAUSE]

Before you start driving, you must have a learner's instructional permit. You must also have your learner's permit with you every time you drive, even in an empty parking lot.

[PAUSE]

**My family is going on vacation. What if I want to drive in another state?**

[PAUSE]

You should check with the Motor Vehicle Administration in those states before driving there. A state may not accept a Maryland learner's permit, and you would not be able to drive there even if you are with a supervising driver over 21 with three years of driving experience.

[PAUSE]

**I just got my receipt from the MVA. What if I want to start driving?**

[PAUSE]

You can drive but you must keep your receipt with you when you drive.

[PAUSE]

**What if my learner's permit expires before I get my provisional license?**

[PAUSE]

You will need to **retake the law test** and get a new learner's permit before you will be allowed to take the driving skills test.

[PAUSE]

## [9:30 - 11:00] Technology During Skills Test

Here is a critical fact the MVA loves to test.

[PAUSE]

**Can I use parking assist, backup cameras, and other technologies when I take my skills test?**

[PAUSE]

You may use your **backup camera** and the vehicle's **onboard radar system**.

[PAUSE]

But you may **not** use the **parking assist** feature.

[PAUSE]

The distinction is about driver control. Backup cameras help you see, but parking assist takes over steering. The MVA wants to test your ability to steer and park.

[PAUSE]

The MVA exam loves to ask: Can you use parking assist during the skills test? The answer is **no**.

[PAUSE]

Remember: **Camera yes, assist no**.

[PAUSE]
