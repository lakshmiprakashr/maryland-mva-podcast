# Chapter-04 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Chapter 4 provides detailed information about learner's permit requirements in Maryland. It covers the Graduated Licensing System chart, holding periods, practice hours, driver education requirements, and special situations like driving in other states, using technology during the skills test, and what happens when permits expire.

## Why It Matters

The MVA tests extensively on permit requirements and special situations. Knowing the exact requirements and common pitfalls is essential for both the knowledge test and for maintaining your driving privileges.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Holding periods**: 9 months (18 or under, no diploma), 3 months (18 with diploma, 19-24), 45 days (25+)
- **Practice hours**: 60 hours (under 25, 10 at night), 14 hours (25+, 3 at night)
- **Driver education**: 30 classroom + 6 behind-the-wheel (required for all)
- **Supervisor**: 21+ years old, 3+ years licensed
- **Permit validity**: 2 years (24 months)
- **Moving violation/PBJ**: Extends holding period to 9 months for all
- **Skills test technology**: Backup camera allowed, parking assist NOT allowed
- **Permit expired**: Must retake law test
- **Driving in another state**: Check with that state's MVA first

## MVA Test Traps

- **"Backup camera vs parking assist" trap**: Backup camera is allowed, parking assist is NOT allowed. Don't confuse these two technologies.
- **"Permit expired" trap**: You must retake the LAW test, not the skills test. Don't confuse which test you need to retake.
- **"Driving in another state" trap**: Other states may NOT accept your Maryland learner's permit. Don't assume reciprocity.
- **"Receipt driving" trap**: You CAN drive with just the receipt, but you MUST keep it with you.

## Common Mistakes

- Forgetting that parking assist is prohibited during the skills test
- Assuming your Maryland permit automatically works in other states
- Not knowing you must retake the law test if your permit expires
- Forgetting to bring the receipt when driving with a new permit

## Memory Tricks

- **"Camera yes, assist no"** — backup camera is okay, parking assist is not
- **"Receipt is your permit"** — you can drive with the receipt, just keep it with you
- **"Law test, not skills"** — if permit expires, retake the law test
- **"Check before you drive"** — check with other states before driving there

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

No road signs or signals are covered in this chapter. Focus on permit requirements and special situations.

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **Learner's Permit Requirements**: Specific holding periods, practice hours, and education requirements by age
- **Skills Test Technology**: Backup camera and onboard radar allowed; parking assist prohibited
- **Permit Expiration**: Must retake law test if permit expires before provisional license
- **Out-of-State Driving**: Must check with other states' MVA before driving there
- **Receipt Driving**: Can drive with receipt but must keep it in possession
