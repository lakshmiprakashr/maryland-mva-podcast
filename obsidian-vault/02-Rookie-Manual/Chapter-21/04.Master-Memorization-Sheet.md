# Chapter 21 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Maryland's impaired driving laws and the practice log requirements for new drivers. Under 21 faces zero tolerance for alcohol. Over 21, BAC thresholds determine DWI (.07-.079) versus DUI (.08+). Implied consent applies to all license holders. New drivers under 25 need 60 hours of practice (10 at night); drivers 25+ need 14 hours (3 at night).

## Why It Matters

Impaired driving is the leading cause of traffic fatalities. Knowing the exact BAC thresholds and penalties helps you avoid life-altering consequences. The practice log ensures new drivers build real-world skills before driving independently.

## High-Yield Facts

- Under 21: **zero tolerance** — any alcohol = citation
- BAC .07 to .079 = **Driving While Impaired (DWI)**
- BAC .08 or above = **Driving Under the Influence (DUI)**
- **Implied Consent**: by accepting any MD license/permit, you agree to drug and alcohol testing when requested by law enforcement
- Refusing a test can result in loss of driving privilege
- DUI, DWI, and Refusal to Test **cannot be expunged** from your record
- Under 25: minimum **60 hours** practice, **10 hours at night**
- 25 and older: minimum **14 hours** practice, **3 hours at night**
- Practice hours are **in addition to** the 6 hours of behind-the-wheel instruction from driver education
- 60 hours is a **suggested minimum** — more practice is always better

## MVA Test Traps

- **Trap:** Confusing DWI and DUI thresholds. DWI is .07-.079; DUI is .08+. The MVA loves to swap these.
- **Trap:** Thinking implied consent only applies to full license holders. It applies to permits, provisional licenses, and full licenses.
- **Trap:** Believing you can refuse a breathalyzer without consequences. Refusal = automatic loss of driving privilege.
- **Trap:** Thinking practice hours include the 6 hours from driver education. They do not — practice hours are **in addition to** driver education hours.

## Common Mistakes

- Mixing up the BAC numbers: remember .07 is the DWI floor, .08 is the DUI floor
- Forgetting that under 21 has zero tolerance — not a .08 threshold
- Assuming DUI/DWI convictions can be erased from your record — they cannot
- Not understanding that practice log hours are separate from driver education hours

## Memory Tricks

- **"7-8" mnemonic**: .0**7** = DWI, .0**8** = DUI — the 7 comes first (DWI is the lesser charge)
- **"Under 21, zero tolerance"** — say it three times
- **"60 and 10"** for under 25 (60 total, 10 night)
- **"14 and 3"** for 25 and older (14 total, 3 night)
- **"Implied = automatic"** — by driving, you already agreed

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- Maryland zero tolerance for drivers under 21
- DWI threshold: .07 BAC (Maryland Transport. Art. § 21-902)
- DUI threshold: .08 BAC (Maryland Transport. Art. § 21-902)
- Implied Consent Law applies to all Maryland license holders
- DUI/DWI/Refusal cannot be expunged from driving record
- Practice hours required before taking skills test: 60 hrs (under 25) or 14 hrs (25+)
