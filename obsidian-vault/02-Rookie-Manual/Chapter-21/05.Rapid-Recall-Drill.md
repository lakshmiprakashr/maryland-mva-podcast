## Questions

**Q1. [Type: RECALL]** What is Maryland's zero tolerance policy for drivers under 21 regarding alcohol?

**Answer:** Drivers under 21 receive a citation for consuming even one drink and driving — any amount of alcohol is illegal.

**Explanation:** Maryland enforces zero tolerance for drivers under 21 because they are not legally old enough to drink. Any detectable alcohol while driving results in a citation, regardless of BAC level.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse this with the .08 BAC threshold that applies to drivers 21 and over. Zero tolerance means **any** alcohol for under-21 drivers.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking under-21 drivers get the same .08 BAC threshold as adults.

---

**Q2. [Type: RECALL]** What BAC range constitutes Driving While Impaired (DWI) for drivers 21 and over?

**Answer:** A BAC of .07 to .079 constitutes DWI for drivers 21 and over.

**Explanation:** Maryland law defines DWI as operating a vehicle with a BAC between .07 and .079 for drivers 21 and older. This is a lesser charge than DUI but still carries serious penalties.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse DWI (.07-.079) with DUI (.08+). The MVA frequently swaps these thresholds.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking DWI and DUI are the same charge or that .08 is the DWI threshold.

---

**Q3. [Type: RECALL]** What BAC level constitutes Driving Under the Influence (DUI) for drivers 21 and over?

**Answer:** A BAC of .08 or above constitutes DUI for drivers 21 and over.

**Explanation:** DUI is the more serious impaired driving charge in Maryland. A BAC of .08 or higher triggers DUI charges for drivers 21 and older, with penalties including potential license suspension and criminal record.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse DUI (.08+) with DWI (.07-.079). Remember: the higher number is the more serious charge.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting that both DUI and DWI are separate charges with different BAC thresholds.

---

**Q4. [Type: RECALL]** What is Implied Consent in Maryland?

**Answer:** Implied Consent means that by signing and accepting any Maryland driver's license or permit, you agree to be tested for drugs and alcohol when requested by law enforcement.

**Explanation:** When you accept a Maryland Learner's Permit, Provisional License, or full Driver's License, you automatically consent to chemical testing. Refusing to comply may result in the loss of your driving privilege.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think Implied Consent only applies to full license holders — it applies to permits, provisional licenses, and full licenses.

**Common Mistake:** Believing you can refuse a breathalyzer without consequences.

---

**Q5. [Type: RECALL]** Can DUI, DWI, and Refusal to Test be expunged from your driving record?

**Answer:** No — DUI, DWI, and Refusal to Test cannot be expunged from your driving record.

**Explanation:** These charges remain permanently on your driving record. Unlike some other violations, there is no process to have them removed, which can affect insurance rates and employment opportunities.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume these charges can be erased after a certain period. They are permanent.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that completing a program or waiting a certain number of years will result in expungement.

---

**Q6. [Type: RECALL]** How many hours of practice driving are required for new drivers under 25?

**Answer:** A minimum of 60 hours, including at least 10 hours of nighttime driving.

**Explanation:** Maryland requires drivers under 25 to complete 60 hours of behind-the-wheel practice. At least 10 of those hours must be at night. These hours are in addition to the 6 hours from driver education.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't forget the 10 nighttime hours — they are part of the 60 total, not additional.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing the 60-hour requirement with the 14-hour requirement for drivers 25 and older.

---

**Q7. [Type: RECALL]** How many hours of practice driving are required for drivers 25 and older?

**Answer:** A minimum of 14 hours, including at least 3 hours of nighttime driving.

**Explanation:** Drivers 25 and older need fewer practice hours — 14 total with 3 at night. Like the under-25 requirement, these hours are in addition to the 6 hours from driver education.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't swap the 60-hour and 14-hour requirements. Use the age threshold of 25 to keep them straight.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting that nighttime hours are included in the total, not separate.

---

**Q8. [Type: RECALL]** Are practice driving hours in addition to or inclusive of the 6 hours from driver education?

**Answer:** Practice hours are in addition to the 6 hours of behind-the-wheel instruction from driver education.

**Explanation:** The 60 hours (under 25) or 14 hours (25+) are completely separate from the 6 hours received during the required driver education course. You must complete both.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think the 6 hours of driver education counts toward your practice hours. They are entirely separate requirements.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming that the 6 hours from driver education can be subtracted from the total practice requirement.

---

**Q9. [Type: APPLICATION]** A 20-year-old driver has completed 50 hours of practice, including 8 hours at night. Are they ready to take the skills test?

**Answer:** No — they need 60 total hours with at least 10 at night. They are short 10 total hours and 2 nighttime hours.

**Explanation:** Drivers under 25 must complete 60 hours total with 10 at night. This driver has only 50 total hours and 8 nighttime hours, so they still need 10 more hours (including at least 2 more at night).

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume meeting one requirement means you meet both. You need both the total hours AND the nighttime minimum.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to check both the total hours and the nighttime hours separately.

---

**Q10. [Type: APPLICATION]** A 30-year-old has completed 14 hours of practice including 3 hours at night. Is this sufficient?

**Answer:** Yes — drivers 25 and older need 14 hours total with 3 at night. Both requirements are met.

**Explanation:** The 30-year-old meets the 14-hour minimum and the 3-hour nighttime requirement. They have completed the practice driving requirements for their age group.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't apply the under-25 requirements (60 hours) to a driver over 25.

**Common Mistake:** Using the wrong age threshold to determine practice hour requirements.

---

**Q11. [Type: APPLICATION]** A driver is pulled over and refuses to take a breathalyzer test. What happens?

**Answer:** Refusing to comply with a requested drug or alcohol test may result in the loss of driving privilege.

**Explanation:** Under Maryland's Implied Consent law, refusing a chemical test when requested by law enforcement carries automatic consequences, including potential loss of driving privilege.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think refusal is a way to avoid DUI charges — it carries its own penalty.

**Common Mistake:** Believing that refusing a breathalyzer means you cannot be charged with impaired driving.

---

**Q12. [Type: APPLICATION]** A new driver's practice log shows 55 hours total with 9 hours at night, and they are 22 years old. What is missing?

**Answer:** They need 60 total hours (5 short) and 10 nighttime hours (1 short).

**Explanation:** At 22, this driver falls under the under-25 category and needs 60 hours total with 10 at night. They are 5 hours short of the total and 1 hour short of the nighttime requirement.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't round up — even being 1 hour short means the requirement is not met.

**Common Mistake:** Not counting the nighttime hours separately from the total.

---

**Q13. [Type: APPLICATION]** A parent asks if their 16-year-old's 6 hours of driver education can count toward the 60 practice hours. What is the correct answer?

**Answer:** No — the 60 hours are in addition to the 6 hours of driver education.

**Explanation:** Practice hours and driver education hours are completely separate requirements. The 60 hours of supervised practice must be completed independently of the 6 hours from the driver education course.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume any overlap between practice hours and driver education.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that any driving instruction counts toward the practice requirement.

---

**Q14. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** How do the DWI and DUI thresholds differ, and what are the implications for drivers 21 and over?

**Answer:** DWI is .07-.079 BAC; DUI is .08 or higher. Both are serious charges, but DUI carries more severe penalties.

**Explanation:** Maryland distinguishes between two levels of impaired driving for adults. DWI is the lesser charge at .07-.079 BAC, while DUI at .08+ carries more severe consequences. Both can result in license suspension and criminal records.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't treat DWI and DUI as synonyms — they are separate legal charges with different BAC thresholds.

**Common Mistake:** Failing to recognize that impairment can begin before the legal .08 limit.

---

**Q15. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Compare the practice hour requirements for drivers under 25 versus 25 and older. Why do you think the requirements differ?

**Answer:** Under 25: 60 hours (10 night). 25+: 14 hours (3 night). The difference reflects that younger drivers need more supervised experience.

**Explanation:** Maryland requires significantly more practice hours for younger drivers because they have less overall driving experience. The 60-hour requirement for under-25 drivers ensures more supervised practice before independent driving.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't forget that both age groups must complete nighttime driving hours as part of their total.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the difference is only about total hours, not also about nighttime hours.

---

**Q16. [Type: RECALL]** What happens to your driving privilege if you refuse a chemical test requested by law enforcement?

**Answer:** Refusing to comply may result in the loss of your driving privilege.

**Explanation:** Under Maryland's Implied Consent law, refusing a requested drug or alcohol test carries immediate consequences, including potential suspension or revocation of your driving privilege.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think refusal is consequence-free — it carries its own penalty separate from any DUI charge.

**Common Mistake:** Believing that only a failed test has consequences, not a refused test.

---

**Q17. [Type: RECALL]** Is it possible to have a DUI or DWI conviction removed from your Maryland driving record?

**Answer:** No — DUI, DWI, and Refusal to Test cannot be expunged from your driving record.

**Explanation:** These charges are permanent on your record. They cannot be erased through any process, which is why Maryland treats them as extremely serious offenses.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume there is a waiting period after which these charges disappear. They are permanent.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing expungement eligibility for other offenses with DUI/DWI charges.

---

**Q18. [Type: APPLICATION]** A driver is 24 years old and wants to know their practice hour requirement. They have completed 14 hours including 3 hours at night. Are they ready for the skills test?

**Answer:** No — at 24, they fall under the under-25 requirement and need 60 hours total with 10 at night.

**Explanation:** Drivers under 25 must complete 60 hours (10 at night). This driver has only 14 hours total and 3 at night, which meets the 25+ requirement but not the under-25 requirement.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't let the 14-hour number trick you — apply the correct age threshold.

**Common Mistake:** Confusing which age group applies when the driver is close to 25.

---

**Q19. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Explain why Maryland has both DWI and DUI charges instead of a single impaired driving law.

**Answer:** Maryland recognizes that impairment begins before the .08 BAC threshold. DWI (.07-.079) captures drivers who are impaired but below the DUI level, ensuring accountability at lower BAC levels.

**Explanation:** Having two levels allows Maryland to address a wider range of impaired driving. Drivers at .07 BAC are still impaired and dangerous, even if they haven't reached the .08 DUI threshold. This tiered approach promotes safer roads.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume .08 is the only number that matters — .07 is also a legal threshold.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that only DUI charges exist or that DWI is just a lesser version of DUI.

---

**Q20. [Type: APPLICATION]** A parent is filling out a practice log for their 17-year-old. They have logged 58 hours total, including 9 hours at night. The parent signs the certification anyway. What are the consequences?

**Answer:** Signing a false certification is perjury — the parent may be prosecuted and the license may be canceled.

**Explanation:** The practice certification is signed under penalty of perjury. Falsely certifying that a driver has completed the required hours is illegal and can result in prosecution and license cancellation.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume the certification is just a formality — it carries legal weight.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that a small shortfall in hours is not a big deal. It is a legal violation.
