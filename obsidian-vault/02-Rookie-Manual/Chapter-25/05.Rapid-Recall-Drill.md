## Questions

**Q1. [Type: RECALL]** What does the Maryland Driver's Manual say is the most hazardous period for new drivers?

**Answer:** The first year of independent driving is the most hazardous for any new driver.

**Explanation:** Statistics show that new drivers face the greatest risk during their first year of driving independently, even if they are careful and cautious.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think the danger period is during the learning phase — it is after obtaining the license.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming that completing driver education and practice hours eliminates driving risk.

**Q2. [Type: RECALL]** Approximately how many people are killed annually on Maryland roads?

**Answer:** More than 400 people are killed annually on Maryland roads.

**Explanation:** This statistic underscores the ongoing danger of driving in Maryland and the importance of continued safe driving habits, especially for new drivers.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume this number is low — 400+ annual fatalities is significant.

**Common Mistake:** Underestimating the danger of Maryland roads based on personal driving experience.

**Q3. [Type: RECALL]** Where can new drivers find information about the Maryland driving test?

**Answer:** At mva.maryland.gov, where a brief video describes the driving test.

**Explanation:** The MVA website provides resources including a video that describes what to expect on the driving test. This is the official source for test preparation information.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume the MVA website is just for scheduling — it has educational resources too.

**Common Mistake:** Not checking the official MVA website for test preparation materials.

**Q4. [Type: RECALL]** What should coaches do after their new driver obtains a provisional license?

**Answer:** Continue monitoring the new driver, follow Graduated Licensing System limitations, and ride with them periodically.

**Explanation:** The manual explicitly states that the coach's role does not end with the provisional license. Ongoing monitoring and periodic ride-alongs help ensure continued safe driving.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume the coach's job is done after the license is obtained.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that coaching ends once the new driver passes the skills test.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think the test is purely about skill — staying calm is equally important.

**Common Mistake:** Not knowing the MVA contact information for the exam.

**Q15. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Why does the manual emphasize that the first year of independent driving is the most hazardous?

**Answer:** New drivers have the skills to pass the test but lack the experience to handle unexpected situations. The first year bridges this gap through continued practice and coaching.

**Explanation:** Statistics show that new drivers are at highest risk during their first year. Despite passing the skills test, they have not yet developed the judgment that comes only from extensive real-world driving experience.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume passing the skills test eliminates risk — experience is the key factor.

**Common Mistake:** Underestimating the danger of the first year of independent driving.

**Q16. [Type: RECALL]** What is the purpose of the Maryland Graduated Licensing System?

**Answer:** To progressively grant driving privileges as new drivers demonstrate competence and maturity, reducing risk during the learning period.

**Explanation:** The three-stage system (Learner's Permit, Provisional License, Full License) provides structured progression with increasing freedom and decreasing restrictions.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think the GDL is just bureaucratic — it serves a specific safety purpose.

**Common Mistake:** Not understanding that each stage has a purpose in driver development.

**Answer:** They can drive independently, but they must follow all provisional license restrictions (nighttime curfew, passenger limits, phone rules if under 18).

**Explanation:** The provisional license allows independent driving but with specific restrictions. These restrictions are designed to reduce risk during the initial period of independent driving.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think "provisional" means "unrestricted" — significant limitations still apply.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the provisional license is identical to a full license.
