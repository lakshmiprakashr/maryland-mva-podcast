## [1:30] Section 1: A Message for New Drivers

Congratulations. If you have made it to this chapter, you have completed your required hours of practice time and driver education. You are ready to take your skills test.

[PAUSE]

The manual has two pieces of advice for test day. First, **practice**. Make sure you have practiced the specific maneuvers that will be on the test. Second, **stay calm**. Nervousness can affect your performance, so take a deep breath and trust your preparation.

[PAUSE]

For more information about the driving test, the manual directs you to **mva.maryland.gov**. There, you can watch a brief video that describes what to expect on the driving test. This is an official MVA resource, so use it.

[PAUSE]

Here is what the MVA exam might ask about this chapter. They may ask where to find information about the driving test, and the answer is mva.maryland.gov. They may ask what the manual recommends for test day, and the answer is practice and stay calm.

## [4:00] Section 2: A Message for Driving Coaches

Now, a message for the driving coaches. The Motor Vehicle Administration and all the drivers on Maryland roads thank you for helping your new driver learn to drive safely and legally.

[PAUSE]

But here is the important reminder. The first year of independent driving is the **most hazardous** for any new driver. Even careful, cautious new drivers can get into serious crashes. On average, more than **four hundred people** are killed annually on Maryland roads.

[PAUSE]

This statistic is not meant to scare you. It is meant to emphasize that the coach's role does not end when the new driver gets their provisional license. You should **continue to monitor** your new driver and follow the limitations set out in the Maryland Graduated Licensing System.

[PAUSE]

The manual recommends that you **continue to ride with** your new driver from time to time to see how they are progressing and to offer comments or suggestions. Hold your new driver to the contract you made in the Driver-Coach Agreement. You may even want to **update the agreement** as your new driver gains experience and skill.

[PAUSE]

Memory trick. Think "coach for life." The coaching relationship should continue beyond licensure. Safe driving does not end when the new driver gets a provisional license. It is a **commitment for life** for both you and your new driver.

[PAUSE]

The MVA website is **www.MVA.Maryland.gov**. This is where you can find information about the driving test, schedule appointments, and access other MVA services.

[PAUSE]

The physical address of the MVA is **6601 Ritchie Highway, Northeast, Glen Burnie, Maryland, 21062**. This is where you go for in-person services.

[PAUSE]

Here is what the MVA exam might ask. They may ask for the MVA phone number, the website, or the address. These are straightforward facts that you should memorize.

## [10:30] Section 4: Key Takeaways

Let us pull together the key messages from this chapter and from the entire Rookie Manual.

[PAUSE]

First, the first year of independent driving is the most hazardous. This is why the Graduated Licensing System exists, with its restrictions that gradually decrease as the driver gains experience.

[PAUSE]

Second, coaching should continue after licensure. The manual is clear about this. Periodic ride-alongs, maintaining the agreement, and providing ongoing support are all recommended.

[PAUSE]

Third, safe driving is a lifelong commitment. It does not end with passing the skills test. It requires continuous attention, practice, and responsibility.

[PAUSE]

Fourth, know your MVA resources. The website, the phone numbers, and the physical address are all important to remember for the exam and for real life.
