# Chapter 20 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary
This chapter covers crash reporting procedures and Maryland's impaired driving laws. You must know when law enforcement contact is required after a crash, the correct steps for different crash scenarios, and the specific BAC thresholds for DUI and DWI. Maryland has zero tolerance for drivers under 21 and the Implied Consent law means you agree to testing by obtaining any Maryland driver's license.

## Why It Matters
Crash procedures determine whether you fulfill your legal obligations and protect yourself legally. Impaired driving laws have severe consequences that can permanently affect your driving privilege and criminal record. Understanding these laws is critical for the MVA knowledge test and for real-world decision-making.

## High-Yield Facts
- **Call law enforcement when:** Someone injured, vehicle cannot be moved, driver under influence, driver has no license, driver tries to leave scene, public property damaged
- **Injury crash:** Call 911 immediately, stay at scene, do NOT move vehicles
- **No injuries, cannot move vehicle:** Call 911, move to safe location, use emergency flashers/flares
- **No injuries, can move vehicle:** Move out of travel lane, collect: name, address, phone, insurance, plate, make/model/year, witnesses
- **Domestic animal strike:** Must notify law enforcement
- **Unattended vehicle/property:** Find owner and provide contact info, or leave your contact info securely and notify insurance
- **Under 21:** Zero tolerance for alcohol — any alcohol consumption while driving = citation
- **21 and over DWI:** BAC of .07 to .079 = Driving While Impaired
- **21 and over DUI:** BAC of .08 or above = Driving Under the Influence
- **Both DUI and DWI:** Extremely serious charges that may result in loss of driving privilege for an extended period
- **Implied Consent:** By accepting any Maryland driver's license, you agree to be tested for drugs and/or alcohol when requested by law enforcement
- **Refusing to comply** with testing may result in loss of driving privilege
- **DUI, DWI, and Refusal to Test:** Cannot be expunged from your driving record
- **Insurance card:** Required at all times since October 1, 2016; electronic format accepted

## MVA Test Traps
- **Trap:** "Under 21 can have one drink and drive" — WRONG. Zero tolerance means ANY alcohol.
- **Trap:** "DWI and DUI are the same thing" — WRONG. DWI is .07-.079 BAC, DUI is .08+.
- **Trap:** "You can refuse a breathalyzer without consequences" — WRONG. Implied Consent means refusal can result in license loss.
- **Trap:** "DUI can be expunged from your record" — WRONG. DUI, DWI, and refusal cannot be expunged.
- **Trap:** "You only need to call 911 for injury crashes" — WRONG. Six situations require law enforcement contact.

## Common Mistakes
- Confusing DWI (.07-.079) with DUI (.08+) thresholds
- Thinking Implied Consent only applies to full license holders — it applies to permits and provisional licenses too
- Not knowing that refusal to test has its own separate consequences
- Believing impaired driving charges can be removed from your record
- Not calling law enforcement for crashes involving unlicensed drivers or property damage
- Collecting incomplete information after a minor crash
- Leaving the scene before emergency responders arrive in an injury crash

## Memory Tricks
- **"Zero Under 21"** — Zero tolerance for alcohol under age 21
- **".07 = DWI, .08 = DUI"** — The threshold separates the two charges
- **"Implied Consent = License = Testing"** — Getting any Maryland license means you consent to testing
- **"Cannot Expunge"** — DUI, DWI, and refusal stay on your record forever
- **"Call 911 for six"** — Injuries, immovable, DUI, no license, hit-and-run, public property
- **"Name, Phone, Plate, Insurance"** — The four essentials after a crash

## Maryland-Specific Laws
- **Zero tolerance under 21:** Any alcohol consumption while driving results in citation
- **DWI threshold (21+):** BAC .07 to .079
- **DUI threshold (21+):** BAC .08 or above
- **Implied Consent:** Accepting any Maryland license = consent to drug/alcohol testing on request
- **Refusal consequences:** May result in loss of driving privilege
- **Permanent record:** DUI, DWI, and refusal to test cannot be expunged
- **Insurance requirement (October 1, 2016):** Valid insurance card at all times; electronic accepted; must present on request
