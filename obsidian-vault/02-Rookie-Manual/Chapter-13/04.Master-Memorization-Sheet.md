# Chapter 13 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

This chapter covers roundabouts in detail, driving with bicyclists, the Coach's Checklist for skill advancement, expressway driving basics, the I.P.D.E. system, and entering highways from cross streets.

## Why It Matters

Roundabouts reduce fatal crashes by 90 percent compared to traditional intersections. Expressway driving involves high speeds and multiple lanes, making it the most demanding driving environment for new drivers. The I.P.D.E. system is the foundational hazard perception framework used on the Maryland knowledge test.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Roundabout:** Slow down, obey signs and markings, yield to pedestrians and bicyclists when entering and exiting, yield to traffic from your LEFT, keep speed low, signal right at your exit
- **Bicyclists:** 3-foot minimum space cushion required on Maryland roads
- **Expressway entry:** Identify entrance ramp, accelerate to highway speed, signal, select safe gap, merge into closest lane
- **Following distance on expressway:** minimum 3 to 4 seconds
- **Expressway exit:** Identify exit in advance, signal, use mirrors and head checks, use deceleration lane, adjust speed after exiting
- **I.P.D.E.:** Identify, Predict, Decide, Execute
- **Cross street entry:** Stop completely unless entering on green signal, select a large gap, accelerate into proper lane
- **Start expressway practice** on light-traffic days, good weather, weekend mornings

## MVA Test Traps

- **Roundabout yield direction:** You yield LEFT, not right
- **I.P.D.E. acronym:** Students mix up the order. Remember: Identify, Predict, Decide, Execute
- **Expressway speed on entry:** You must accelerate to highway speed on the ramp, not enter slowly
- **Following distance:** 3 to 4 seconds, not 2 seconds
- **Cross street stop:** You MUST stop completely unless entering on a green signal

## Common Mistakes

- Yielding to the wrong direction in a roundabout
- Entering an expressway too slowly, creating a speed differential
- Forgetting to signal when exiting the expressway
- Not performing head checks before changing lanes on the expressway
- Using high beams in fog (reduces visibility instead of improving it)
- Not practicing night driving for the required 10 hours under GDL

## Memory Tricks

- **"Left Yield Round"** — in a roundabout, yield to traffic from your LEFT
- **"I-P-D-E"** — Identify, Predict, Decide, Execute (in that order)
- **"Ramp Up"** — accelerate on the entrance ramp to match highway speed
- **"3 to 4 on the floor"** — maintain 3 to 4 seconds following distance
- **"Green means go, red means stop"** — at cross street entries, stop unless you have a green signal

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

- **Roundabout warning sign** — yellow diamond with circular arrows
- **Yield signs** at roundabout entrances
- **Expressway entrance sign** — green with highway number
- **Expressway exit sign** — green with exit number and destination
- **Merge sign** — yellow warning that lanes will merge
- **Lane ends sign** — yellow warning that a lane is ending
- **Advisory speed signs** — yellow with recommended speed for curves or exits

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **I.P.D.E. System:** Required hazard perception method taught in Maryland Driver's Manual
- **Expressway Following Distance:** Minimum 3 to 4 seconds under ideal conditions; increase in bad weather or at night
- **Graduated Driver Licensing (GDL):** Requires 10 hours of supervised night driving practice
- **High Beam Usage:** Must switch to low beams when approaching oncoming vehicles and in fog
- **Cross Street Highway Entry:** Must stop completely before entering unless on green signal
