## [1:00 - 4:00] Roundabouts

A roundabout is sometimes called a traffic circle, but it works differently from older traffic circles. A roundabout allows vehicles to move more efficiently than a traffic light.

[PAUSE]

Here is what you do when you get to one. Slow down. Obey all traffic signs and pavement markings.

Yield to pedestrians and bicyclists when entering AND exiting the roundabout. This is important. You yield at both points.

[PAUSE]

Now the big rule. Yield to traffic on your LEFT that is already in the roundabout. You do NOT yield to the right.

[EMPHASIS: left]

[PAUSE]

Keep your speed low while you are in the roundabout. And as you approach your exit, turn your right turn signal on.

[PAUSE]

So the mantra is. Left yield round. Exit right. Yield to pedestrians entering and exiting.

The MVA exam will test you on the yielding direction. Always yield to traffic from your left. That is the one fact you cannot get wrong.

## [4:00 - 7:00] Bicyclists and the I.P.D.E. System

Maryland law requires you to maintain a three-foot space cushion between you and a bicyclist. Many Maryland roads do not have bicycle lanes, but the three-foot rule applies on all roads.

[PAUSE]

Now let us talk about the I.P.D.E. system. This stands for Identify, Predict, Decide, Execute. It is the hazard perception framework you need for both the knowledge test and safe driving.

[PAUSE]

Identify means spot the potential risk. A pedestrian at a crosswalk, a car approaching an intersection, or a cyclist ahead.

Predict means think about what could happen. The pedestrian might step into the road. The other car might run the light.

[PAUSE]

Decide means choose your best course of action. Slow down, prepare to stop, or change lanes.

Execute means carry out that decision smoothly and decisively.

[PAUSE]

The MVA exam loves to ask about I.P.D.E. The order matters. Identify, Predict, Decide, Execute. Never skip a step. And never reverse Predict and Decide. You predict BEFORE you decide.

## [7:00 - 11:00] Expressway Driving

Expressway driving is the most challenging environment for new drivers. Let us break it down step by step.

[PAUSE]

Entering the expressway. Identify the entrance ramp. Accelerate to highway speed on the ramp and signal to indicate you are merging. Select a safe gap and merge into the closest lane.

[EMPHASIS: accelerate]

The most common mistake is entering too slowly. The ramp is designed for acceleration. Use it.

[PAUSE]

Driving on the expressway. Select the proper driving lane. Maintain a minimum of three to four seconds of following space. Change lanes only after signaling, checking mirrors, and performing head checks.

[PAUSE]

Exiting the expressway. Identify your exit well in advance. Signal your intent. Check mirrors and perform head checks. Use the deceleration lane if it is available. And adjust your speed after exiting.

[PAUSE]

Entering from a cross street is different. You must stop completely unless you have a green signal. Signal your direction. Carefully select a large gap in traffic. Gap selection is critical at highway speeds. Then accelerate into the proper lane.

[PAUSE]

Now night driving. Before you drive, check that all lights work. Clean your windows and headlights. Avoid high beams in fog and when approaching oncoming vehicles. Use the day-night rear-view mirror to reduce glare. Keep your eyes moving. Increase your following distance.

The GDL system requires ten hours of night driving practice. Many teen crashes happen at night because new drivers do not make these adjustments.

## [11:00 - 12:30] Quick Review and Closing

Let us do a quick review of the must-know facts.

[PAUSE]

Roundabouts. Yield to traffic from your LEFT. Yield to pedestrians entering and exiting. Signal right at your exit.

[PAUSE]

I.P.D.E. Identify, Predict, Decide, Execute. The order matters.

[PAUSE]

Expressway entry. Accelerate to highway speed on the ramp. Signal. Merge when safe.

[PAUSE]

Expressway following distance. Three to four seconds minimum.

[PAUSE]

Expressway exit. Signal, mirrors, head checks, deceleration lane.

[PAUSE]

Cross street entry. Stop completely unless on green signal.

[PAUSE]

Night driving. Low beams in fog and with oncoming traffic. Ten hours practice required.
