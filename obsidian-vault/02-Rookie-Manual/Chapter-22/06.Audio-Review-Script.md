## [1:30] Section 1: Practice Hour Requirements

The first thing you need to know is that Maryland requires different amounts of practice depending on your age. If you are under twenty-five, you need a minimum of **sixty hours** of supervised practice driving. Of those sixty hours, at least **ten hours** must be at night.

[PAUSE]

If you are twenty-five or older, you need **fourteen hours** of practice, and at least **three hours** must be at night.

[PAUSE]

Here is a critical detail. The practice hours are **in addition to** the six hours of behind-the-wheel instruction you receive during your required driver education course. They are completely separate. You cannot count the driver education hours toward your practice hours.

[PAUSE]

Memory trick time. Think of it this way. Under twenty-five, you need more time on the road because you have less overall driving experience. Think "under twenty-five, sixty is the number." For twenty-five and older, think "older and wiser, fourteen will do." And always remember, night hours are part of the total, not extra.

[PAUSE]

One more thing the manual emphasizes. Sixty hours is described as a **suggested minimum**. The manual says you may always practice more, and more practice leads to safer driving. So do not just aim for the minimum.

## [4:30] Section 2: What Goes in the Practice Log

Now let us talk about what information the practice log must record for each driving session. There are five required fields.

[PAUSE]

First, the **date** of the practice session. Second, the **start time**. Third, the **end time**. Fourth, the **skill or activity practiced**. And fifth, the **total number of hours completed**.

[PAUSE]

All five fields are required for every entry. The practice log is a legal document. It is signed under **penalty of perjury** when you submit it with your license application. This means that if you provide false information, you can be prosecuted.

[PAUSE]

Here is what the MVA exam might ask. They may ask what information the practice log must include, or they may ask about the legal significance of the log. Remember, it is not just a tracking tool. It is a legal certification.

[PAUSE]

Another important detail. The manual recommends that new drivers practice in **bad weather** when possible. This builds experience handling challenging conditions under supervision.

## [7:30] Section 3: Who Can Supervise

Your practice sessions must be supervised by a specific type of driver. The supervising driver must be a **valid license holder**, must be at least **twenty-one years of age**, and must have been licensed for at least **three years**.

[PAUSE]

All three requirements must be met. It is not enough to be twenty-one. It is not enough to have a valid license. The supervising driver must meet all three criteria.

[PAUSE]

The MVA exam may present a scenario where the supervising driver meets some requirements but not all. For example, a twenty-two-year-old who has only been licensed for two years. That person is not eligible to supervise because they do not meet the three-year licensing requirement.

[PAUSE]

Test day tip. When you see a question about supervising drivers, check all three requirements. Valid license, twenty-one or older, and licensed for at least three years. If any one is missing, the answer is no.

## [10:00] Section 4: Legal Implications

The practice certification is signed under **penalty of perjury**. This is one of the most important facts in this chapter. Falsely certifying that a driver has completed the required hours is a criminal offense.

[PAUSE]

If you certify that a driver has completed sixty hours but the log only shows fifty-eight, that is a false statement. It can result in prosecution and the cancellation of the driver's license. Even a small shortfall matters.

[PAUSE]

This is why accuracy in the practice log is so important. Every entry should be recorded at the time of the session, not estimated from memory later. Start time, end time, skills practiced, all of it should be documented accurately.

[PAUSE]

The manual is clear. More practice leads to safer driving. So aim to exceed the minimum requirements, not just meet them.

## [12:00] Quick Review

Let us do a rapid recap of the must-know facts.

[PAUSE]

Under twenty-five, sixty hours of practice with ten at night. Twenty-five and older, fourteen hours with three at night. Night hours are part of the total. Practice hours are in addition to six hours of driver education. The log records date, start time, end time, skills practiced, and total hours. Supervising driver must be valid license holder, twenty-one plus, licensed three plus years. Certification is signed under penalty of perjury. Practice in bad weather is recommended.

[PAUSE]

Test day tip. When the exam asks about practice hours, always identify the driver's age first to determine which requirement applies. Then check both total hours AND nighttime hours. Both must be met.
