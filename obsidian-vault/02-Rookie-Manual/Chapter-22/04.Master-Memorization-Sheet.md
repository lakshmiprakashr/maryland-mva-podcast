# Chapter 22 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

The Maryland practice log tracks all supervised driving hours required before a new driver can take the skills test. Drivers under 25 must log 60 hours (including 10 at night); drivers 25 and older must log 14 hours (including 3 at night). The log records date, start/end times, skills practiced, and total hours. Practice hours are in addition to the 6 hours from driver education.

## Why It Matters

Without a completed and accurate practice log, you cannot obtain your provisional license. The log is a legal document — falsifying it is perjury. The MVA tests your knowledge of exact hour requirements and the distinction between age groups.

## High-Yield Facts

- Under 25: **60 hours** minimum practice, **10 hours at night**
- 25 and older: **14 hours** minimum practice, **3 hours at night**
- Practice hours are **in addition to** the 6 hours from driver education
- Log tracks: **date, start time, end time, skill or activity practiced, total hours**
- 60 hours is a **suggested minimum** — you may always practice more
- Practice in **bad weather** is recommended when possible
- More practice leads to **safer driving**
- The practice log must be **completed honestly** — it is part of the license application
- The supervising driver must be a **valid license holder, 21+, licensed for at least 3 years**

## MVA Test Traps

- **Trap:** Confusing the hour requirements. Under 25 = 60 hours. 25+ = 14 hours. The MVA will swap these.
- **Trap:** Thinking the 6 hours of driver education counts toward the practice hours. It does not — practice hours are **in addition to** driver education.
- **Trap:** Believing night driving hours are included in the total. Night hours are part of the total (10 of the 60, or 3 of the 14).
- **Trap:** Thinking the practice log is optional. It is a **required** document for license application.

## Common Mistakes

- Confusing "60 hours" with "14 hours" — use the age threshold of 25
- Forgetting that night driving hours are a **subset** of total hours, not additional
- Not logging bad weather driving when recommended
- Assuming the supervising driver can be any licensed adult — they must be 21+ and licensed for at least 3 years

## Memory Tricks

- **"Under 25 = 60"** — think "under 25, you need more time on the road"
- **"25 and up = 14"** — think "older drivers need less practice but still need some"
- **"10 of 60" and "3 of 14"** — night hours are always a fraction of the total
- **"In addition to"** — driver education hours are separate, always
- **"More practice = safer driving"** — the manual's own words

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

No specific road signs in this chapter. Focus on practice hour requirements and log documentation.

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- Practice hour requirements before skills test: 60 hrs (under 25) or 14 hrs (25+)
- Night driving: 10 hours (under 25) or 3 hours (25+) as part of total
- Supervising driver: valid license holder, age 21+, licensed for at least 3 years
- Practice hours are in addition to the 6 hours from driver education
- Falsifying practice log information is illegal (considered part of license application)
