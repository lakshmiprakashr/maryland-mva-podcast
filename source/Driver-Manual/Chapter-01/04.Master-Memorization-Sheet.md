# Chapter 01 — Driving Tests Requirements — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Maryland requires three licensing tests — vision screening, knowledge test, and driving skills test — before you can legally drive. The Graduated Licensing System (GLS) walks first-time drivers through a Learner's Permit, Provisional License, and finally a full Driver's License. Knowing what documents to bring, vehicle requirements for the road test, and permit restrictions will save you time at the MVA.

## Why It Matters

Every question on the Maryland knowledge test comes from the Driver's Manual. Chapter 1 sets the foundation: if you don't know the licensing process, you may show up unprepared, miss documents, or fail to meet test requirements. MVA examiners also test your knowledge of these requirements on the driving skills test.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Three licensing requirements:** Vision screening, knowledge test, driving skills test
- **Vision standards:** Binocular vision, 20/40 acuity in each eye, 140-degree continuous field of vision
- **Knowledge test:** 25 questions, 20 minutes, taken at an MVA location
- **Driving skills test:** Includes public road driving AND a special course at the testing location
- **Vehicle requirements for road test:** Up-to-date plates/registration, proof of insurance, safe to drive, no windshield cracks, at least half tank of gas
- **Supervising driver:** Must accompany you to the test site (Section 10 details who qualifies)
- **Only allowed in vehicle during test:** You, the examiner, and an MVA-approved interpreter (if needed)
- **Type 1 Learner's Permit:** For first-time drivers, valid up to 2 years
- **Type 2 Learner's Permit:** For drivers changing license class, valid up to 180 days, must be 16 years 6 months old
- **Minimum age for Learner's Permit:** 15 years 9 months (applicants under 25)
- **Parent/guardian co-sign required:** If applicant is under 18
- **DL-300 form required:** If applicant is under 16 (Learner's Permit School Attendance Certification)
- **Noncommercial license:** Also called a Class C license — covers cars, pickup trucks, vans
- **Required documents at MVA:** Age proof with full name, lawful status proof (if born outside U.S.), SSN or proof of ineligibility, TWO documents proving Maryland residency
- **Document rules:** Originals or certified copies only — no photocopies, notarized copies, or altered documents
- **Reportable medical conditions:** State law requires you to notify MVA of conditions affecting driving ability
- **Littering fines:** Up to $1,500 or 30 days in prison
- **Driver education:** Required while holding a Learner's Permit (Maryland Driver Education Program)
- **Appointment scheduling:** Online at mva.maryland.gov or call 410-768-7000 / 1-800-492-4575 (TTY)

## MVA Test Traps

| Trap | Reality |
|------|---------|
| "Knowledge test has 20 questions" | It has **25** questions |
| "You have 30 minutes for the knowledge test" | You have **20** minutes |
| "Vision screening only done at MVA" | Your **eye doctor** can also do it |
| "Any car can be used for the driving test" | It must meet ALL requirements: plates, registration, insurance, safe condition, half tank of gas, no windshield cracks |
| "Your friends can ride along during the road test" | **Only you and the examiner** (plus MVA-approved interpreter if needed) |
| "Type 2 Permit lasts 2 years" | Type 2 lasts **180 days**; Type 1 lasts 2 years |
| "You can drive alone with a Learner's Permit" | You need a supervising driver at all times |
| "Photocopies of documents are fine" | MVA rejects photocopies — bring originals or certified copies |
| "Licensing requirements are only for new drivers" | Some people must meet **additional** requirements depending on medical history |
| "You bring the MVA's car to the road test" | You bring **your own** vehicle |

## Common Mistakes

1. **Confusing Type 1 and Type 2 Permits** — Type 1 = first-time drivers (2 years); Type 2 = changing license class (180 days, age 16.5+)
2. **Forgetting the half-tank of gas rule** — vehicle must have at least a half tank; running low will disqualify you
3. **Bringing wrong documents** — must be originals or certified copies, not photocopies
4. **Underestimating the vision requirement** — 20/40 in EACH eye, not combined; 140-degree field of vision
5. **Not knowing who can be in the car** — only you and the examiner (plus approved interpreter); supervising driver comes but stays outside
6. **Thinking the knowledge test covers only road signs** — it also covers motor vehicle laws and safe driving practices
7. **Skipping the Driver Education Program** — required while holding a Learner's Permit for applicants under 25

## Memory Tricks

- **"25 in 20"** — 25 questions in 20 minutes on the knowledge test
- **"20/40, 140"** — vision: 20/40 acuity, 140-degree field
- **"Half tank = full test"** — half tank of gas minimum for road test
- **"Type 1 = 2 years, Type 2 = 180 days"** — Type 1 is longer because it's for new drivers
- **"15-9-18"** — minimum age 15 years 9 months, parent co-sign if under 18
- **"O-N-E" for documents:** Originals, Notarized copies (NO), Enhanced/certified copies (YES) — the MVA only accepts originals or certified copies
- **"Eyes, Brain, Wheels"** — the three tests: Vision, Knowledge, Skills

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

This chapter focuses on licensing requirements rather than road signs. Key knowledge for the test:

- **MVA** — Maryland Department of Transportation Motor Vehicle Administration
- **GLS / GDL** — Graduated Licensing System (sometimes called Graduated Driver Licensing)
- **Class C** — Noncommercial driver's license (regular vehicles)
- **Type 1 Permit** — First-time learner's permit (2 years)
- **Type 2 Permit** — License class change permit (180 days)
- **DL-300** — Learner's Permit School Attendance Certification form
- **TTY** — Telecommunications Device for the Deaf (1-800-492-4575)
- **Supervising driver** — Qualified adult who must accompany permit holders (Section 10)

## Maryland-Specific Laws

1. **Licensing requirement (State law):** You MUST have a valid driver's license before operating any motor vehicle (cars, vans, pick-up trucks)
2. **Vision screening requirement:** Binocular vision, 20/40 acuity each eye, 140-degree continuous field
3. **Glasses/contacts restriction:** If required for vision, your license will carry a restriction — you MUST wear them while driving
4. **Reportable medical conditions:** Maryland state law requires disclosure of medical conditions affecting driving ability (Section 10)
5. **GLS applies to ALL ages:** The graduated licensing system is not just for teens — it applies to anyone who has never held a driver's license
6. **Parent/guardian co-sign:** Required for applicants under 18
7. **Driver Education Program:** Mandatory for permit holders under 25
8. **Document requirements:** originals or certified copies only — photocopies, notarized copies, and altered documents are rejected
9. **Littering penalties:** Up to $1,500 fine or 30 days in prison (Maryland state roads)
