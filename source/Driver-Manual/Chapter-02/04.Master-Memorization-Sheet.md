# Chapter 02 - Licensing Requirements and Process - Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Maryland uses a Graduated Driver Licensing (GDL) System to get new drivers from permit to full license in three stages: Learner's Instructional Permit, Provisional License, and full Driver's License. Each stage has specific age, time, practice hour, and violation requirements. This chapter also covers required documents, co-signer rules for minors, out-of-country license transfers, and MVA accessibility accommodations.

## Why It Matters

Understanding the GDL system is critical for passing the Maryland knowledge test. The MVA heavily tests on specific numbers (ages, hours, days), the differences between permit types, and the conditions for each licensing stage. Confusing these details is the number one reason students fail on Chapter 02 questions.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Class C License** = noncommercial driver's license for regular vehicles (cars, pickups, vans)
- **GDL System** applies to anyone who has **never held a driver's license**, regardless of age
- **Three stages:** Learner's Permit -> Provisional License -> Driver's License
- **Type 1 Learner's Permit:** For first-time applicants. Valid up to **2 years**. Requires vision screening + knowledge test.
- **Type 2 Learner's Permit:** For existing license holders switching classes. Must be **at least 16 years 6 months old**. Valid up to **180 days**. Can test after **14 days**.
- **Under 25 practice hours:** **60 hours** total, **10 hours** must be at night (30 min before sunset to 30 min after sunrise)
- **25 and older practice hours:** **14 hours** total, **3 hours** must be at night
- **Permit hold time:** Under 25 = **45 days** minimum. With violations = up to **9 months**
- **Provisional License duration:** Minimum **18 months** before eligible for full license
- **Full license eligibility:** Age **18+**, had Provisional for **18 months**, no moving violations in past 18 months
- MVA **automatically** switches Provisional to full Driver's License when requirements are met

## MVA Test Traps

1. **Type 1 vs Type 2 Permit confusion** - Type 1 is for first-timers (2 years, vision + knowledge test only). Type 2 is for existing license holders wanting a different class (180 days, must be 16.5+). The MVA loves swapping details between these two.

2. **Practice hours for under 25 vs 25+** - Under 25 needs 60 hours (10 night). 25+ needs only 14 hours (3 night). Students often mix up the night hour counts or forget that 25 is the cutoff age, not 18.

3. **Violation hold times** - Moving violation conviction or PBJ at ANY age = 9 months hold. Under 18 clean record = 9 months. Age 18 with diploma/GED or ages 19-24 clean record = 3 months. The MVA tests whether you know when it is 9 months versus 3 months.

4. **Provisional License automatic switch** - Students think they must go back to the MVA. The MVA does it **automatically** and mails a card. You must carry that card with your Provisional License.

5. **Co-signer cancellation** - Co-signer cancels by submitting a **written request** to MVA's **Administrative Adjudication Division**. License stays cancelled until 18 unless a new co-signer signs.

## Common Mistakes

- Forgetting that the **45-day minimum permit hold** only applies to applicants 25 and older who are clean. Violations extend this dramatically.
- Thinking the GDL only applies to teenagers. It applies to **all ages** if you have never held a license.
- Confusing "Probation Before Judgment" (PBJ) with a conviction. Both count equally for the 9-month hold.
- Assuming you need to visit the MVA to get your Provisional switched to a full license. It is automatic.
- Missing that under-16 applicants need the **DL-300** School Attendance Certification form.

## Memory Tricks

- **"60 before 25, 14 after"** - Under 25 needs 60 practice hours. 25 and older needs only 14.
- **"10 and 3 at night"** - Under 25 needs 10 night hours. 25+ needs 3 night hours.
- **"Type 1 = First-timer, Type 2 = Transfer"** - Type 1 is for new drivers. Type 2 is for license class changers.
- **"2 years, 180 days"** - Type 1 lasts up to 2 years. Type 2 lasts up to 180 days.
- **"9 for under, 3 for old enough"** - Under 18 with clean record = 9 months. 18+ with diploma or 19-24 = 3 months.
- **"18 months provisional, then it is automatic"** - Provisional lasts at least 18 months, then MVA auto-converts.

## Documents Required to Apply

| Document | Details |
|----------|---------|
| Proof of age + full name | Birth certificate or passport |
| Lawful status proof | Required if born outside the United States |
| Social Security Number | SSN or proof of ineligibility |
| Maryland residency | **Two** documents proving Maryland residence |

**Key rule:** Only original or agency-certified copies accepted. No photocopies. No notarized copies. No altered documents.

## Co-Signer Rules for Minors (Under 18)

- Parent or legal guardian must co-sign
- **Proof:** Original or certified birth certificate with parent's name, OR court guardianship documents
- **Alternative co-signers:** Spouse (18+) with marriage proof, or responsible adult (with proof like death certificates or emancipation papers)
- **Emancipation** = court has ruled minor is no longer under parental control
- Co-signer **can cancel** via written request to MVA Administrative Adjudication Division
- License stays cancelled until age 18 unless new co-signer is obtained

## Out-of-Country License Transfer

Must complete **all four:**
1. Vision screening
2. Knowledge test
3. Driving skills test
4. Three-hour MVA-approved roadway safety education program

**If license is not in English:** Must have International License OR English translation by MVA-approved provider, court translator, or country's embassy. Translation must include translator's printed name, phone number, and signature.

## MVA Accessibility Accommodations

- **Foreign language:** Interpreter allowed after attempting knowledge test **five times** in available language
- **Disability accommodations:** Extra time, ASL support, oral testing, screen readers, headphones, visual aids
- **Developmental Disability Disclosure Card:** Voluntary blue card, available at any MVA location, no documentation required
- **Interpreter rules:** Must show valid photo ID, cannot provide other services (certify residence, run driving school, issue leases)
