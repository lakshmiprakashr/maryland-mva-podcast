# Chapter-05 - Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary
Pavement markings (line types and meanings), driving conditions (reduced visibility, night, fog, weather, snow/ice), skidding, hydroplaning, railroad crossings, work zones, roundabouts, interstate driving, funeral processions, slow-moving vehicles, and alcohol/drugs.

## Why It Matters
This chapter covers real-world driving scenarios tested heavily on the Maryland knowledge exam. Pavement markings, weather conditions, and DUI laws are frequent exam topics.

## High-Yield Facts
### Pavement Markings
- **Single broken white**: Separates same-direction traffic; lane change permitted
- **Single broken yellow**: Center line on 2-lane 2-way road; passing permitted with care
- **Single solid white**: Right edge of road; lane change discouraged
- **Single solid yellow**: Left edge on divided highways, one-way roads, ramps
- **Double broken yellow**: Reversible lane edges
- **Double solid white**: No lane change allowed; separates general from HOV lanes
- **Double solid yellow**: No passing in either direction; left turns across allowed (e.g., into driveway)
- **Solid plus broken yellow**: Passing allowed only on broken-line side
- **Dotted yellow or white**: Continues lane lines through intersections
- **Stop line**: White solid line where you must stop for stop sign or red light
- **Yield lines**: Rows of small triangles showing where to yield
- **Crosswalk lines**: White solid lines showing where pedestrians cross

### Driving Conditions
- **Headlights**: Required when you cannot see 1,000 feet ahead; required when using windshield wipers
- **Low beams**: Normal driving, fog, rain, snow, tunnels, construction areas
- **High beams**: Roads without streetlights; switch to low beams 500 feet before oncoming vehicles, 300 feet when following
- **Night driving**: Use headlights; do not stare into oncoming headlights; look to the right edge of your lane; increase following distance
- **Fog**: Reduce speed; use low beams only; if fog is too thick, pull off road and use emergency flashers
- **Inclement weather**: Keep safe distance (greater on wet roads); reduce speed; good tire tread; avoid slamming brakes; pump brakes if no ABS
- **Snow/ice**: No completely safe speed; slow down; clear windows/lights; start slowly; test brakes gently; start slowing early for intersections; keep gas tank full
- **Black ice**: Thin layer of ice on blacktop; hard to see; caused by melting and refreezing
- **Four-wheel drive**: Can also slide on ice; not a guarantee of safety

### Skidding
- Release accelerator or brake
- Steer in the direction you are skidding
- Look where you want to go
- Brake only after regaining traction
- Slowly accelerating may help if back wheels lose traction

### Hydroplaning
- Tires ride on a sheet of water; lose contact with road
- Prevention: good tires, reduce speed on wet roads
- If hydroplaning: foot off gas, do not brake, keep going straight, slow down gradually

### Railroad Crossings
- Trains cannot stop quickly; use extra caution
- Buses, school buses, hazmat trucks must stop before every crossing
- Look for trains from both directions
- If gates are down or lights flashing: stop and wait
- After train passes: look both ways before crossing
- Never start crossing unless you can get completely over
- Emergency on tracks: get out of vehicle, move away from tracks
- Blue and white ENS sign for reporting emergencies

### Work Zones
- Orange is the color for work zones
- Flashing amber/yellow = workers present
- Flashing blue = speed cameras active
- Flashing green = highway maintenance vehicles
- Speeding fines are higher in work zones, even higher with workers present
- Stay alert, watch speed, expect the unexpected, minimize distractions, obey flagger

### Roundabouts
- Yield to traffic already in the circle
- Reduce speed, keep right of island
- Yield to pedestrians in crosswalk
- Multi-lane: stay in lane; signal right when exiting; move to outside lane after passing the exit before yours

### Interstate Driving
- Enter via entrance ramp and acceleration lane
- Do not cross solid lines dividing entrance from highway
- Slower traffic must drive in right lane
- Exit: move to right lane well before exit; slow down in deceleration lane
- Never stop and back up on highway
- Emergency stop on shoulder only; use flashers; stay in vehicle

### Funeral Processions
- Must turn on headlights and flashing hazard lights
- May go through red signal with lights on
- Other vehicles must yield even if they have green signal

### Slow-Moving Vehicles
- Slow-moving sign on back means 25 mph or less
- Be aware, change speed/position, pass only when safe
- Use emergency flashers if following and waiting to pass

### Alcohol and Drugs
- Maryland BAC limit: .08 or higher = DUI
- Any amount of alcohol can affect driving ability
- Under 21: zero alcohol tolerance; license suspended/revoked
- Open container law: no open alcohol in passenger area
- CDS (drugs): illegal, some prescription, and inhalants can impair driving; license suspended/revoked
- Read prescription labels for driving warnings

## MVA Test Traps
- "Double solid yellow lines mean you cannot turn left across them" — you CAN make left turns across double solid yellow (e.g., into a driveway)
- "You can use high beams in fog" — use LOW beams only in fog; high beams reflect back
- "Four-wheel drive vehicles are safe on ice" — they can also slide; always drive carefully
- "You can stop on the interstate shoulder for any reason" — only for disabled vehicles or emergencies
- "Flashing blue lights in work zone mean workers present" — flashing blue means speed cameras active; amber/yellow means workers present
- "A solid white line means lane change is prohibited" — solid white means lane change is discouraged, not prohibited

## Common Mistakes
- Using high beams in fog (reflects back and reduces visibility)
- Slamming brakes on wet/snowy roads (causes skidding)
- Not clearing ice/snow from vehicle before driving
- Not increasing following distance in bad weather
- Starting to cross railroad tracks without room on the other side
- Speeding through work zones
- Not yielding to traffic already in a roundabout

## Memory Tricks
- **Pavement markings**: "White on Right, Yellow on Left" — white lines mark right edge, yellow lines mark left edge
- **Skid response**: "Gas, Steer, Look" — release gas, steer in skid direction, look where you want to go
- **Hydroplaning**: "Gas off, brakes off, straight and slow" — foot off gas, do not brake, keep straight, slow gradually
- **Work zone lights**: "Amber = Workers, Blue = Cameras, Green = Maintenance"
- **BAC limit**: "Point oh eight is too late" — .08 is the legal limit
- **High beams**: "Five for oncoming, three for following" — 500 feet for oncoming, 300 feet when following

## Maryland-Specific Laws
- Maryland law requires headlights when using windshield wipers in inclement weather
- Maryland BAC limit: .08 or higher
- Zero alcohol tolerance for drivers under 21
- Open container law prohibits open alcohol in passenger area
- Speeding fines are higher in work zones; even higher when workers present
- Funeral processions may go through red signals with headlights and hazard lights on
- Buses, school buses, and hazmat trucks must stop at every railroad crossing

## Rapid Recall Questions
1. What do white pavement markings indicate? → Right edge of road or same-direction traffic
2. What do yellow pavement markings indicate? → Left edge of road or opposite-direction traffic
3. When must you use low beam headlights instead of high beams? → In fog, rain, snow, tunnels, and construction areas
4. How far before an oncoming vehicle must you switch to low beams? → 500 feet
5. What should you do if your vehicle begins to skid? → Release gas/brake, steer in skid direction, look where you want to go
6. What is black ice and when does it form? → Thin ice on blacktop from melting and refreezing; hard to see
7. What vehicles must stop at every railroad crossing? → Buses, school buses, and trucks carrying hazardous materials
8. What does a flashing blue light in a work zone mean? → Speed cameras are active
