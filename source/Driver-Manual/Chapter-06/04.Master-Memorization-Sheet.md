# Chapter-06 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Covers four dangerous driving behaviors: impaired driving (alcohol, drugs, CDS), aggressive driving and road rage, distracted driving (cell phones, other activities), and drowsy driving. Each behavior significantly increases crash risk and carries legal penalties.

## Why It Matters

These behaviors are leading causes of crashes, injuries, and fatalities on Maryland roads. Understanding the laws and risks helps you pass the MVA test and drives home life-saving decisions every day.

## High-Yield Facts

- **BAC limit:** Maryland legal limit is .08 — but ANY amount of alcohol can impair driving and lead to criminal charges
- **Under 21:** Zero tolerance — any alcohol = license suspension/revocation plus possible criminal charges
- **Open container:** Illegal to drive with open alcohol in the passenger area; a six-pack with missing bottles counts
- **CDS:** Illegal drugs, some prescriptions, and inhalants can suspend/revote your license; always check drug labels for driving warnings
- **Child in vehicle:** Drunk/drugged driving conviction with a child = doubled fine and jail time
- **Aggressive driving examples:** Speeding, tailgating, ignoring signals, unsafe lane changes, not yielding, unsafe passing
- **Distracted driving:** Causes more police-reported crashes than anything else — one-third of fatal crashes
- **Cell phones:** Hand-held use (including texting) illegal while driving; under 18 = no cell phone use at all (except 911)
- **Drowsy driving:** Impairs judgment, slows reaction time — same as drugs/alcohol
- **Emergency vehicles:** Move to edge of road, stay out of intersections, remain stopped until passed

## MVA Test Traps

- Thinking .08 is the "safe" limit — any amount impairs you and can result in charges
- Believing hands-free phone use is always legal — drivers under 18 cannot use phones at all, even hands-free
- Confusing open container law — a six-pack with missing bottles is an open container
- Forgetting that drowsy driving is compared to impaired driving on the exam
- Thinking aggressive driving is just "road rage" — it's a broader category including speeding, tailgating, etc.

## Common Mistakes

- Thinking you can estimate your own BAC accurately — weight, food, fatigue, and mood all affect it
- Assuming prescription drugs are always safe to drive on — read labels for warnings
- Not knowing that distracted driving includes eating, adjusting radio, dealing with children
- Forgetting that drivers under 21 face license suspension for any alcohol
- Thinking you can pass a stopped emergency vehicle on the same side of the road

## Memory Tricks

- **BAC = .08** → "Point-oh-EIGHT, don't drive late"
- **Under 21 = ZERO** → "Zero tolerance, under twenty-one"
- **Open container** → "Any open bottle, can, or missing piece of a six-pack = trouble"
- **Aggressive driving** → Think: Speeding, Tailgating, Ignoring signals, Unsafe lane changes, Not yielding, Unsafe passing = "STI-UN-U"
- **Distracted driving** → Eyes, Hands, Mind — if any leave driving, you're distracted
- **Drowsy signs** → "Eyes close, head drops, yawning won't stop, thoughts wander, miles forgotten, exit missed, lane drifts, speed changes"

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- BAC legal limit: .08
- Zero tolerance under 21
- Open container law covers passenger area (driver + passenger seats + reachable areas)
- CDS driving = license suspension or revocation
- Child present during impaired offense = doubled fine and jail
- Hand-held cell phone use illegal while driving
- Drivers under 18: no cell phone use while driving, even hands-free (except 911)
- Emergency vehicles: pull to edge, stay out of intersections, do not pass
- Move over law: must move over or slow down for stopped emergency/service vehicles with flashing lights
