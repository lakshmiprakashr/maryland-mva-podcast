# Chapter 12 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

This chapter covers the shared left turn lane in depth, including entering a driveway, entering traffic, and using multiple turn lanes. It also introduces roundabouts, driving with bicyclists, and the Coach's Checklist for advancing to the next skill level.

## Why It Matters

Shared left turn lanes reduce head-on collisions and improve traffic flow. Using them incorrectly puts you directly in the path of oncoming traffic. Roundabouts are becoming more common in Maryland and require specific yielding rules. Bicyclists share the road and must be protected.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Signal 3 to 4 seconds** before moving into the shared left turn lane
- **Enter shared lane no more than 2 to 3 seconds** before your turn
- **When entering traffic:** signal left, stop at edge, check left and right, switch to right signal if needed
- **Multiple turn lanes:** identify your lane 1 to 2 blocks ahead
- **Roundabout rules:** slow down, yield to traffic from your LEFT, yield to pedestrians and bicyclists, signal right as you approach your exit
- **Bicyclists:** maintain a 3-foot space cushion on Maryland roads
- **Coach's Checklist:** must pass all items before advancing to the next skill set
- **Trucks and buses** need more room to turn

## MVA Test Traps

- **Shared lane as a driving lane:** You CANNOT drive in the shared left turn lane — it is only for turning
- **Roundabout yielding direction:** You yield to traffic coming from your LEFT, not your right
- **Roundabout signal timing:** Signal right only as you approach your exit, not when entering
- **Bicycle distance:** It is 3 feet, not 4 feet or 2 feet
- **Entering traffic procedure:** Students forget to switch from left to right signal when moving into the lane from the shared turn lane

## Common Mistakes

- Using the shared left turn lane as a regular travel lane
- Failing to signal before entering the shared lane
- Entering the shared lane too far in advance of the turn
- Yielding to the wrong direction in a roundabout
- Forgetting to signal right when exiting a roundabout
- Not maintaining the required 3-foot distance from bicyclists

## Memory Tricks

- **"2 to 3 for me"** — enter the shared left turn lane 2 to 3 seconds before your turn
- **"Signal, then Steer"** — signal 3 to 4 seconds before moving into the shared lane
- **"Left Yield Round"** — in a roundabout, yield to traffic from your LEFT
- **"3 feet on the street"** — maintain 3 feet from bicyclists
- **"Exit Right"** — signal right as you approach your roundabout exit

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

- **Two-way left turn lane sign** — yellow diamond with opposing arrows
- **Roundabout warning sign** — yellow diamond with circular arrows
- **Yield signs** at roundabout entrances
- **Bicycle warning signs** — yellow diamond with bicycle symbol
- **Pavement markings:** solid yellow outer lines with dashed yellow inner lines for shared left turn lane

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **Shared Left Turn Lane (MD Vehicle Law 21-702):** May only be used for making left turns into or out of driveways and for entering traffic
- **Three-Foot Passing Law:** Minimum 3 feet clearance when passing a bicycle
- **Roundabout Yield Rules:** Drivers entering the roundabout must yield to traffic already circulating from the left
- **Coach's Checklist:** Parent-supervised drivers must demonstrate proficiency at each skill level before advancing
