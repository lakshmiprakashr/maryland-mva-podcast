# Chapter 20 - Audio Review Script

## [0:00] Introduction

Welcome to your audio review for Maryland Driver's Manual chapter twenty, covering **crash procedures** and **driving under the influence laws**. [PAUSE]

This is one of the most important chapters in the entire manual. We will walk through exactly what to do after a crash in every scenario, and then cover Maryland's impaired driving laws including the critical BAC numbers you must know for the MVA test. [PAUSE]

Let us start with crash reporting. [LONG PAUSE]

## [1:30] Section 1: When to Call Law Enforcement

After a crash, your first question is whether you need to call law enforcement. The manual lists six situations where you **must** always contact them. [PAUSE]

One, someone has been injured. Two, a vehicle cannot be moved. Three, a driver appears to be under the influence. Four, a driver does not have a license. Five, a driver tries to leave the scene without giving proper information. And six, public property has been damaged. [PAUSE]

That is six situations. Remember them. The MVA test will ask about them. [EMPHASIS: six situations] [LONG PAUSE]

## [3:30] Section 2: Crash Scenarios

Now let us break down the three different crash scenarios the manual covers. [PAUSE]

**Scenario one. Someone is injured.** Call nine-one-one immediately. Stay at the scene until help arrives. And do **not** move the vehicles. Injured people may have spinal or internal injuries that could be made worse by movement. Emergency responders need the scene preserved. [EMPHASIS: do not move] [PAUSE]

**Scenario two. No one is injured, but your vehicle cannot move.** Call nine-one-one and seek assistance. Move yourself to a safe location away from traffic. Use your emergency flashers or flares to warn other motorists. [PAUSE]

**Scenario three. No one is injured and your vehicle can move.** Move your vehicle out of the travel portion of the road. Then collect all the required information. [LONG PAUSE]

## [6:00] Section 3: Information to Collect

For that third scenario, here is exactly what you need to collect from the other driver. [PAUSE]

Their **name**, **address**, **phone number**, **insurance information**, **vehicle license plate number**, and the **make, model, and year** of their vehicle if possible. [PAUSE]

Also get the names and contact phone numbers of any **witnesses** to the crash. Witness statements can be critical for insurance claims and legal proceedings. [PAUSE]

Two more special situations. If you strike and injure a **domestic animal**, you must notify law enforcement. If you strike an **unattended vehicle or property**, you must attempt to find the owner and provide your contact information. If you cannot find the owner, leave your information in a secure place and notify your insurance company. [LONG PAUSE]

## [8:30] Section 4: Impaired Driving Laws

Now let us cover Maryland's impaired driving laws. These are some of the most tested topics on the Maryland knowledge test. [PAUSE]

Let us start with **drivers under twenty-one**. Maryland has **zero tolerance** for alcohol. That means you can receive a citation for consuming one drink and driving. You are not legally old enough to drink, so any alcohol while driving is a violation. [EMPHASIS: zero tolerance] [PAUSE]

Now for drivers **twenty-one and over**. Maryland has two separate impaired driving charges. [PAUSE]

**Driving While Impaired**, or DWI, is when your Blood Alcohol Content, or BAC, is between **.07 and .079**. [PAUSE]

**Driving Under the Influence**, or DUI, is when your BAC is **.08 or above**. [PAUSE]

Both DWI and DUI are extremely serious charges. They may result in you losing your privilege to drive for an extended period. [LONG PAUSE]

## [11:00] Section 5: Implied Consent and Permanent Records

Here is a concept that surprises many people. It is called **Implied Consent**. [PAUSE]

By signing and accepting your Learner's Instructional Permit, Provisional License, or full Driver's License, you also agree to be tested for the presence of drugs and alcohol when requested by a law enforcement official. [PAUSE]

This means that by getting any Maryland driver's license, you have already agreed to take a breathalyzer or other test if an officer asks. Refusing to comply may result in the loss of your driving privilege. [EMPHASIS: may result in loss] [PAUSE]

And here is one more critical fact. DUI, DWI, and Refusal to Test **cannot** be expunged from your driving record. These entries are permanent. [EMPHASIS: cannot be expunged] [LONG PAUSE]

## [12:30] Quick Review

Let us do a rapid recap of everything we covered. [PAUSE]

Six situations require law enforcement: injuries, immovable vehicle, DUI, no license, hit-and-run, and public property damage. [PAUSE]

Injury crash: call nine-one-one, stay at scene, do not move vehicles. [PAUSE]

No injuries but can move: move out of travel lane, collect information. [PAUSE]

Under twenty-one: zero tolerance for alcohol. [PAUSE]

DWI threshold: .07 BAC. DUI threshold: .08 BAC. [PAUSE]

Implied Consent: getting any Maryland license means you agree to testing. [PAUSE]

Refusing a test may result in license loss. [PAUSE]

DUI, DWI, and refusal cannot be removed from your record. [LONG PAUSE]

## [13:30] Closing

You have now reviewed two of the most critical topics in the Maryland Driver's Manual. Crash procedures protect you legally and ensure proper documentation. Impaired driving laws exist because impaired driving is one of the leading causes of fatal crashes. [PAUSE]

The numbers to remember are zero for under twenty-one, .07 for DWI, and .08 for DUI. These appear on the Maryland knowledge test regularly. [PAUSE]

If any of these concepts need reinforcement, replay this audio. You are building the knowledge to be a safe, law-abiding driver. Good luck on your Maryland knowledge test. [LONG PAUSE]

## Pronunciation Guide

- **MVA** (em-vee-AY) — Motor Vehicle Administration
- **GDL** (jee-dee-EL) — Graduated Driver Licensing
- **DUI** (dee-you-EYE) — Driving Under the Influence
- **DWI** (dee-dub-lyu-EYE) — Driving While Impaired
- **BAC** (bee-ay-SEE) — Blood Alcohol Content
- **Implied Consent** (im-PLAID con-SENT) — agreement to testing by accepting a license
- **Expunged** (eks-PUNJD) — removed or erased from a record
