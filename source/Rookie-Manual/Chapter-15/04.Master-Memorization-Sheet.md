# Chapter 15 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

This chapter covers passing another vehicle, being passed, proper following distance measurement, night driving rules including high beam and low beam usage, and the GDL night driving practice requirement.

## Why It Matters

Improper passing is one of the leading causes of head-on collisions. Night driving accounts for a disproportionate number of fatal crashes, especially for teen drivers. Understanding when to use high beams versus low beams and how to maintain proper following distance saves lives.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Passing steps:** maintain proper interval, select adequate gap, signal, check mirrors and head check, steer into passing lane and accelerate, return to original lane when entire front of passed vehicle is visible in rear-view mirror
- **You cannot exceed the posted speed limit even when passing**
- **Being passed:** maintain speed and lane position, check for oncoming traffic hazards, slow if necessary to let the passing vehicle reenter
- **Following distance:** 3 to 4 seconds under ideal conditions; measure by counting when vehicle ahead passes a stationary object
- **Increase following distance** in bad weather and at night
- **Night driving:** check all lights, clean windows and headlights, low beams in fog and with oncoming traffic, adjust rear-view mirror for glare, avoid interior lights while driving, keep eyes moving, increase following distance, take breaks for fatigue
- **GDL requires 10 hours of night driving practice**
- **High beams reflect off fog** and reduce visibility

## MVA Test Traps

- **Speed limit while passing:** You CANNOT exceed the speed limit even when passing. This is a very common test question.
- **When to return to your lane:** Only when the entire front of the passed vehicle is visible in your rear-view mirror. Not just the front bumper.
- **Following distance measurement:** Count seconds from when the vehicle ahead passes a stationary object to when you reach the same object.
- **High beams in fog:** They reduce visibility instead of improving it.
- **Interior lights at night:** Do NOT use interior lights while driving. Pull over if you need to check something.

## Common Mistakes

- Exceeding the speed limit while passing
- Returning to the original lane too soon before fully passing
- Not checking for oncoming traffic before passing
- Using high beams in fog
- Not cleaning windows and headlights before night driving
- Using interior lights while driving at night
- Not increasing following distance at night
- Driving while fatigued without taking breaks

## Memory Tricks

- **"See the whole front"** — you can return to your lane when the entire front of the passed vehicle is visible in your rear-view mirror
- **"Speed limit means limit"** — even when passing, you cannot exceed the posted speed
- **"Count the seconds"** — use a stationary object to measure following distance
- **"Low in Fog"** — use low beams in fog, not high beams
- **"Pull over for the light"** — do not use interior lights while driving; pull over first

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

- **No Passing Zone sign** — pennant-shaped yellow sign with black text
- **Double solid yellow lines** — no passing allowed from either direction
- **Broken yellow line** — passing allowed from your side if safe
- **Solid yellow on your side, broken on the other** — passing allowed only from the other direction
- **High beam indicator** — blue dashboard light when high beams are active

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **Speed Limit During Passing (MD Vehicle Law 21-702):** Drivers must not exceed the posted speed limit even when overtaking another vehicle
- **Following Distance:** Minimum 3 to 4 seconds under ideal conditions; increase in adverse conditions
- **High Beam Usage (MD Vehicle Law 22-401):** Must dim high beams when approaching oncoming vehicles within 500 feet and when following another vehicle within 200 feet
- **GDL Night Driving:** Minimum 10 hours of supervised night driving required
- **Interior Light Restriction:** Maryland manual advises against using interior lights while driving
