# Chapter 25 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

The final chapter congratulates new drivers on completing their required hours and driver education, and provides critical information about the skills test and ongoing safety. It emphasizes that the first year of independent driving is the most hazardous and that coaches should continue monitoring new drivers. It includes MVA contact information and reminds both driver and coach that safe driving is a lifelong commitment.

## Why It Matters

This chapter reinforces that getting a provisional license is not the finish line — it is the beginning of independent driving. The first year is statistically the most dangerous. Coaches must continue their role. The MVA exam tests your knowledge of MVA resources, the graduated licensing system's purpose, and the ongoing responsibilities of new drivers.

## High-Yield Facts

- The first year of independent driving is the **most hazardous** for any new driver
- More than **400 people** are killed annually on Maryland roads
- New drivers should **practice and stay calm** on the day of the skills test
- The driving test video is available at **mva.maryland.gov**
- Coaches should **continue monitoring** new drivers after they get their provisional license
- Coaches should **continue riding with** new drivers periodically
- The **Coach-New Driver Agreement** should be maintained and updated as the driver gains experience
- Safe driving is a **commitment for life** for both driver and coach
- **Maryland Graduated Licensing System** limitations must continue to be followed after licensure

## MVA Test Traps

- **Trap:** Thinking the coach's job is done after the provisional license is obtained. The manual explicitly states coaching should continue.
- **Trap:** Believing the provisional license means unrestricted driving. Restrictions still apply (nighttime, passengers, phone for under 18).
- **Trap:** Not knowing the MVA website or contact information. The exam may reference mva.maryland.gov for resources.
- **Trap:** Thinking 400 annual fatalities is a low number. Maryland roads are statistically dangerous for new drivers.

## Common Mistakes

- Assuming that passing the skills test means no more restrictions
- Forgetting that the coach's role continues after licensure
- Not knowing where to find MVA resources (mva.maryland.gov)
- Underestimating the danger of the first year of independent driving
