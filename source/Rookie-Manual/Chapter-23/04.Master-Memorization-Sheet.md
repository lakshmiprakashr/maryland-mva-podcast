# Chapter 23 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

The certification for rookie drivers and the Driver-Coach Agreement establish the legal requirements and ground rules for new drivers. Certification requires a parent/guardian to verify practice hours under penalty of perjury. The Driver-Coach Agreement sets expectations for both driver and coach, including consequences for violations. The license comparison chart shows how restrictions change across Learner's Permit, Provisional License, and full Driver License.

## Why It Matters

Signing the certification is a **legal obligation** — false information can be prosecuted. The Driver-Coach Agreement creates accountability for both parties. Understanding how restrictions change with each license stage helps new drivers know what is and is not allowed at each step.

## High-Yield Facts

### Certification Requirements
- Under 25: parent certifies **60 hours** (10 night), in addition to **6 hours** from driver education
- 25+: parent certifies **14 hours** (3 night), in addition to **6 hours** from driver education
- Supervising driver must be **21+, licensed 3+ years**
- Certification is **part of the license application** — false statements = prosecution and/or license cancellation
- Signed under **penalty of perjury**

### Driver-Coach Agreement — Rookie Driver Commitments
- Set specific **driving times and roads**
- **No texting or phone use** while driving
- **Always wear safety belt** — and ensure passengers do too
- **No alcohol or drugs** and drive
- **Obey provisional license restrictions**
- Face escalating consequences for violations (first, second, third offense)

### Driver-Coach Agreement — Coach Commitments
- Supervise completion of **60 hours** of supervised driving
- Be a **model driver** and follow all Maryland laws
- Provide **constructive, helpful** comments
- Set limits and **enforce Maryland driving law**

### License Comparison Chart — Key Differences

| Feature | Learner's Permit | Provisional License | Full License |
|---------|-----------------|---------------------|--------------|
| Minimum Age | 15 years, 9 months | 16 years, 6 months | 18 years |
| Cosigner | Required (any reason) | Required if under 18 | None |
| Cell Phone | No use (911 only) | No use under 18; hands-free if 18+ | Hands-free allowed |
| Texting | No (911 only) | No (911 only) | No (911 only) |
| Nighttime (under 18) | N/A | No driving 12am-5am (exceptions) | No restrictions |
| Passengers (under 18) | N/A | No non-family for first 151 days | No restrictions |
| Alcohol | Zero tolerance | Zero tolerance | Under 21: zero tolerance |

### Consequences for Under 18
- 1st offense: DIP Class
- 2nd offense: 30 days suspension + 90 days work/school restriction
- 3rd offense: 180 days suspension/revocation + 180 days restriction + Young Driver Improvement Program
- 4th offense: 180 day revocation

### Point System
- 5-7 points: DIP class required
- 8 points: License suspension (possible additional penalties)
- 12 points: License revocation (possible additional penalties)

## MVA Test Traps

- **Trap:** Confusing under 25 and 25+ certification hours. Under 25 = 60 hrs; 25+ = 14 hrs.
- **Trap:** Thinking the cosigner requirement disappears at 18 for provisional licenses. Cosigner is only required if under 18.
- **Trap:** Believing provisional license holders over 18 can text. **No texting** applies to all license types — only 911 texts are allowed.
- **Trap:** Thinking nighttime restrictions apply to all provisional license holders. They only apply if **under 18**.
- **Trap:** Confusing suspension (temporary) with revocation (permanent loss). Revocation is more severe.

## Common Mistakes

- Forgetting that certification is signed under **penalty of perjury**
- Mixing up the provisional license passenger restriction: it is the **first 151 days**, and only for **non-family** members
- Assuming the Driver-Coach Agreement is optional — it is a recommended formal commitment
- Not understanding that hands-free phone use is only allowed for provisional license holders **18 and over**

## Memory Tricks

- **"Perjury = serious"** — signing the certification falsely is a crime
- **"151 days"** — think "1-5-1" for the passenger restriction period
- **"12 to 5"** — curfew is midnight to 5am (under 18)
- **"5-7-8-12"** — DIP class at 5-7, suspension at 8, revocation at 12
- **"Coach = role model"** — the coach must model good driving behavior

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

No specific road signs in this chapter. Focus on license stages, restrictions, and consequences.

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- Certification signed under penalty of perjury (part of license application)
- False certification = prosecution and/or license cancellation
- Graduated Licensing System with three stages: Learner's Permit, Provisional License, Full License
- Nighttime restriction: 12am-5am for provisional license holders under 18
- Passenger restriction: no non-family passengers for first 151 days (under 18)
- Point system: 5-7 = DIP, 8 = suspension, 12 = revocation
