# Chapter 09 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Lesson 4 covers **entering traffic from the side of the road** (full procedure), **approaching and navigating intersections**, **school bus stopping rules**, **turning at intersections** (check, blind spots, signal 3-4 seconds, position, stop if required, safe gap, steer into proper lane), and **leaving traffic** (parking procedure including the 12-inch curb rule). Also covers **blind spot awareness** and the **coach's readiness checklist**.

## Why It Matters

Entering traffic and navigating intersections are where most new-driver crashes occur. The 3-4 second signal requirement, the 12-inch curb rule, and the school bus law are among the most commonly tested items on the Maryland knowledge test.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Entering traffic:** Check rear/sides/front, signal, select safe gap, enter lane, cancel signal, accelerate to speed
- **Intersection approach:** Check for vehicles/pedestrians/signs/devices, adjust speed, tap brake to warn behind, full stop at stop signs and red lights, clear intersection before proceeding on green
- **School bus law:** No roadway divider + red lights + stop arm = STOP AND WAIT
- **Turning at intersections:** Check everything, turn head to check blind spots, signal at least 3-4 seconds in advance, position vehicle correctly, stop if required (full stop before right turn on red), identify safe gap, check mirrors for hazards, steer into proper lane, adjust speed
- **Leaving traffic/parking:** Search ahead for safe/legal spot, check mirrors, signal, slow down, steer within 12 inches of the curb
- **Blind spots:** Mirrors cannot see everything; you MUST turn your head to check blind spots before changing lanes or turning
- **Right turn on red:** Must come to a complete stop first, then proceed when safe

## MVA Test Traps

| Trap | Correct Answer |
|------|---------------|
| "Signal for 1-2 seconds before turning" | Must signal at least 3-4 seconds in advance |
| "You can stop close to the curb" | Must steer within 12 inches of the curb when parking |
| "Right turn on red means go after slowing" | Must come to a FULL STOP first, then proceed when safe |
| "Mirrors show all blind spots" | You must physically turn your head to check blind spots |
| "Yellow school bus lights mean stop" | Yellow = caution; red lights + stop arm + no divider = stop |

## Common Mistakes

- Not turning head to check blind spots before turning
- Signaling too late (less than 3-4 seconds before turning)
- Not coming to a full stop before right turn on red
- Parking more than 12 inches from the curb
- Not checking the intersection is clear on green before proceeding
- Forgetting to cancel the turn signal after entering traffic
- Not searching ahead for a safe parking spot before needing to stop

## Memory Tricks

- **"3 to 4 seconds signal"** — Signal early. Count it: one-one-thousand, two-one-thousand, three-one-thousand, four-one-thousand, then turn
- **"12 inches curb"** — When parking, your tires must be within 12 inches of the curb. Think "one foot close"
- **"Head turn, not just mirror"** — Mirrors don't show blind spots. Always physically turn your head
- **"Full stop, then red"** — Before right turn on red: full stop first, then check and go
- **"Search, Signal, Slow, Curb"** — Leaving traffic sequence: search for spot, signal, slow down, steer to curb

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

- **Stop sign** — Full stop required; one of the most tested items
- **School bus stop arm** — Red flashing lights + extended stop arm
- **Traffic signals** — Green (proceed if clear), Yellow (stop if safe), Red (stop completely)
- **Turn signals** — Required 3-4 seconds before turns and lane changes
- **Parking regulations** — Within 12 inches of curb when leaving traffic

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **Signal requirement (MD Transportation Article 21-604):** Signal at least 3-4 seconds before turning or changing lanes
- **Right turn on red:** Full stop required, then proceed when safe and clear
- **Parking distance from curb:** Within 12 inches when parallel parking or pulling over
- **School bus law (MD Transportation Article 21-706):** Stop for school bus with red lights and stop arm when no physical roadway divider
- **Blind spot check:** Required before all lane changes and turns
