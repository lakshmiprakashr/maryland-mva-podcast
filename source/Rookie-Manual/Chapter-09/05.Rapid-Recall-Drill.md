**Q1. [Type: RECALL]** How far in advance must you signal before turning at an intersection?

**Answer:** At least 3 to 4 seconds in advance.

**Explanation:** Maryland law requires signaling at least 3-4 seconds before turning. This gives other drivers adequate time to react to your maneuver.

**MVA Test Trap:** "1-2 seconds is enough" — the manual specifies 3-4 seconds. Signaling too late is a common violation.

**Common Mistake:** Waiting until right before the turn to signal, giving other drivers no time to react.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Turning at Intersection section

**Q2. [Type: RECALL]** When parking or leaving traffic, how close to the curb must your vehicle be?

**Answer:** Within 12 inches of the curb.

**Explanation:** Parking more than 12 inches from the curb is a violation. It also creates a hazard for cyclists and pedestrians, and can obstruct traffic.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Close to the curb" is vague — the manual specifies 12 inches, which is about one foot.

**Common Mistake:** Parking 2-3 feet from the curb and thinking it's close enough.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Leaving Traffic section

---

**Q3. [Type: RECALL]** What must you do before making a right turn on a red light?

**Answer:** Come to a full and complete stop first, then proceed when the intersection is clear and safe.

**Explanation:** A right turn on red is only legal after a complete stop. You cannot roll through the stop. After stopping, check for traffic and pedestrians, then proceed when safe.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Slow down and look" is not enough. You must come to a FULL STOP.

**Common Mistake:** Performing a rolling stop before turning right on red.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Turning at Intersection section

---

**Q4. [Type: RECALL]** Why must you turn your head to check blind spots before turning or changing lanes?

**Answer:** Mirrors cannot show all areas around the vehicle. Blind spots exist in areas that mirrors don't cover, and only physically turning your head reveals them.

**Explanation:** Every vehicle has blind spots in the areas just beside and behind it. These spots are not visible in side or rearview mirrors. Failing to check them is a leading cause of lane-change collisions.

**MVA Test Trap:** "My mirrors cover everything" — they don't. The manual specifically says to turn your head.

**Common Mistake:** Relying only on mirrors when changing lanes or turning.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Blind Spots section

---

**Q5. [Type: RECALL]** What is the complete sequence for turning at an intersection?

**Answer:** Check for vehicles, pedestrians, signs, and devices. Turn head to check blind spots. Signal 3-4 seconds in advance. Position vehicle in correct lane. Adjust speed and stop if required. Identify safe gap. Check mirrors for hazards. Steer into proper lane. Adjust speed as necessary.

**Explanation:** This is a nine-step sequence. The MVA tests whether you know each step, especially the blind spot check and the 3-4 second signal requirement.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Just signal and turn" — there are nine steps, including blind spot checks and speed adjustments.

**Common Mistake:** Skipping the blind spot check because mirrors "seem to show everything."

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Turning at Intersection section

**Q6. [Type: APPLICATION]** A driver approaches an intersection on a green light. They don't check if the intersection is clear and proceed. A car from the cross street is still in the intersection. What went wrong?

**Answer:** The driver failed to verify the intersection was clear before proceeding. A green light means permission to go, not that it's automatically safe.

**Explanation:** The manual specifically states to "make sure that the intersection is clear before proceeding" when the light turns green. Other vehicles may still be clearing the intersection.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Green light means I have right of way" — right of way doesn't help if a car is physically in the intersection.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming green automatically means safe to go.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Intersections section

**Q7. [Type: APPLICATION]** A student is preparing to turn right at an intersection. They check traffic, signal for 2 seconds, and begin turning. The coach tells them to redo it. Why?

**Answer:** The signal was given for only 2 seconds. Maryland requires signaling at least 3-4 seconds in advance.

**Explanation:** Two seconds is not enough time for other drivers to react to your signaled intention. The 3-4 second minimum ensures adequate warning.

**MVA Test Trap:** "2 seconds feels like a long time" — the law requires 3-4 seconds, not your perception of time.

**Common Mistake:** Signaling too late because 3-4 seconds feels like an eternity at an intersection.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Turning at Intersection section

**Q8. [Type: APPLICATION]** A driver needs to park on a busy street. They find a spot and pull over, ending up about 3 feet from the curb. Is this acceptable?

**Answer:** No. Maryland law requires the vehicle to be within 12 inches of the curb when parking.

**Explanation:** Three feet (36 inches) is far more than the 12-inch maximum. This creates a hazard for cyclists and other vehicles.

**MVA Test Trap:** "3 feet is pretty close" — the law is specific: 12 inches maximum.

**Common Mistake:** Misjudging the distance to the curb and parking too far out.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Leaving Traffic section

**Q9. [Type: APPLICATION]** A new driver is changing lanes on a multi-lane road. They check the rearview mirror, check the side mirror, and begin moving into the next lane. What did they forget?

**Answer:** They forgot to turn their head and check the blind spot. Mirrors alone do not show all areas around the vehicle.

**Explanation:** The manual specifically requires turning your head to check blind spots before changing lanes. Mirrors have inherent blind spots that can hide vehicles.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Both mirrors are enough" — mirrors are not enough. Head turn is required.

**Common Mistake:** Relying solely on mirror checks without the physical head turn.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Blind Spots section

**Q10. [Type: APPLICATION]** A school bus ahead displays yellow flashing lights. The new driver asks if they should stop. What should the coach say?

**Answer:** Yellow lights mean the bus is preparing to stop. Slow down and be prepared to stop, but do not stop yet. Only stop when the red lights activate and the stop arm extends.

**Explanation:** Yellow lights are a warning, not a legal trigger to stop. Red lights plus the stop arm are what require you to stop (assuming no physical divider).

**MVA Test Trap:** "Yellow means stop" — yellow means caution. Red plus stop arm means stop.

**Common Mistake:** Stopping prematurely for yellow lights, creating a rear-end collision risk.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, School Bus section

**Q11. [Type: APPLICATION]** The new driver needs to park. They should search ahead for a safe and legal spot. What does "safe and legal" mean?

**Answer:** Safe means not blocking traffic, not too close to intersections or driveways, not on a hill without wheel chocks. Legal means no parking restrictions, no fire hydrants, no bus stops, no handicapped zones without permits.

**Explanation:** Parking must be both safe (not creating hazards) and legal (not violating parking regulations). The manual says to "search ahead" — don't wait until the last second.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Any spot is fine" — many spots are illegal or dangerous. Planning ahead is required.

**Common Mistake:** Parking in restricted areas without noticing the signs.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Leaving Traffic section

---

**Q12. [Type: APPLICATION]** After making a left turn, the new driver stays in the left-turn lane instead of moving into the appropriate travel lane. What's wrong?

**Answer:** After completing a turn, you should steer into the proper lane for continuing travel. Staying in a turn-only lane can obstruct traffic and create a hazard.

**Explanation:** The manual says to "steer into the proper lane" after turning. This means transitioning to the correct travel lane promptly.

**MVA Test Trap:** "The turn is done, I'm fine" — you must complete the maneuver by entering the correct lane.

**Common Mistake:** Lingering in a turn lane after the turn is complete.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Turning at Intersection section

**Q13. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Compare entering traffic from a parked position with turning at an intersection. What steps are shared?

**Answer:** Both require checking traffic, using turn signals, identifying a safe gap, adjusting speed, and checking for hazards. Both also require blind spot awareness.

**Explanation:** These are two different scenarios that follow the same safety principles. The sequence may differ, but the core steps of observe-signal-proceed are universal.

**MVA Test Trap:** "They're totally different" — they share the fundamental safety pattern.

**Common Mistake:** Treating each driving scenario as completely unique without recognizing the common safety framework.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Entering Traffic and Turning sections

**Q14. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Why does Maryland require a 3-4 second signal before turning, rather than just "signal before turning"?

**Answer:** The specific time requirement ensures other drivers have adequate warning. "Signal before turning" is vague and could mean signaling one second before the turn, which is insufficient.

**Explanation:** A specific time standard (3-4 seconds) provides a measurable, enforceable rule. It ensures the signal is active long enough for following drivers to see it and react.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Any signal timing is fine" — the law specifies 3-4 seconds for a reason.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking the exact timing doesn't matter as long as you signal.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Turning at Intersection section

**Q15. [Type: RECALL]** What is the correct procedure when leaving traffic to park?

**Answer:** Search ahead for a safe and legal place, check mirrors, turn on your turn signal, slow the vehicle, and steer within 12 inches of the curb.

**Explanation:** Five steps. Planning ahead is critical — don't suddenly swerve to park. Signal your intention, slow down, and park close to the curb.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Just pull over when you see a spot" — the manual says to search ahead, signal, and follow the proper sequence.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to signal before pulling over to park.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Leaving Traffic section

**Q16. [Type: APPLICATION]** A driver is at a red light in the right lane. The light turns green, and they immediately accelerate without looking. A pedestrian is in the crosswalk. What went wrong?

**Answer:** The driver did not check that the intersection was clear before proceeding. Pedestrians may still be in the crosswalk when the light changes.

**Explanation:** A green light means you may proceed when the intersection is clear. Pedestrians have the right of way in crosswalks, and the driver must verify the path is clear.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Green means go" — green means go when it's safe, not automatically.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming all pedestrians have cleared the intersection when the light changes.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Intersections section

**Q17. [Type: RECALL]** What are the steps for turning at an intersection? List them in order.

**Answer:** (1) Check for vehicles, pedestrians, signs, and devices, (2) turn head to check blind spots, (3) signal 3-4 seconds in advance, (4) position vehicle correctly, (5) adjust speed and stop if required, (6) identify safe gap, (7) check mirrors for hazards, (8) steer into proper lane, (9) adjust speed.

**Explanation:** Nine steps in sequence. The MVA exam may ask about specific steps, especially the blind spot check and signal timing.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Just signal and turn" — there are nine distinct steps. Skipping any one creates risk.

**Common Mistake:** Remembering only 5-6 steps and forgetting the blind spot check and speed adjustments.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Turning at Intersection section

**Q18. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** How does the blind spot check relate to the school bus stopping rule? What's the common principle?

**Answer:** Both require you to go beyond what mirrors show. Blind spots require a head turn; the school bus rule requires evaluating whether a physical divider exists. Both demand thorough situational awareness.

**Explanation:** Safe driving consistently requires looking beyond the obvious. Mirrors aren't enough for lane changes, and "seeing a bus" isn't enough — you must evaluate the divider condition.

**MVA Test Trap:** "These are unrelated topics" — they share the principle of complete situational assessment.

**Common Mistake:** Memorizing rules in isolation without connecting them to the broader principle of thorough awareness.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Blind Spots and School Bus sections

**Q19. [Type: APPLICATION]** A student signals to make a right turn, but they signal for only 1 second before turning. A car behind them doesn't react and nearly hits them. Why did this happen?

**Answer:** The signal was too brief. At 1 second, the following driver had no time to see and react to the signal. The minimum is 3-4 seconds.

**Explanation:** Signal timing exists to give other drivers adequate warning. One second is insufficient for a following driver to process the signal and adjust their driving.

**MVA Test Trap:** "1 second is still signaling" — it is, but it's not enough. The law requires 3-4 seconds minimum.

**Common Mistake:** Signaling at the last moment because the turn feels "obvious."

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Turning at Intersection section

**Q20. [Type: RECALL]** What must you verify before entering traffic from the side of the road?

**Answer:** You must check to the rear, sides, and front of the vehicle to ensure the way is clear.

**Explanation:** All three directions must be checked — rear, sides, and front. Checking only one or two directions can miss approaching traffic.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Check forward and to the sides" — the rear check is specifically required and often forgotten.

**Common Mistake:** Focusing only on cross-traffic and not checking for vehicles approaching from behind.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 9, Entering Traffic section

## Quick Reference

- **Signal timing:** At least 3-4 seconds before turns and lane changes
- **Parking distance:** Within 12 inches of the curb
- **Right turn on red:** FULL STOP first, then proceed when safe
- **Blind spots:** Always turn head to check; mirrors alone insufficient
- **Intersection on green:** Check intersection is clear before proceeding
- **Turning at intersections:** 9-step sequence including blind spots and speed adjustments
- **Leaving traffic:** Search ahead, check mirrors, signal, slow, steer to curb
- **School bus:** Red lights + stop arm + no divider = stop and wait
- **Entering traffic:** Check rear/sides/front, signal, gap, enter, cancel, accelerate

**Test Day Tip:** The 3-4 second signal rule and the 12-inch curb rule are specific numbers the MVA loves to test. Memorize both precisely.
