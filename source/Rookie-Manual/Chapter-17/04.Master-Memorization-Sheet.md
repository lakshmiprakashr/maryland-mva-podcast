# Chapter 17 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary
Adverse weather conditions — rain, fog, snow, and ice — require specific driving adjustments. Each condition presents unique hazards: hydroplaning in rain, zero visibility in fog, and dramatically increased stopping distances on snow and ice. The key to all adverse conditions is slower speed, smoother inputs, and increased awareness.

## Why It Matters
Maryland experiences a wide variety of weather conditions across the state. New drivers are much more likely to be involved in crashes than experienced drivers, and adverse conditions multiply that risk. Understanding how each weather condition affects your vehicle and knowing the correct response can prevent loss of control, hydroplaning, and multi-car pileups.

## High-Yield Facts
- **Rain:** Headlights on even in light rain, use defoggers, increase following distance, brake earlier and less forcefully
- **Hydroplaning:** Caused by water deeper than tire tread plus excessive speed — reduces friction and can cause total loss of control
- **Fog:** Slow down, use **low beams only** (high beams reflect back), use fog lights if available
- **Snow:** Clear ALL snow and ice from vehicle before driving, steer smoothly, travel slowly, brake gently
- **Stopping on snow:** Distances are **10 times greater** than on dry roads
- **Ice hotspots:** Bridges, overpasses, and shaded areas freeze first
- **Pedestrians in rain:** Watch for distracted people opening umbrellas or rushing — they may step into the road
- **Pull over rule:** If you cannot see the road or the car ahead, or feel anxious, pull over to a safe spot
- **Parallel parking:** Vehicle must be no more than **12 inches** from the curb
- **Parallel parking gap:** Stop parallel with **2-3 feet** between vehicles before backing in
- **Angle parking:** Signal, move slowly, center the vehicle, pull forward to front of space
- **Exiting angle parking:** Check all directions, back up carefully, turn wheel toward travel direction

## MVA Test Traps
- **Trap:** "High beams improve visibility in fog" — WRONG. They reflect off fog and make it worse.
- **Trap:** "You should brake hard on wet roads to stop faster" — WRONG. Brake earlier and less forcefully.
- **Trap:** "Hydroplaning only happens in heavy rain" — WRONG. Any standing water with speed can cause it.
- **Trap:** "Stopping distance on snow is about twice as long" — WRONG. It is 10 times greater.
- **Trap:** "Parallel parking is not on the skills test" — WRONG. It is specifically tested.
- **Trap:** "You can park as far from the curb as you want" — WRONG. Maximum 12 inches from the curb.

## Common Mistakes
- Not clearing the roof of the car before driving in snow (flying snow obstructs other drivers)
- Using high beams in fog out of desperation for better visibility
- Braking too hard on wet roads, causing skidding
- Not watching for pedestrians who are distracted by rain
- Pulling over in the travel lane instead of completely off the road
- Parallel parking too far from the curb (over 12 inches)
- Not signaling before parallel parking maneuvers
- Forgetting to check all directions before exiting any parking space

## Memory Tricks
- **"Fog = Low"** — Always low beams in fog
- **"10x Snow"** — Stopping distance multiplies by 10 on snow
- **"Bridges Freeze First"** — Ice forms on bridges before regular roads
- **"12 Inches Max"** — Maximum distance from curb when parking
- **"2-3 Feet Apart"** — Gap between vehicles for parallel parking setup
- **"Smooth = Snow"** — Smooth steering, slow travel, gentle braking on snow

## Must-Know Signs and Signals
- No specific road signs covered in this chapter — focus is on driving behavior adjustments for weather conditions and parking procedures

## Maryland-Specific Laws
- Headlights required when visibility is reduced by weather (rain, fog, snow)
- Maryland experiences diverse weather conditions statewide — drivers must adapt
- Parallel parking is tested on the MVA Skills Test
- Vehicle must be within 12 inches of curb when parked
