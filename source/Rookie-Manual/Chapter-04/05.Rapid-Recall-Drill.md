## Questions

**Q1. [Type: RECALL]** Can you use a backup camera during the Maryland skills test?

**Answer:** Yes. You may use your backup camera and the vehicle's onboard radar system during the skills test.

**Explanation:** Maryland allows the use of backup cameras and onboard radar systems during the skills test, as these are considered standard safety features.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse backup camera with parking assist. Camera is allowed; parking assist is not.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming all technology is prohibited during the skills test.

---

**Q2. [Type: RECALL]** Can you use parking assist during the Maryland skills test?

**Answer:** No. You may not use the parking assist feature during the skills test.

**Explanation:** While backup cameras and onboard radar are allowed, parking assist is specifically prohibited because it takes over steering control, which the MVA wants to test you on.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse parking assist with backup camera. They are different technologies with different rules.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming all driving assistance technology is treated the same way.

---

**Q3. [Type: RECALL]** What happens if your learner's permit expires before you get your provisional license?

**Answer:** You must retake the law test and get a new learner's permit before you can take the driving skills test.

**Explanation:** An expired permit means you lose your driving privileges. You must go through the permit process again, including passing the law test.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse which test you need to retake. It's the LAW test, not the skills test.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking you can just renew the permit without retaking any test.

---

**Q4. [Type: RECALL]** Can you drive in another state with a Maryland learner's permit?

**Answer:** Not necessarily. You should check with the Motor Vehicle Administration in that state first, as they may not accept a Maryland learner's permit.

**Explanation:** Each state has its own rules about recognizing out-of-state learner's permits. A state may not accept your Maryland permit even if you are with a supervising driver over 21 with three years of experience.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume your Maryland permit works in all states. You must verify with each state.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming reciprocity without checking.

---

**Q5. [Type: RECALL]** Can you start driving if you just received your learner's permit receipt from the MVA?

**Answer:** Yes, but you must keep your receipt with you when you drive.

**Explanation:** The receipt serves as proof of your permit while you wait for the actual permit card. You can drive with it as long as it's in your possession.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think you must wait for the physical card. The receipt is valid for driving.

**Common Mistake:** Waiting to practice until you receive the physical permit card.

---

**Q6. [Type: RECALL]** How long is a Maryland learner's instructional permit valid?

**Answer:** 2 years (24 months) from the date it is issued.

**Explanation:** The permit has a strict expiration date. If it expires before you get your provisional license, you must retake the law test.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse the 2-year validity with the holding periods. The permit can be valid longer than the holding period.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting the expiration date and letting the permit lapse.

---

**Q7. [Type: RECALL]** What is the minimum holding period for a learner's permit for someone 25 or older?

**Answer:** 45 days, provided they do not receive any moving violation citations or PBJ.

**Explanation:** Drivers 25 and older have the shortest holding period of just 45 days, but this can be extended to 9 months if they receive a moving violation or PBJ.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't forget that the 45-day period can be extended. It's not guaranteed.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the 45-day period is absolute and cannot be extended.

---

**Q8. [Type: RECALL]** What happens to your holding period if you receive a moving violation or PBJ?

**Answer:** Your holding period extends to 9 months, regardless of your age.

**Explanation:** A moving violation or PBJ (probation before judgment) triggers the maximum 9-month holding period for all age groups.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think that being over 25 exempts you from the 9-month extension. It applies to everyone.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the 45-day period for 25+ is immune to extensions.

---

**Answer:** Yes, but you must keep the receipt with you when you drive.

**Explanation:** The receipt serves as proof of your permit. You can drive immediately with it, but it must be in your possession at all times while driving.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think you must wait for the physical card. The receipt is valid.

**Common Mistake:** Waiting to practice until the physical permit arrives.

**Q15. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Compare what happens when a 17-year-old and a 26-year-old receive moving violations while practicing.

**Answer:** Both face a 9-month holding period extension. The 17-year-old's holding period extends from 9 months to 9 months (no change), while the 26-year-old's extends from 45 days to 9 months (significant extension).

**Explanation:** The moving violation extension applies equally to all ages, but the impact is more severe for older drivers who would normally have a shorter holding period.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think the extension only affects younger drivers. It affects everyone.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming older drivers are exempt from the extension.

---

**Q16. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Why does Maryland allow backup cameras but prohibit parking assist during the skills test?

**Answer:** Backup cameras provide visibility without taking control, while parking assist takes over steering. The MVA wants to test your ability to steer and park without assistance.

**Explanation:** The distinction is about driver control. Backup cameras help you see, but parking assist does the steering for you, which defeats the purpose of the skills test.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think all technology is treated the same. The distinction is about driver control.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the MVA is anti-technology rather than testing specific skills.

---

**Q17. [Type: RECALL]** What must you bring to the MVA when taking your skills test?

**Answer:** Completed skills practice log, completed certification page, and valid Maryland learner's instructional permit.

**Explanation:** All three documents are required. The log documents your practice hours, the certification page certifies completion, and the permit proves you are authorized.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think you only need the permit. The log and certification page are mandatory.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to bring the signed practice log.

---

**Q18. [Type: RECALL]** What is PBJ and how does it affect your licensing timeline?

**Answer:** PBJ stands for Probation Before Judgment. If convicted or granted PBJ for a moving violation, your holding period extends to 9 months.

**Explanation:** PBJ is a legal disposition that can affect your driving privileges. It triggers the maximum holding period regardless of your age.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse PBJ with a regular conviction. Both trigger the same 9-month extension.

**Common Mistake:** Not understanding what PBJ means and its consequences.

---

**Q19. [Type: RECALL]** What is the minimum age for a supervising driver, and how long must they have held their license?

**Answer:** At least 21 years old and must have held a driver's license for at least 3 years.

**Explanation:** Both requirements must be met: age 21+ AND 3+ years of licensed driving experience.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think being licensed is enough. Both age AND experience requirements must be met.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming any licensed adult can supervise.

---

**Q20. [Type: RECALL]** How are nighttime practice hours defined in Maryland?

**Answer:** ½ hour before sunset until ½ hour after sunrise.

**Explanation:** Nighttime includes the twilight periods around sunset and sunrise, not just dark hours. This captures the most challenging visibility conditions.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse with "after dark" or "sunset to sunrise." The definition includes the half-hour transitions.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking nighttime starts at sunset.
