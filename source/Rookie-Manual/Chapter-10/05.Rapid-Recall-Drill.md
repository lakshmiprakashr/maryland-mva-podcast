**Q1. [Type: RECALL]** What are the seven steps for changing lanes?

**Answer:** (1) Maintain proper space cushion, (2) check traffic in all directions, (3) select a safe gap, (4) use turn signal, (5) recheck in the direction of the lane change, (6) adjust speed and steer smoothly into the lane, (7) cancel turn signal once established.

**Explanation:** This is a complete sequence. The recheck step is critical because traffic conditions can change between your initial check and the lane change.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Signal and go" — there are seven steps, including maintaining space, rechecking, and canceling the signal.

**Common Mistake:** Skipping the recheck step or forgetting to cancel the signal after the lane change.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 10, Changing Lanes section

**Q2. [Type: RECALL]** Why is risk always present while driving, even on quiet roads?

**Answer:** A child may run into traffic, a driver ahead may stop suddenly, someone may swerve into your lane while looking at a phone. Unexpected hazards can appear at any time.

**Explanation:** The manual states that "risk is the constant danger that something unexpected will happen on the road." It is not limited to busy roads or bad weather.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Risk is only in heavy traffic" — risk exists everywhere, all the time.

**Common Mistake:** Letting your guard down on quiet or residential roads because they "seem safe."

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 10, Risk Is Everywhere section

**Q3. [Type: RECALL]** How close to the curb must you be when leaving traffic to park?

**MVA Test Trap:** "Close to the curb" is not specific enough — the manual says 12 inches maximum.

**Q4. [Type: RECALL]** What must you do before changing lanes that mirrors alone cannot accomplish?

**Answer:** Physically turn your head to check blind spots.

**Explanation:** Mirrors have blind spots that cannot show vehicles approaching from behind in adjacent lanes. A head turn is the only way to see into these areas.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Both mirrors cover blind spots" — they don't. The manual explicitly requires a head turn.

**Common Mistake:** Relying on mirrors because turning your head feels inconvenient.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 10, Blind Spots section

**Q6. [Type: APPLICATION]** A driver wants to change from the right lane to the left lane on a multi-lane road. They check the left mirror, signal, and move over. What step did they miss?

**Answer:** They missed the recheck in the direction of the lane change. After signaling and before moving, they must recheck the left side to confirm the gap is still safe.

**Explanation:** Traffic conditions change quickly. A car may have accelerated into the gap between the initial check and the lane change. The recheck is a critical safety step.

**MVA Test Trap:** "One check is enough" — the manual requires an initial check AND a recheck.

**Common Mistake:** Checking once and assuming the situation hasn't changed.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 10, Changing Lanes section

**Q20. [Type: RECALL]** What are the five steps for leaving traffic to park?

**Answer:** (1) Search ahead for a safe and legal place to park, (2) check mirrors, (3) turn on your turn signal, (4) slow the vehicle, (5) steer within 12 inches of the curb.

**Explanation:** Planning ahead is step one — don't suddenly swerve to park. Signal, slow, and park within the 12-inch curb requirement.

**MVA Test Trap:** "Just pull over" — five steps are required, including searching ahead and signaling.

**Common Mistake:** Forgetting to signal before pulling over to park.

**Reference:** Driver's Manual Chapter 10, Leaving Traffic section

## Quick Reference

- **Turning (9 steps):** Check, blind spots, signal 3-4 sec, position, speed/stop, gap, mirrors, steer, adjust
- **Lane changes (7 steps):** Space cushion, check all, gap, signal, recheck, steer smoothly, cancel signal
- **Blind spots:** Always turn head; mirrors alone are insufficient
- **Signal timing:** 3-4 seconds minimum before turns and lane changes
- **Parking distance:** Within 12 inches of curb
- **Risk is everywhere:** Children, sudden stops, distracted drivers — expect the unexpected
- **Novice drivers:** More likely to miss risks; actively learn hazard recognition
- **Busy road practice:** 35-50 mph roads; focus on looking ahead and hazard watching
- **Space cushion:** First step in lane changes — determines if change is possible

**Test Day Tip:** The two numbers on the MVA exam: **3-4 seconds** for signals, **12 inches** for parking. Know them cold.
