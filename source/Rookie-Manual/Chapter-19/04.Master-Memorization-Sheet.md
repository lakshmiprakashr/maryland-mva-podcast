# Chapter 19 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary
This chapter covers what to do during a traffic stop, consequences of citations for provisional license holders, insurance requirements, and when you must contact law enforcement after a crash. It bridges the gap between traffic stop procedures and crash reporting responsibilities.

## Why It Matters
Every driver will eventually be pulled over or witness a crash. Knowing the correct procedures protects your rights, ensures compliance with Maryland law, and keeps you and others safe. For provisional license holders, understanding citation consequences is critical because a single violation can reset your entire licensing timeline.

## High-Yield Facts
- **Traffic stop procedure:** Pull off road far from traffic, engine off, radio off, stay in vehicle, seatbelt on, hands visible on steering wheel, sign citation without arguing
- **Signing citation:** NOT an admission of guilt — you can contest it in court
- **Provisional license consequences:** Moving violation or PBJ conviction = restart 18-month waiting period
- **Driver Improvement Program:** Required for permit and provisional holders; failure to attend = license suspension
- **Insurance requirement:** Valid insurance card required at all times since October 1, 2016; electronic format accepted
- **Contact law enforcement if:** Someone injured, vehicle cannot be moved, driver under influence, driver has no license, driver tries to leave scene, public property damaged
- **Crash with injuries:** Call 911 immediately, stay at scene, do NOT move vehicles
- **Crash without injuries, cannot move:** Call 911, move to safe location, use emergency flashers or flares
- **Crash without injuries, can move:** Move vehicle out of travel lane, collect information
- **Information to collect:** Name, address, phone, insurance, license plate, make/model/year, witness names and contact info
- **Domestic animal strike:** Must notify law enforcement
- **Unattended vehicle/property strike:** Find owner and provide contact info, or leave your contact info securely and notify insurance

## MVA Test Traps
- **Trap:** "You should get out of the car during a traffic stop" — WRONG. Stay in your vehicle.
- **Trap:** "Signing a citation admits guilt" — WRONG. It acknowledges receipt only.
- **Trap:** "You only need to call 911 for crashes with injuries" — WRONG. Also for immovable vehicles, DUI, unlicensed drivers, hit-and-runs, and public property damage.
- **Trap:** "You should move injured people from vehicles after a crash" — WRONG. Do not move vehicles or people; wait for emergency responders.
- **Trap:** "Insurance only needs to be shown during traffic stops" — WRONG. Must be carried at all times.

## Common Mistakes
- Reaching for documents before the officer asks — hands should stay visible
- Getting out of the vehicle during a traffic stop
- Not collecting complete information after a minor crash
- Forgetting to get witness information
- Not calling 911 for crashes that seem minor but have immovable vehicles
- Not knowing that striking a domestic animal requires law enforcement notification
- Leaving contact info in an obvious place when striking unattended property

## Maryland-Specific Laws
- **Proof of insurance law (October 1, 2016):** Valid insurance card required at all times; electronic format accepted; must present on request; fines for non-compliance
- **Provisional license violations:** Moving violation or PBJ = restart 18-month waiting period
- **Driver Improvement Program:** Mandatory for provisional/permit holders with violations
- **Crash reporting:** Multiple situations require law enforcement contact by law
- **Domestic animal strike:** Notification to law enforcement is required
- **Unattended property strike:** Must attempt to find owner or leave contact info
