# Chapter 19 - Audio Review Script

## [0:00] Introduction

Welcome to your audio review for Maryland Driver's Manual chapter nineteen, covering **traffic stops** and **crash reporting procedures**. [PAUSE]

This chapter is essential for every driver. We will cover exactly what to do when a police officer pulls you over, the consequences of citations for provisional license holders, and the rules for reporting crashes in different situations. [PAUSE]

Knowing these procedures protects your safety, your rights, and your driving privilege. Let us begin. [LONG PAUSE]

## [1:30] Section 1: Traffic Stop Protocol

Picture this. You are driving and you see red and blue lights behind you. What do you do? [PAUSE]

First, **pull off to the side of the roadway** as far away from traffic as possible. Do not just pull to the nearest shoulder if there is a safer spot a little farther ahead. [PAUSE]

Once you are stopped, turn off your **engine**, your **radio**, and any other device. This eliminates distractions and lets you hear the officer clearly. [PAUSE]

**Stay in your vehicle** and keep your seatbelt fastened. Do not get out of the car. [EMPHASIS: stay in your vehicle] [PAUSE]

Keep your hands visible at all times, preferably on the steering wheel. Do not reach for your wallet or documents until the officer asks you for them. This is important for your safety and the officer's peace of mind. [PAUSE]

If the officer issues you a citation, **sign it**. Do not argue. Now here is the fact that surprises most people. Signing a citation is **not** an admission of guilt. You are simply acknowledging that you received it. You can contest it later in court. [EMPHASIS: not an admission of guilt] [LONG PAUSE]

## [4:30] Section 2: Citation Consequences for Provisional Drivers

Now, if you hold a provisional driver's license, there are additional consequences you need to know about. [PAUSE]

If you are convicted of a moving violation, or if you receive a **Probation Before Judgment**, you will be required to **restart your eighteen-month waiting period**. That means the clock starts over from zero. [EMPHASIS: restart] [PAUSE]

You will also be required to attend the **Driver Improvement Program**. This program reviews safe driving concepts taught in driver education. Whether you hold a learner's permit or a provisional license, this program is mandatory for any violation. [PAUSE]

And here is a critical point. If you fail to attend the Driver Improvement Program, your license will be **suspended**. This is not optional. [PAUSE]

The manual also includes a sobering statistic. Each year in Maryland, more than **sixty people** are killed and more than **seven thousand eight hundred** people are injured in crashes involving young drivers. New drivers are much more likely to receive a citation in their first year. [LONG PAUSE]

## [7:00] Section 3: Insurance Requirements

Let us talk about insurance. Since **October first, 2016**, all Maryland drivers must have proof of insurance with them at all times while driving. [PAUSE]

This proof can be in **electronic format**, like on your phone. It does not have to be a physical card. You must present it on request of a law enforcement officer. [PAUSE]

Failure to carry proof of insurance may result in fines. So make sure your insurance information is accessible, whether it is in your glove box or on your phone. [LONG PAUSE]

## [9:00] Section 4: Crash Reporting

Now let us cover what to do after a crash. The manual breaks this into different scenarios based on two questions. Is anyone injured? And can the vehicle move? [PAUSE]

**If someone is injured**, immediately call nine-one-one. Stay at the scene until help arrives. Do not move the vehicles. Injured persons may have spinal injuries that could be worsened by movement. [EMPHASIS: do not move] [PAUSE]

**If no one is injured but you cannot move your vehicle**, call nine-one-one and seek assistance. Move yourself to a safe location away from traffic. Use your emergency flashers or flares to warn other motorists. [PAUSE]

**If no one is injured and your vehicle can move**, move it out of the travel portion of the road. Then collect all the pertinent information. [PAUSE]

That information includes the other driver's **name**, **address**, **phone number**, **insurance information**, **vehicle license plate number**, and the **make, model, and year** of their vehicle if possible. Also get the names and contact numbers of any witnesses. [LONG PAUSE]

## [11:30] Section 5: Special Crash Situations

There are two more situations the manual addresses. [PAUSE]

If you strike and injure a **domestic animal**, you are required to notify law enforcement. This is the same as any other incident involving damage or injury. [PAUSE]

If you strike an **unattended vehicle or property**, you must attempt to find the owner and provide your contact information. If you cannot locate the owner, leave your contact information in a secure place on the vehicle or property. Then notify your insurance company. [PAUSE]

Both of these situations carry the same principle. When you cause damage, you have a legal obligation to take responsibility and document it. [LONG PAUSE]

## [12:30] Quick Review

Let us recap the essentials. [PAUSE]

Traffic stop: pull over, engine off, stay in car, hands on steering wheel. [PAUSE]

Signing a citation is not admitting guilt. [PAUSE]

Provisional license violation: restart the eighteen-month waiting period. [PAUSE]

Driver Improvement Program is mandatory for violations. [PAUSE]

Proof of insurance required at all times since October 2016. [PAUSE]

Call nine-one-one for injuries, immovable vehicles, DUI, unlicensed drivers, hit-and-runs, and public property damage. [PAUSE]

After a minor crash: move vehicles, collect name, address, phone, insurance, plate, and witness information. [PAUSE]

Striking domestic animals or unattended property requires documentation and notification. [LONG PAUSE]

## [13:30] Closing

You have reviewed two topics that every Maryland driver will eventually face. Traffic stops are part of driving, and knowing the correct procedure keeps you safe and protects your rights. Crash reporting is a legal responsibility that ensures everyone involved is properly documented and supported. [PAUSE]

If you need to review any of these procedures, replay this audio. The more you hear these steps, the more automatic they become when you need them. [PAUSE]

Keep studying, stay focused, and good luck on your Maryland knowledge test. [LONG PAUSE]

- **MVA** (em-vee-AY) — Motor Vehicle Administration
- **GDL** (jee-dee-EL) — Graduated Driver Licensing
- **PBJ** (pee-bee-JAY) — Probation Before Judgment
- **Citation** (sy-TAY-shun) — traffic ticket or notice of violation
