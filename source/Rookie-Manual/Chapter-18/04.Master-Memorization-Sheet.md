# Chapter 18 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary
This chapter covers parking techniques (parallel and angle), what to do when pulled over by police, consequences of traffic citations for provisional license holders, the importance of carrying proof of insurance, and statistics about young driver crashes in Maryland.

## Why It Matters
Parking is one of the most technically demanding skills for new drivers and is specifically tested on the MVA Skills Test. Knowing what to do during a traffic stop and understanding citation consequences protects your driving privilege and keeps you safe during encounters with law enforcement.

## High-Yield Facts
- **Parallel parking steps:** Signal, stop parallel to vehicle ahead (front seat aligned, 2-3 feet gap), foot on brake, shift to reverse, check all directions, yield to all traffic/pedestrians, back slowly with sharp turn, align with rear bumper, turn opposite, stop before touching, shift to drive, adjust — **12 inches max from curb**
- **Exiting parallel park:** Check traffic, signal, brake, shift to reverse, back close to car behind, shift to drive, turn toward travel direction, check front bumper, steer into traffic gap
- **Angle parking entering:** Signal, move slowly, center vehicle, pull forward to front, secure vehicle
- **Angle parking exiting:** Check all directions, back up carefully, turn wheel toward travel direction, shift to drive when clear
- **Traffic stop procedure:** Pull off road far from traffic, engine off, radio off, stay in vehicle, seatbelt on, hands visible on steering wheel, sign citation without arguing
- **Signing citation:** NOT an admission of guilt
- **Provisional license consequences:** Moving violation or PBJ conviction = restart 18-month waiting period
- **Driver Improvement Program:** Required for permit and provisional holders convicted of violations; failure to attend = license suspension
- **Proof of insurance:** Required at all times since October 1, 2016; can be electronic; must show on request of law enforcement
- **Young driver crash stats:** More than 60 killed, more than 7,800 injured annually in Maryland crashes involving young drivers

## MVA Test Traps
- **Trap:** "Signing a citation means you admit guilt" — WRONG. Signing is required and is NOT an admission of guilt.
- **Trap:** "You can argue with the officer if you disagree with the citation" — WRONG. Sign it and address it later.
- **Trap:** "Parallel parking is not on the MVA test" — WRONG. It is specifically tested.
- **Trap:** "You only need insurance in your glove box" — WRONG. Electronic proof is acceptable since 2016.
- **Trap:** "A citation on a provisional license has no extra consequences" — WRONG. You restart the 18-month waiting period.

## Common Mistakes
- Getting out of the vehicle during a traffic stop — always stay inside
- Taking hands off the steering wheel to reach for documents before the officer asks
- Arguing with the officer about the citation instead of signing it
- Not carrying proof of insurance (physical or electronic)
- Parallel parking more than 12 inches from the curb
- Not checking all directions when exiting any parking space
- Forgetting that PBJ (Probation Before Judgment) also triggers the 18-month restart

## Memory Tricks
- **"Stay, Seatbelt, Steering Wheel"** — Stay in car, keep seatbelt on, hands on steering wheel
- **"Sign it, fight it later"** — Sign the citation, contest in court if needed
- **"18 months restarted"** — Any moving violation or PBJ during provisional license resets the clock
- **"12 inches max"** — Maximum distance from curb when parallel parking
- **"2 to 3 feet"** — Gap between vehicles when setting up parallel park
- **"Insurance always"** — Physical or electronic proof required at all times

## Must-Know Signs and Signals
- No specific road signs covered in this chapter — focus is on parking procedures, traffic stop protocol, and citation consequences

## Maryland-Specific Laws
- **Proof of insurance law (effective October 1, 2016):** All drivers must carry valid insurance identification card at all times while operating a motor vehicle; electronic format accepted; must present on request of law enforcement; failure to comply may result in fines
- **Provisional license violations:** Conviction for moving violation or PBJ requires restarting the 18-month waiting period
- **Driver Improvement Program:** Mandatory for provisional/permit holders with violations; non-attendance results in license suspension
- **Citations cannot be ignored:** Must sign when issued; contest through proper legal channels
