## Questions

**Q1. [Type: RECALL]** When parallel parking, how far apart should your vehicle be from the car you are parking behind?

**Answer:** Two to three feet between vehicles.

**Explanation:** This gap provides enough room to maneuver into the space. Your front seat should be in line with the vehicle you are parking behind before you begin backing in.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking you should be closer — being too close limits maneuvering room and increases the risk of contact.

**Common Mistake:** Not aligning the front seat with the other vehicle before starting the backing maneuver.

---

**Q2. [Type: RECALL]** What is the maximum distance your vehicle may be from the curb after parallel parking?

**Answer:** No more than 12 inches from the curb.

**Explanation:** Maryland law requires parallel-parked vehicles to be within 12 inches of the curb. This prevents the vehicle from protruding into the travel lane.

**MVA Test Trap:** Confusing the 12-inch curb distance with other parking measurements.

**Common Mistake:** Not checking the final curb distance after completing the park — always verify before exiting the vehicle.

---

**Q3. [Type: RECALL]** What should you do if a police officer pulls you over?

**Answer:** Pull off the roadway as far from traffic as possible, turn off the engine, radio, and any other devices, stay in your vehicle with your seatbelt fastened, and keep your hands visible on the steering wheel.

**Explanation:** This sequence keeps you safe and shows the officer you are cooperative. Staying in the vehicle with hands visible reduces the officer's uncertainty.

**MVA Test Trap:** Getting out of the car to meet the officer — this is potentially dangerous and should never be done.

**Common Mistake:** Reaching for your wallet or documents before the officer asks — keep your hands visible first.

---

**Q4. [Type: RECALL]** Does signing a traffic citation mean you are admitting guilt?

**Answer:** No, signing a citation is not an admission of guilt.

**Explanation:** Signing acknowledges that you received the citation. You still have the right to contest it in court. Refusing to sign can result in additional charges.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking signing equals admitting guilt — this is a common misconception that the MVA test specifically addresses.

**Common Mistake:** Arguing with the officer instead of signing — address disputes through the legal process later.

---

**Q5. [Type: RECALL]** What happens if you are convicted of a moving violation while holding a provisional driver's license?

**Answer:** You are required to restart your eighteen-month waiting period.

**Explanation:** The provisional license period is eighteen months. A moving violation conviction or Probation Before Judgment resets this clock, extending the time before you can obtain a full license.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking only serious offenses trigger the restart — any moving violation conviction counts.

**Common Mistake:** Not realizing that a PBJ also triggers the eighteen-month restart.

---

**Q6. [Type: RECALL]** What is the penalty for failing to attend the Driver Improvement Program?

**Answer:** Suspension of your license.

**Explanation:** The Driver Improvement Program is mandatory for provisional and permit holders convicted of violations. Not attending results in automatic license suspension.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking the program is optional or that missing it only results in a warning.

**Common Mistake:** Underestimating the seriousness of the Driver Improvement Program requirement.

---

**Q7. [Type: RECALL]** Since when are Maryland drivers required to carry proof of insurance at all times?

**Answer:** Effective October 1, 2016.

**Explanation:** Maryland law requires all drivers to have a valid insurance identification card in their possession at all times while operating a motor vehicle. The proof may be in electronic format.

**MVA Test Trap:** Not knowing the specific date or thinking insurance only needs to be shown during a traffic stop.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking physical proof is the only acceptable form — electronic proof is legal.

---

**Q8. [Type: RECALL]** How many people are killed annually in Maryland crashes involving young drivers?

**Answer:** More than 60 people.

**Explanation:** The Maryland Driver's Manual cites that more than 60 people are killed and more than 7,800 are injured each year in crashes involving young drivers. New drivers are much more likely to be involved in crashes.

**MVA Test Trap:** Confusing the number killed with the number injured.

**Common Mistake:** Not connecting these statistics to the importance of the GDL program and supervised practice.

---

**Q9. [Type: RECALL]** What is the first step when entering an angle parking space?

**Answer:** Identify the space and signal your intentions.

**Explanation:** Signaling alerts other drivers and pedestrians that you are about to park. This is the foundation of all safe parking maneuvers.

**MVA Test Trap:** Skipping the signal and going straight into the space — this is unsafe and illegal.

**Common Mistake:** Not signaling because "nobody is around" — always signal regardless.

---

**Q10. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are parallel parking on a street and a pedestrian walks behind your car as you begin to back in. What should you do?

**Answer:** Stop immediately and yield to the pedestrian. Wait until they have safely passed before continuing.

**Explanation:** The manual states you must check traffic in all directions and yield to pedestrians, bicyclists, and other vehicles during parallel parking. Pedestrians always have the right of way.

**MVA Test Trap:** Continuing to back up slowly and expecting the pedestrian to move — you must stop and yield.

**Common Mistake:** Only checking for vehicles and forgetting about pedestrians.

---

**Q11. [Type: APPLICATION]** An officer pulls you over and issues a citation. You believe the citation is unfair. What should you do?

**Answer:** Sign the citation without arguing. Address the dispute through the proper legal channels later — you can contest it in court.

**Explanation:** Signing is a legal acknowledgment of receipt, not an admission of guilt. Arguing with the officer on the roadside will not change the outcome and may escalate the situation.

**MVA Test Trap:** Refusing to sign because "you did nothing wrong" — refusal can result in additional charges.

**Common Mistake:** Becoming confrontational instead of remaining calm and cooperative.

---

**Q12. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are exiting an angle parking space in a busy lot. You cannot see past the vehicles parked on either side of you. What is the safest approach?

**Answer:** Back up very slowly while checking mirrors and looking over both shoulders. If visibility is severely limited, inch out gradually until you can see past the adjacent vehicles, then continue backing.

**Explanation:** Angle parking creates blind spots on both sides. Slow, incremental movement gives you time to react if a vehicle or pedestrian suddenly appears.

**MVA Test Trap:** Backing out quickly to "get it over with" — this is the most common cause of parking lot accidents.

**Common Mistake:** Only looking in one direction instead of checking both sides continuously.

---

**Q13. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are a provisional license holder and receive a PBJ for a speeding citation. What are the consequences?

**Answer:** You must restart your eighteen-month waiting period and you are required to attend the Driver Improvement Program. Failure to attend will result in license suspension.

**Explanation:** A PBJ (Probation Before Judgment) is treated the same as a conviction for provisional license holders. Both trigger the eighteen-month restart and the mandatory Driver Improvement Program.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking a PBJ is more lenient than a conviction for provisional license holders — the consequences are the same.

**Common Mistake:** Not attending the Driver Improvement Program because the PBJ "was not a real conviction."

---

**Q14. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are pulled over for a traffic violation and the officer asks for your insurance information. You only have it stored on your phone. Is this acceptable?

**Answer:** Yes, electronic proof of insurance is acceptable under Maryland law since October 1, 2016.

**Explanation:** The law specifically allows insurance identification cards in electronic format. You must present it on request of a law enforcement officer.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking you must have a physical card — electronic proof is fully legal.

**Common Mistake:** Not having your insurance information accessible on your phone because you assumed only a paper card was valid.

---

**Q15. [Type: APPLICATION]** After a fender bender where no one is hurt and both cars can move, what information should you collect from the other driver?

**Answer:** Name, address, phone number, insurance information, vehicle license plate number, and the make, model, and year of their vehicle if possible. Also get names and contact information from any witnesses.

**Explanation:** The manual specifies this exact list of information. Having complete information protects you during the insurance claim process.

**MVA Test Trap:** Only getting the license plate number and forgetting insurance and witness information.

**Common Mistake:** Not getting witness contact information, which can be critical for insurance disputes.

---

**Q16. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are practicing parallel parking with your supervising driver and you hit the curb. What should you do?

**Answer:** Stop, assess if there is damage, and then reposition the vehicle to try again. Parallel parking is a skill that requires practice — the manual says to continue practicing if you cannot park successfully.

**Explanation:** Hitting the curb during practice is normal. The key is to learn from it and adjust your steering timing. The manual explicitly states that if your new driver cannot parallel park successfully, continue to practice.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking one failed attempt means you have failed the skills test — you get multiple attempts on the actual test.

**Common Mistake:** Giving up on parallel parking practice instead of continuing to develop the skill.

---

**Q17. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Compare the procedures for entering and exiting a parallel parking space. How do the steps mirror each other?

**Answer:** Entering involves: signal, align parallel, 2-3 feet gap, brake, reverse, check traffic, back with sharp turn, turn opposite, adjust. Exiting involves: check traffic, signal, brake, reverse toward car behind, drive forward with turn toward travel direction, check front bumper, steer into traffic. The steps essentially reverse — entering goes back into the space, exiting pulls forward out of it, and both require checking all directions.

**Explanation:** The parallel parking maneuver is symmetrical — what you do going in is essentially the reverse of what you do going out. Both require signaling, checking traffic, and careful steering.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking exiting is just "drive away" — it requires the same careful checking and maneuvering as entering.

**Common Mistake:** Not treating exiting with the same care as entering — both are potentially dangerous maneuvers.

---

**Q18. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Explain the chain of consequences that begins with a single moving violation for a provisional license holder in Maryland.

**Answer:** A moving violation leads to a conviction (or PBJ), which triggers three things: (1) the eighteen-month provisional waiting period restarts from zero, (2) mandatory attendance at the Driver Improvement Program, and (3) if you fail to attend the program, your license is suspended. This chain means one ticket can set back your driving privilege progression significantly.

**Explanation:** The Maryland GDL system has escalating consequences designed to encourage safe driving during the provisional period. Each consequence builds on the previous one.

**MVA Test Trap:** Thinking only the fine matters — the procedural consequences are far more significant for provisional drivers.

**Common Mistake:** Not understanding that a PBJ carries the same GDL consequences as a full conviction.

---

**Q19. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Why does the Maryland Driver's Manual include statistics about young driver crashes alongside information about traffic stops and citations?

**Answer:** The statistics provide context for why the rules exist. More than 60 people die and 7,800 are injured annually in Maryland crashes involving young drivers. The citation consequences, Driver Improvement Program, and GDL requirements are all designed to reduce these numbers. The manual connects individual responsibility (obeying traffic laws) to the broader public safety goal.

**Explanation:** The manual uses data to motivate compliance — when you understand the real-world impact of unsafe driving, the rules feel less like arbitrary requirements and more like life-saving protections.

**MVA Test Trap:** Seeing the statistics as separate from the legal requirements — they are directly connected.

**Common Mistake:** Not recognizing that the GDL system exists because of these crash statistics.

---

**Q20. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Compare the procedures for angle parking versus parallel parking. What actions are similar and what is unique to each type?

**Answer:** Both require signaling intentions, moving slowly, checking for traffic, and centering the vehicle. Angle parking is simpler — you pull forward into the space and back out. Parallel parking is more complex — you must align parallel to another vehicle, back in with specific steering inputs, and end up within 12 inches of the curb. Exiting angle parking requires backing up; exiting parallel parking requires a combination of reversing and moving forward.

**Explanation:** Angle parking is a single-direction maneuver (pull in, back out). Parallel parking is a multi-direction maneuver that tests steering precision and spatial judgment.

**MVA Test Trap:** Treating parallel parking as just another form of angle parking — the techniques are fundamentally different.

**Common Mistake:** Not practicing both types because "I will only use one" — both are tested and both are encountered in daily driving.

## Quick Reference

- **Parallel parking:** Signal, 2-3 feet gap, back in, 12 inches max from curb
- **Angle parking:** Signal, slow, center, pull forward; back out carefully
- **Traffic stop:** Pull over, engine off, stay in car, hands on wheel, sign citation
- **Signing citation:** NOT an admission of guilt
- **Provisional violation:** Restart 18-month waiting period
- **PBJ:** Same consequences as conviction for provisional holders
- **Driver Improvement:** Mandatory for violations; non-attendance = suspension
- **Proof of insurance:** Required at all times since October 1, 2016; electronic accepted
- **Young driver crashes:** 60+ killed, 7,800+ injured annually in Maryland
- **Information after crash:** Name, address, phone, insurance, plate, make/model/year, witnesses

**Test Day Tip:** The two most tested facts in this chapter are: (1) signing a citation is NOT an admission of guilt, and (2) the twelve-inch maximum distance from the curb. Both appear on nearly every Maryland knowledge test.
