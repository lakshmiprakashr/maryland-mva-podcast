# Chapter 07 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Lesson 2 covers the fundamental vehicle operations: **starting and moving the vehicle**, making **turns** (right and left), **backing up** with correct procedure, proper **steering hand positions**, and **lane positioning** (center, left, right). Also covers common backing errors and the coach's readiness checklist.

## Why It Matters

These are the first actual driving skills. A driver who can't move smoothly, turn safely, or back up without hitting something lacks the foundation for all advanced driving. MVA road tests directly evaluate every skill in this chapter.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Starting sequence:** Foot on brake, start engine, shift to Drive, slowly release brake
- **Practice pedal feel:** Alternate brake and gas several times while stationary before moving
- **Turning steps:** Check traffic all directions (use mirrors), signal intention, turn wheel smoothly, enter nearest lane for desired direction, accelerate smoothly
- **Backing procedure:** Brake, shift to reverse, hand at 12 o'clock, visual search all around, turn body right with arm over seat, release brake (no gas), slow walking pace
- **Steering positions:** Left hand at 9, right hand at 3 (preferred); also acceptable: 8 and 4, 10 and 2; avoid single-hand driving
- **Lane positions:** Center (1) = most common; Left (2) = hills, curves, next to parked cars; Right (3) = preparing for right turn
- **Common backing errors:** Improper body position, moving too fast, failing to check behind, not looking during whole maneuver, relying on mirrors, not steering in intended direction
- **Coach's checklist:** Knows controls? Checks vehicle before moving? Brakes smoothly? Backs up correctly? ALL must be yes before progressing

## MVA Test Traps

| Trap | Correct Answer |
|------|---------------|
| "Press the gas when shifting to Drive" | Release the brake slowly — no gas needed to start moving |
| "10 and 2 is the best steering position" | 9 and 3 is preferred for control and airbag safety |
| "Use mirrors when backing" | Must turn body and look through rear window; mirrors alone are insufficient |
| "Center lane position is only for highways" | Center is the default position for ALL driving |
| "Left lane position is for passing" | Left position is for hills, curves, and alongside parked cars |

## Common Mistakes

- Pressing gas immediately after shifting to Drive
- Jerking the steering wheel instead of turning smoothly
- Not signaling before turns during practice in parking lots
- Backing up while looking only in mirrors
- Using the gas pedal during backing instead of idle creep
- Forgetting to cancel turn signal after completing a turn
- Not entering the nearest lane for the desired direction during turns

## Memory Tricks

- **"Brake, Drive, Release"** — Start sequence: Foot on **brake**, shift to **drive**, **release** brake slowly
- **"Signal, Check, Turn, Enter"** — Turn sequence: **Signal** your intent, **check** traffic, **turn** the wheel, **enter** the nearest lane
- **"Hand on top, body turned, no gas"** — Backing basics: hand at 12 o'clock, body turned right, idle speed only
- **"Center is king"** — Center lane position is your default; only shift left or right for specific reasons
- **"Smooth hands, smooth ride"** — Jerky steering = jerky driving. Practice smooth, controlled turns

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

- **Turn signal** — Required at least 3-4 seconds before turning (later chapters); signals intent to other drivers
- **Hazard lights** — Warn other drivers of a stopped or disabled vehicle
- **Lane markings** — Solid white = don't cross; dashed white = lane change OK; yellow = opposing traffic

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **Signal requirement:** Maryland law requires signaling before every turn and lane change
- **Backing liability:** Driver is responsible for all hazards while backing; if you hit something while backing, you are at fault
- **Coach's responsibility:** Under Maryland GDL, the supervising driver (coach) must ensure the new driver demonstrates competency before progressing
