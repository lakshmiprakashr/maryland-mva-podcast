## Questions

**Q1. [Type: RECALL]** How many supervised practice hours are required for drivers 25 and older?

**Answer:** 14 hours of practice with a supervising driver, including 3 hours at night.

**Explanation:** Drivers 25 and older need significantly fewer practice hours (14 vs 60) and fewer night hours (3 vs 10) compared to younger drivers.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse with 60 hours (for under 25). The age-based difference is significant.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming all drivers need 60 practice hours.

---

**Q2. [Type: RECALL]** What is the minimum holding period for a learner's permit for drivers 25 and older?

**Answer:** 45 days, provided they do not receive any moving violation citations or PBJ.

**Explanation:** Drivers 25 and older have the shortest holding period of just 45 days, but this can be extended to 9 months if they receive a moving violation or PBJ.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't forget that the 45-day period can be extended. It's not guaranteed.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the 45-day period is absolute and cannot be extended.

---

**Q3. [Type: RECALL]** Do drivers 25 and older still need to complete a driver education course?

**Answer:** Yes. You still need to complete a Maryland MVA approved driver education course.

**Explanation:** Driver education is required for all ages, including drivers 25 and older. The course consists of 30 classroom hours and 6 behind-the-wheel hours.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume that being over 25 exempts you from driver education. It's required for everyone.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that age 25+ means no education requirements.

---

**Q4. [Type: RECALL]** Do drivers 25 and older still need a supervising driver?

**Answer:** Yes. You still need a supervising driver or coach to help you practice.

**Explanation:** Even though drivers 25+ have reduced requirements, they still need a qualified supervisor during practice driving.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume that being over 25 means you can practice alone. Supervision is still required.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that age 25+ means no supervision needed.

---

**Q5. [Type: RECALL]** What happens if a driver 25 or older receives a moving violation or PBJ while practicing?

**Answer:** They will be required to hold the learner's instructional permit for 9 months before being allowed to take the tests to become a fully licensed driver.

**Explanation:** The 9-month extension applies to all ages, regardless of the normal holding period. Even the 45-day period for 25+ can be extended.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think that being over 25 exempts you from the 9-month extension. It applies to everyone.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the 45-day period is immune to extensions.

---

**Q6. [Type: RECALL]** Can you use parking assist during the Maryland skills test?

**Answer:** No. You may not use the parking assist feature during the skills test.

**Explanation:** While backup cameras and onboard radar are allowed, parking assist is specifically prohibited because it takes over steering control.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse parking assist with backup camera. They are different technologies with different rules.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming all driving assistance technology is treated the same way.

---

**Q7. [Type: RECALL]** Can you use a backup camera during the Maryland skills test?

**Answer:** Yes. You may use your backup camera and the vehicle's onboard radar system during the skills test.

**Explanation:** Maryland allows the use of backup cameras and onboard radar systems during the skills test, as these are considered standard safety features.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't confuse backup camera with parking assist. Camera is allowed; parking assist is not.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming all technology is prohibited during the skills test.

---

**Q8. [Type: RECALL]** Who must wear a seatbelt in Maryland?

**Answer:** Everybody, no matter where they are seated in the vehicle and no matter what their age.

**Explanation:** Maryland has a universal seatbelt law that requires all occupants to wear seatbelts. The driver can be ticketed if everyone in the vehicle is not buckled up.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think seatbelts are only required for front seat passengers. Everyone must wear one.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming back seat passengers don't need seatbelts.

---

**Q9. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are 26 years old and want to practice driving. What do you still need to do?

**Answer:** Complete a Maryland MVA approved driver education course and practice with a supervising driver who is 21+ with 3+ years licensed.

**Explanation:** Even though drivers 25+ have reduced requirements, they still need driver education and a qualified supervisor.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume that being over 25 means you can skip education or supervision.

**Common Mistake:** Thinking that age 25+ means no requirements.

---

**Q10. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are about to get in your car to practice driving. What should you check before entering the vehicle?

**Answer:** Check all around the vehicle for hazards such as broken glass, body damage, condition of tires and lights, fluid leaks under the vehicle, and people or objects.

**Explanation:** The pre-entry check is essential for safety. It helps you identify potential hazards before you start driving.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't skip the pre-entry check. It's a critical safety step.

**Common Mistake:** Jumping into the car without checking for hazards.

---

**Q11. [Type: APPLICATION]** What should you do after entering the vehicle but before starting to drive?

**Answer:** Adjust seat, safety belts, and mirrors; know where all controls are (headlights, turn signal, hazard lights, horn, heater/defroster/AC, parking brake, windshield wipers, door locks, gear shift, gas pedal, brake pedal); fasten seat belts securely; turn off all electronic devices.

**Explanation:** These pre-driving steps ensure you are comfortable and in control before the vehicle moves.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't forget to turn off electronic devices. Distraction is a major cause of accidents.

**Common Mistake:** Skipping the adjustment steps and assuming everything is already set correctly.

---

**Q12. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are practicing driving and want to get a feel for the steering wheel. What should you do?

**Answer:** Turn the wheel slightly to the right and to the left to develop a feel for how the vehicle will move when the wheel turns.

**Explanation:** This basic steering exercise helps new drivers understand the vehicle's response to steering inputs before attempting more complex maneuvers.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't try to drive before getting comfortable with the steering wheel.

**Common Mistake:** Attempting to drive without first developing a feel for the steering.

---

**Q13. [Type: APPLICATION]** You are on a family vacation and want to drive in Virginia. You have a Maryland learner's permit. Can you drive there?

**Answer:** Not necessarily. You should check with Virginia's DMV first, as they may not accept a Maryland learner's permit.

**Explanation:** Each state has its own rules about recognizing out-of-state learner's permits. Virginia may not accept Maryland's permit.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume your Maryland permit works in Virginia. You must verify.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming all states accept Maryland learner's permits.

---

**Q14. [Type: APPLICATION]** Your learner's permit expires before you get your provisional license. What must you do?

**Answer:** You must retake the law test and get a new learner's permit before you can take the driving skills test.

**Explanation:** An expired permit means you lose your driving privileges. You must restart the permit process, including passing the law test again.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think you can just renew the permit. You must retake the law test.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming you can continue practicing with an expired permit.

---

**Q15. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Compare the requirements for a 20-year-old versus a 26-year-old applying for a provisional license.

**Answer:** A 20-year-old needs 60 practice hours (10 at night) and a 3-month holding period. A 26-year-old needs only 14 practice hours (3 at night) and a 45-day holding period. Both need driver education and a supervising driver.

**Explanation:** Maryland reduces requirements for older drivers, recognizing they may have more maturity and life experience. However, both must complete driver education.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't assume the requirements are the same for all ages. The age-based differences are significant.

**Common Mistake:** Focusing only on practice hours and forgetting the different holding periods.

---

**Q16. [Type: SYNTHESIS]** Why does Maryland require seatbelts for everyone, regardless of age or seating position?

**Explanation:** Seatbelts are the best protection in a crash. Requiring them for everyone reduces injuries and fatalities across all age groups and seating positions.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think there are exceptions for back seat passengers or young children. Everyone must wear one.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming back seat passengers are exempt from seatbelt requirements.

---

**Q17. [Type: RECALL]** What is the pre-entry check for driving?

**Answer:** Check all around the vehicle for hazards such as broken glass, body damage, condition of tires and lights, fluid leaks under the vehicle, and people or objects.

**Explanation:** The pre-entry check is a critical safety step that should be performed before every drive to identify potential hazards.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't skip this step. It's specifically mentioned in the manual.

**Common Mistake:** Jumping into the car without checking for hazards.

---

**Q18. [Type: RECALL]** What controls should a new driver know before starting to drive?

**Answer:** Headlights, turn signal, hazard lights, horn, heater/defroster/AC, parking brake, windshield wipers, door locks, gear shift, gas pedal, and brake pedal.

**Explanation:** Knowing the location and function of all controls is essential for safe driving. This should be learned before the vehicle moves.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't forget any controls. The MVA may ask about specific ones.

**Common Mistake:** Assuming the new driver already knows where all controls are.

---

**Q19. [Type: RECALL]** What should you do with electronic devices before driving?

**Answer:** Turn off all electronic devices.

**Explanation:** Electronic devices are a major distraction. Turning them off before driving helps maintain focus on the road.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think you can use them if they're on silent. They should be turned off.

**Common Mistake:** Keeping electronic devices on and trying to ignore them while driving.

---

**Q20. [Type: RECALL]** Why is practice driving important for new drivers?

**Answer:** Practice driving under the required supervision of an experienced driver helps new drivers develop safe driving habits and gain experience in various conditions.

**Explanation:** The more practice a new driver gets, the safer they become. Practice on a wide variety of roads in a wide variety of conditions is essential.

**MVA Test Trap:** Don't think practice is optional. It's a legal requirement.

**Common Mistake:** Rushing through practice hours without focusing on quality.
