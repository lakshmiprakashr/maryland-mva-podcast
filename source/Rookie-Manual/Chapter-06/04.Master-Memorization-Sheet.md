# Chapter 06 — Master Memorization Sheet

## Chapter Summary

Lesson 1 covers everything before the car moves: the **pre-entry vehicle check**, adjusting seat/mirrors/belts, locating all controls, practicing steering, and understanding Maryland seat belt law. This is the foundation for every driving lesson that follows.

## Why It Matters

Skipping pre-entry checks is the number one habit that separates safe drivers from crash-prone ones. A driver who doesn't know where the brake pedal or headlights are cannot react in an emergency. Maryland law requires everyone in the vehicle to be buckled up — no exceptions.

## High-Yield Facts

- **Pre-entry check** = walk around the vehicle looking for: broken glass, body damage, tire/light condition, fluid leaks, people or objects nearby
- **Controls to know before driving:** headlights, turn signal, hazard lights, horn, heater/defroster/A/C, parking brake, windshield wipers, door locks, gear shift, gas pedal, brake pedal
- **Seat belt law (Maryland):** EVERY person in the vehicle must wear a seat belt, regardless of age or seating position
- **Steering wheel hand position:** Left hand at 9 o'clock, right hand at 3 o'clock — provides full control and minimizes airbag injury risk
- **Backing technique:** Grasp wheel at 12 o'clock, turn body right with arm over seat back, release brake — do NOT use gas pedal; allow slow walking pace
- **Turning steps:** Check traffic all directions, signal, turn wheel smoothly, enter nearest lane for desired direction, accelerate smoothly

## MVA Test Traps

| Trap | Correct Answer |
|------|---------------|
| "Only the driver must wear a seat belt" | ALL occupants must wear seat belts — every seat, every age |
| "10 and 2 is the best hand position" | 9 and 3 is preferred — reduces airbag injury risk |
| "Use gas pedal when backing up" | Never use gas when backing — let the car creep at idle speed |
| "Mirrors are enough when backing" | You MUST turn your body and look behind you, not just use mirrors |

## Common Mistakes

- Forgetting to check fluid leaks under the vehicle before driving
- Adjusting seat and mirrors AFTER starting to drive instead of before
- Not turning off electronic devices before a practice session
- Reaching across the steering wheel instead of turning body when backing
- Steering with one hand or gripping too tightly

## Memory Tricks

- **"Walk the car"** — Before every drive, physically walk around the vehicle. Think of it like a pilot's pre-flight check.
- **"9 and 3, not 10 and 2"** — Your hands make the letter "L" on each side. Think "Level control."
- **"Brake, then look, then creep"** — For backing: foot on brake first, look all around, then release brake slowly (no gas).
- **"Everyone clicks"** — Driver AND all passengers. No exceptions. Every seat.

## Must-Know Signs and Signals

- **Hazard lights** (double triangle button) — Used to warn other drivers of danger or a stopped vehicle
- **Turn signal lever** — Left stalk, push up for right, down for left
- **Parking brake** — Usually a lever between seats or a pedal on the far left; always engage before exiting

## Maryland-Specific Laws

- **Maryland Seat Belt Law:** Every occupant must wear a seat belt. Driver can be ticketed if any passenger is unbuckled.
- **GDL (Graduated Driver Licensing) holders** face additional restrictions on passengers and night driving — but seat belt rules apply equally to all drivers.
- **No electronic device use** while practicing — new drivers must focus entirely on the road.

## One-Minute Review Checklist

- [ ] Pre-entry check: walk around for damage, leaks, hazards, people
- [ ] Adjust seat, mirrors, and fasten seat belt BEFORE moving
- [ ] Know location of: headlights, brake, gas, turn signal, hazards, wipers, parking brake
- [ ] Turn off all electronic devices
- [ ] Steering hand position: 9 and 3
- [ ] When backing: brake, shift to reverse, grasp wheel at 12, turn body right, look behind, release brake slowly (no gas)
- [ ] Maryland law: ALL occupants must wear seat belts
